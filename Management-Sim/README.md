# Management Sim

Research repository in preparation for a **management simulation video game**. The premise under investigation is how organizations are structured, led, and operated — and how management quality, organizational design, and leadership choices translate into measurable outcomes.

Instructions for Agents:
- Each agent should take only the one question assigned. Identify precursor questions and subset questions, write them out, then research step by step using web search and other tools.
- Be thorough, but do not attempt to answer questions that are likely the domain of other agents researching other big questions. Explore and document interesting facts, but strive for independent silos and areas.
- Agents may research different conclusions than previous agents — contradictory information and evidence is good.
- Present findings in a self-contained HTML doc with contained CSS that incorporates diagrams, top-level overviews, and an introductory section describing what the document is and what its aims are (readable by a general audience without repo context).
- After the aims, note limitations of the research — theoretical limitations first, then methodology and literature limitations. Comment on the strength of evidence and the history of the field.
- Cite sources thoroughly. Treat this as a research paper.
- When researching examples, find as many as possible — 20–50 is appropriate where relevant.
- End with a concise conclusion, further research questions, and directions for interesting additional study.

## Research Questions

1. **Management Theory Foundations (complete):** What are the main theories of business and organizational management? What are the most influential books? How has management evolved historically? What does a typical MBA curriculum cover? What does research say about good vs. poor management and outcomes? What are ongoing debates (flat organizations, middle management, Musk-style first-principles management)? See `docs/management-theory-research.html`.
2. **Startup Accelerators & Co-Founder Paths (complete):** How do successful digital founders (especially college dropouts) meet co-founders? What is the history of incubators and accelerators? Which US programs succeed most, what do programs look like, and what are success/failure rates? See `docs/startup-accelerators-cofounder-paths.html`.
