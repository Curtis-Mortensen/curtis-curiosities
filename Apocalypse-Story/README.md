# Apocalypse Story

This is a repo dedicated to research in preperation for creating a story about a post-apocalyptic world. While it's currently text-only, it May eventually evolve into. Programmatic simulation. Of. Different. Locations. And group sizes in the event of a collapse. 

The general premise under investigation is of an epidemic. The disease is dormant for 2-3 days, after which the victim begins vomiting blood which spreads the disease. They're mostly symptomless up to that point. The disease has a higher than 50% mortality rate and victims die with 5 days of vomiting stage. 
The epidemic is not the focus of the investigation, rather the societal implications (primarily focused in the US) of having a rapid reduction in population are. Specifically, researching instances where the population was reduced by 50-90% within a period of weeks to months. 

Several research questions have been identified: 
Historical parallels: What are the major historical parallels of instances of dramatic population loss? Not exclusive to pandemics - invading armies, natural disasters, famines, droughts. Looking back at all of human history, to try to identify fertile areas of further research and investigation. 
Psychology: How do humans react in the face of existential danger? What would be the likely immediate results of widespread panic? When has that happened in recent memory (last 250 years)? For example, how did Japanese citizens react after two of their cities were nuked - facing an overwhelming enemy? How do cities react that know they are likely to be invaded? How often do people flee, and how?  Look at the past 500 years worldwide as the world has gotten more connected and transport has changed. 
Human Inter-reliance: How many people in the US would likely die without care, medical attention, or medicine? What do disaster preparedness response plans state about this possibility? What has the US military and other respected institutions war-gamed in these scenarios? 
Breakdown of Law and Order: How quickly as a situation devolves to people resort to rule-breaking, looting, and greatre violence? What psychological studies and books have been written on the subject? In the case of disasters, do people exhibit significantly different behaviour than in protests or unrest for example? Look at specific studies on the breakdown of law and reference specific case studies and instances. What was learned from BLM protests for example? Look at the past 250 years in developed nations. 
Definitive Predictions: What are the most credible, well funded, well respected predictions? Do academic and government institutions have defined frameworks for predicting extreme crisis? Look worldwide at developed nations and universities, think tanks. Try to list the specific reports in order of credibility and specificity. Also look at books that have been written on the subject, and list by popularity/respectedness. 


Follow-Up Questions for After Initial Research
Logistical effect of epidemics: Given the lack of physical damage associated with pandemics, how often do they result in a disruption of connectivity (internet, phone service) and electricity? How about fuel? What products/necessities most commonly are disrupted first? Could water fail? Ground in the recent history of developed nations and in wargames and theoretical predictions.
