**Rules and Lore:**
+4 in the Treasure room, +2 if 2 rooms away from the Treasure Room, and +1 if 3 rooms away.
The forecourt area counts as "room" at -2. In all rooms you enter throw for Occupants and also for Treasure. For a Fire you may choose not to have gone in! If you leave a room, it may refill with people but not things, except for fires that keep burning. The Agora is the way back out!

*Scum* are those from the market out to loot. Will tend to ignore you but might grab any objects from you if they can. Armed with thrown stones, vases, etc., they will not fight close in. If you fight them, all the other scum will know and then stone you on sight. *RabbleRousers* can call an extra d6 Scum per turn if threatened. Scum tend to flee if overawed. Scum fleeing will go into random adjacent non-fire rooms and be added to the Occupants.

*Fire!* Very destructive, anyone passing through has a 50% chance of being hurt, as do any carried Scrolls and Maps. All flammable objects in the room itself are already reduced to ash.

*Priests* are protecting the ancient building and its contents, and will not let interlopers pass. Armed with staves will defend in hand to hand. ArchPriests will Curse any interlopers.

*Arrange the value of the Gold Plates, Curses, etc., to suit your group (or individual).*
*Is the Annoyed Deity a powerful Man-Beast? Is it immune to Fire? Are any bits of the Deity of substantial worth? Does the Sacred Dagger really do x10 Damage against Annoyed Deities? What travesty does the Scroll of the Dead unleash? Might a Treasure Map lead reveal a secret hatch in one of the 12 rooms leading to unknown catacombs of prize and horror?*

### Map Description for Navigation
The map represents a symmetrical, grid-based stone temple structure accessed from the south. The temple is arranged into five rows of rooms, starting from the "Agora" (bottom) and moving North to the "Inner Sanctum."

**The Layout (South to North):**

*   **Entrance (The Agora):** The very bottom of the page is the "Agora" (market). Above this is a wide, rectangular "Forecourt" that acts as the entry porch to the temple.
*   **The First Tier:** Directly north of the Forecourt are three rooms in a row: 
    *   **House of Morning** (Left)
    *   **Pronaos** (Center)
    *   **House of Books** (Right)
*   **The Second Tier:** Continuing North:
    *   **Life** (Left)
    *   **Sacrifice** (Center)
    *   **Room of Dead** (Right)
*   **The Third Tier:** Continuing North:
    *   **Purity** (Left)
    *   **Blessing** (Center)
    *   **Room of Urns** (Right)
*   **The Final Tier (North-most):** These are the furthest rooms from the entrance:
    *   **Treasure** (Left)
    *   **Inner Sanctum** (Center)
    *   **Deity** (Right)

**Accessibility and Features:**
*   **Flow:** The building is a classic grid. You generally move from the Forecourt into the *Pronaos*, then into the *Sacrifice* chamber, and finally into the *Inner Sanctum*. 
*   **Interconnectivity:** Each of the small rooms on the left (Life, Purity, Treasure) and right (Room of Dead, Room of Urns, Deity) are connected to their central neighbors.
*   **Orientation:** The map is entirely vertical. When playing, treat the *Agora* as the starting safe-ish point, and the *Treasure, Inner Sanctum, and Deity* chambers at the very top as the "end-game" or high-value locations. The corridors are wide, and every room is rectangular, making it easy to track movement on standard graph paper.
