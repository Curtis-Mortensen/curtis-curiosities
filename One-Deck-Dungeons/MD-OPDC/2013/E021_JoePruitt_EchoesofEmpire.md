#### **The Battle (Rules)**
For each type of unit, multiply their Attack Strength (AS, on the chart below) by the number of units. For each army (Imperial and your allies) roll 2D6 and add the number rolled to their total AS. Subtract the lower roll from the higher, and subtract the result from the AS of the side with the lower roll. Continue doing this until the AS of one side drops to 0.

*Example:* The Imperial army starts with an AS of 12 (4 units of Imperial Soldiers with an AS of 3 each). If you have 2 units of Heavy Infantry (AS 3), 1 unit of Elvish Archers (AS 3), and Culsa the Dragon (AS 4), your total AS would be 13. If you roll a 3 and a 2 for the Imperial side, and a 1 and 3 for your side, the totals would be: Allies 17, Imperial 20. Subtract the lower roll from the higher and you get 3, which is subtracted from your AS, leaving you with an AS of 10 for the next round of combat.

#### **The Story**
Many years ago, the town of Bredon was part of the Iconian Empire. As Imperial fortunes waned, their soldiers withdrew, leaving Bredon and the surrounding villages to rule themselves. Now the Empire has returned. Today, an Imperial envoy arrived in Bredon and announced that they were reclaiming these lands. They will return in two weeks’ time...

You, the chosen hero of Bredon, have been assigned the task of visiting the surrounding lands and gathering allies to fight against the Empire. You have two weeks (14 days) to gather as many allies as you can and build as strong an army as possible.

There are three villages in the area: Wayford (West/Dorothil Forest), Orley (North/Dynge Mountains), and Lune (East/Plain of Sileah). The lords of Bredon have given you 100 Gold Crowns for bribes. You start with 2 units of Heavy Infantry. 

Traveling from one hex to an adjacent hex takes one day, and you can only travel along the marked routes (dotted lines). Mark off one day on the turn track every time you move from one hex to another. Start in Bredon (Hex 0605). On Day 14, 4 units of Imperial soldiers arrive at Bredon.

### Map Description (For Playability)

The map is a grid of hexagonal spaces. **Only hexes connected by dotted lines are traversable.** Think of this as a "hub-and-spoke" network. You start at the central hub and travel out to the three main regions: West, North, and East.

**1. The Starting Hub:**
*   **Bredon (0605):** This is your base of operations, located near the center-bottom of the map. It acts as the anchor for your movement.

**2. The Western Path (The Dorothil Forest):**
*   From **Bredon (0605)**, you must travel to **Western Crossroads (0506)**.
*   From the crossroads, you can access **Wayford (0406)** to the west, which connects to the **Elves of Dorothil Forest (0405)**.

**3. The Northern Path (The Dynge Mountains):**
*   From **Bredon (0605)**, you travel north to **Northern Crossroads (0604)**.
*   From here, three paths branch out: 
    *   Northwest to the **Cave of Culsa the Dragon (0504)**.
    *   North to the **Village of Orley (0603)**.
    *   Northeast to **Dagger Pass (0803)**.

**4. The Eastern Path (The Plain of Sileah):**
*   From **Bredon (0605)**, you travel east to **Eastern Crossroads (0706)**.
*   From here, you can reach:
    *   **Village of Lune (0806)** to the east.
    *   **Necromancer of Sileah Tower (0805)**.
    *   **Bloodwolf Orcs (0801)** in the far northeast corner.

**Navigation Rules:**
*   **Time Management:** Every single "hop" between two connected hexes costs one day.
*   **The Return:** You must plan your route so that you are physically located back on the **Bredon (0605)** hex at the end of Day 14. If you are anywhere else, you lose. 
*   **Strategic Choice:** Because travel costs time, you cannot visit every single location. You must choose a path, recruit the allies you can afford or defeat, and calculate the return trip to Bredon before your time expires.
