**Miscegenation of the Ancients**
*An old school Dungeons & Dragons scenario by Eero Tuovinen. Uses rupee standard.*

**What sages know:** A large ark was built by one man to secure all living things against a great flood of the waters. For a year and a day the ark housed one pair of each pre-existing animal. Around the six-month mark the stakes separating male from female were pulled.

**Overview:** An inexplicable, vaguely ship-shaped gopher-wood construct on top of a mountain peak in the highlands. It is 500′ long, near 100′ wide and 50′ high. A large hole has broken in the hull near the bow, seemingly as a result of violent landfall. A wide ramp leads up to an open entryway halfway up the hull-side.

**The Environment:** The mountain slopes are obscured by unnatural mists year-round. Any living being seeing a glimpse of the ark or even its shadow has to save vs. Magic or be confused and lost, never reaching the peak without help. Divine magic cannot be used to find the ark or help reach it as long as the Seal is present.

The ark was abandoned in disarray following its accidental grounding. All pure animals and many impure breeds escaped the ark before the great Seal was set by an archangel. All residents remaining fell into deep slumber in the mercy of the Lord. Since then the ark has been frozen in time.

The Seal is akin to a floating, invisible wheel of fire above the ark. The seal is broken when any being possessing a soul touches the ark. At that point the denizens wake; within 1d6 minutes the most mobile (birds, etc.) begin escaping the ark; within 1d6 hours other animals follow as per the random encounter table; within 1d6 days the intelligent denizens (particularly the Mule) dare to exit. Within 1d6 weeks the entire mountain is colonized by a chaotic hybrid ecology.

**Random Encounters:** After the Seal is broken, check for encounters every 3 Turns.
1. **Minor creatures:** smaller miscegenations or other ark life that won’t be dangerous unless poisonous, etc.
2. **A lone wandering hybrid:** 50% chance the next hybrid encounter is with the same type.
3. **A hybrid unit:** a herd, pack, troop, family or other unit of one hybrid type.
4. **Multiple types:** 1d6 different types of hybrids that may be hostile to each other.
5. **Degenerate ooze:** a random type of ooze, slime or mold, with signs of hybrid ancestry.
6. **Special Encounter:** roll on the table.

**Hybrids:** The ark is teeming with life, the vast majority of which consists of unnaturally miscegenated animals. We’ll call encounter-sized specimens “hybrids.” About 50% of the hybrids are non-viable long-term. Hybrids of the same species breed true. Cross-type fertilization produces various non-viable awfulness that degenerates into primal ooze, except in 5% of cases when it produces a chimera or lycanthrope. 

Any living being that spends a day and a night within the ark gains prodigious procreative powers for the next year.

**Exploring the Ark:** The ark has three decks (A–C) and a sloping roof. Entrance is through the hole to deck C, the ramp to deck B, or by climbing to the roof. The ark is illuminated by glowing stones (15,000 total). Each stone is worth 50 rupees. For each Turn spent exploring, a 1/6 chance of discovering something off this table:
1. **Hatch:** a way up/down.
2. **Secret feature:** forgotten treasure (50%), passage (25%) or trap (25%).
3. **Intelligent denizen:** human (50%), demi-human (25%) or other (25%).
4. **Architecture:** notable feature (skylight, feed station, etc.).
5. **Treasure:** see deck description.
6. **Special encounter.**

**Decks:**
*   **Deck A:** Complex warrens for birds and humans. Contains the Antediluvian library (valued at 100,000 rupees).
*   **Deck B:** Large halls for big animals and plant life. Contains the bones of the First Man (relic, 10,000 xp).
*   **Deck C:** Complex warrens and deep refuse pits. Contains the Gibborim Hoard (50,000 rupees) stored in an oubliette under a heavy stone lid.
