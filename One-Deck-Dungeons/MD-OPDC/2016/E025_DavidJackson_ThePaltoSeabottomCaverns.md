### Transcribed Text

**Overview**
The Palto Seabottom Caverns are a system of dried sea caves in a silt region. An enormous squid-shark has migrated here from the jungle to lay its eggs and die. The life-cycles of these behemoths span centuries, and this death/rebirth cycle can take decades. Aside from a few large egg-sacs (called roe) and some hatchlings roaming about, the caverns contain an ancient terraforming station from a failed alien outpost. It is rumored that there are piles of mixed up silver and iron discs about 6 inches in diameter among these ruins. (Both “Disc Piles” contain 3d8 discs worth 1 barter each.)

**Room/Encounter Descriptions**

*   **The Entrance (Bottom Left):** The wide-mouthed cave narrows and goes uphill. There is a donkey with 4 barter worth of supplies (a tent, some bedrolls, 2 kg food, torches, some gizmos).
*   **The Roe Chamber:** This cavern is filled with a squid-shark roe sack that has yet to hatch. The first person entering has to pass a dexterity check or accidentally release 1d6 larvae. (Larvae: 1d6 appearing, 1d4hp, AC 7, 1d6 damage).
*   **The Shale Slope (Central):** In this area the floor is broken shale at a 30 to 60-degree angle. There is a clutch of 5d6 squid-shark larvae here in a large machine housing a Disc Pile behind a yellow window.
*   **The Trapped Adventurers:** 1d4 level 1 Adventurers have been trapped here for three days. They were exploring and hid here when the clutch of larvae returned from an excursion.
*   **Terraforming Compound (Top Right):** This cavern contains the main compound of the terraforming station. There is a can of Heat Spray: apply to 1 melee weapon granting +1 damage for 1 day.
*   **The Abyss (Middle Right):** There is a gaping abyss; anyone looking in must save vs. spell or lose 1d4 WIS for 24 hours. There is also a Disc Pile here.
*   **Utility Note:** An energy cell can be turned into a Poison Gas Bomb (1d6+2 damage, 10-foot area for 1 round).
*   **The Lair (Bottom Right):** This cavern gradually slopes downward 20 feet to a mud pit containing a Dying Giant Squid-Shark. Unkillable, it has two long tentacles that can reach anywhere in the cavern, and eight that can reach 20 feet from its mouth. The squid-shark is immobile. (2x Tentacles: 3HD, AC 5, Attk: special—when a tentacle hits, the victim is caught and is slowly dragged to a gnashing shark maw in 2d4 rounds and dies).

### Map Description for Players
The map is drawn on a grid of squares, where 1 square equals 10 feet. The dungeon is a branching network of interconnected caverns.

*   **Entrance:** You start at the bottom-left of the map in a wide, funnel-shaped cave opening that leads into the complex.
*   **The Core:** Moving inward, the path hits a central "hub"—a large chamber with a central column of broken shale sloping at a steep angle. This central area serves as the crossroads for the entire dungeon.
*   **The Northern Branch:** From the central hub, a path leads upward toward the top-right section of the map, which houses the main terraforming compound.
*   **The Eastern/Abyss Branch:** A path extends to the right side of the map, opening into a chamber dominated by a large, gaping abyss.
*   **The Southern Branch:** The map slopes downward toward the bottom-right corner. This is a large, wide chamber containing the mud pit where the dying Giant Squid-Shark is trapped.
*   **General Navigation:** Many of the chambers are connected by dashed lines rather than solid walls; these represent narrow passages through the shifting silt. You are generally moving from the bottom-left toward the upper-right (for the station) or the lower-right (for the beast's lair).

**Key landmarks to keep in mind:**
1.  **Donkey:** Near the entrance (bottom-left).
2.  **Roe Sack/Larvae:** Located in a chamber just off the entrance path.
3.  **The Shale Slope:** In the dead center of the map.
4.  **Squid-Shark Lair:** Occupies the entire bottom-right quadrant of the cave system.
