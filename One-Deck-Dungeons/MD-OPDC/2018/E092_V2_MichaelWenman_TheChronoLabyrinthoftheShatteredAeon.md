#### Header/Intro
**The Chrono-Labyrinth of the Shattered Aeon**
For a minute, at the stroke of midnight on the winter solstice, the portal opens and the Chrono-Labyrinth beckons.

This dungeon plays out in real time. Either use a clock or a stopwatch when it is used; the clock never stops while the adventurers are in the labyrinth.

Regardless of the time when the exploration begins, adventurers start at the stairwell in the middle of section 1. At any time when a character leaves a map section, they move into the map section with a number matching the final minute digit in the time, regardless of what section they entered (eg. At 11:57, a character moves into map section 7; at 12:03 they move into map section 3). This means that certain parts of the labyrinth are only accessible at certain times.

If this dungeon is run for a group, the players should map it for themselves; they should not see the clock being used to actively track their time inside (or look at their own watch/clock unless at a “time check” point).

#### Why are the characters here?
*(Choose at least one of the following, or make up your own...)*

*   **Treasure:** You heard that there is a mysterious clockwork treasure within that local scholars will pay a small fortune for.
*   **Missing Child:** Every year when the labyrinth appears, a local child goes missing; you have been paid to find out what happens to them.
*   **The Queen:** The immortal Queen of the Shattered Aeon lives in a treasury at the centre of the labyrinth, ensuring the passage of seasons each year. Any who best her in riddlecraft are granted strength and power for until the next solstice.

### Description of the Map for Non-Visual Players

The map is composed of 10 distinct, square "tiles" (numbered 0–9). They are laid out in a grid. Each tile represents a small chamber or a segment of a corridor.

**How the Labyrinth Works:**
This is not a traditional map where rooms are physically connected in a fixed layout. Instead, **the map is dynamic.** When a player exits a room, they do not go to a specific "adjacent" tile. Instead, they look at the last digit of the current minute on a real-world clock (e.g., if the time is 12:07, they go to Tile 7). 

**The Tile Elements:**
*   **Tile 1 (The Start):** Contains a central stairwell. This is the only place to enter.
*   **The Layout:** Each tile is a square room with hallways branching to the top, bottom, left, or right edges of the square. 
*   **Icons to Watch For:**
    *   **Encounters:** You will see small line-art icons scattered inside the rooms. Skulls represent undead enemies, and gears represent mechanical constructs. The more detailed the icon (e.g., skulls with stars), the more dangerous the enemy.
    *   **Interactive Points:** Look for circles (Time Checks), pedestal icons (Map Pedestals), or treasure chests. These indicate specific utility points—such as checking the clock or seeing a map—that are critical for navigating the labyrinth.
    *   **Exits:** These look like open archways on the walls. When a character passes through an archway, the Dungeon Master checks the clock to determine which of the ten tiles the player now enters.

**Strategic Note:** 
Because movement is time-based, the "map" is a loop. A player can be in Tile 1 and, by walking through an exit at 11:59, end up in Tile 9. If they wait one minute, they are suddenly in Tile 0. Players must use the "Time Check" and "Map Pedestal" rooms to learn the rhythms of the dungeon if they wish to reach the Queen's treasury (usually located in the central or most protected tile) or find the exit.
