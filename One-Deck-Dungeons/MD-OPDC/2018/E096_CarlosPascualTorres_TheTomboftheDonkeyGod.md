## Transcription

This document is a one-page dungeon module titled **"The Tomb of the Donkey God"** by Carlos Pascual "Azafran." It serves as a tabletop RPG resource, combining a top-down isometric map with 10 illustrated panels that detail specific room encounters.

### Transcription of Written Text

**Introductory Text:**
"Will the adventurers have the wits to sack the Tomb of the Donkey God? The strange people of Hollyhead believed in a strange god: a donkey. At least until the poor creature died a few years ago. They didn’t expect that a god could die that easily, so they thought he must be visiting the family in the skies. In order to protect the corpse until he returns they constructed a very intricate tomb. Rumors say they melted all the Town’s gold, making everyone poor, to erect a life-size Donkey statue guarding the body. What will adventurers do?"

**Dungeon Facts:**
*   **Floor:** Sand and debris.
*   **Walls:** Made with stone bricks.
*   **Ceiling:** 4 meters tall.
*   **Illumination:** None - pitch black inside.

**Room Descriptions:**
1.  **Entrance:** A sentient door awaits at the very entrance of the dungeon, just above it the word “DONKEY” can be read engraved in stone. The door is well-mannered but it doesn’t know how to open itself. The secret is to make it say the word “Donkey”.
2.  **The Pit:** Very deep pit, about 10 meters in circumference. The end can’t be seen but if anyone falls it is a good thing to know that it is filled with acid at the bottom. There is a rope hanging in the middle but what adventurers don’t know is that there is barbed wire hidden inside the rope core.
3.  **Bear Trap:** There is a bear trap in the corner. The catch is that if it’s activated a Bear will drop from the ceiling, crushing anything below and attacking the rest.
4.  **Automaton Room:** Six automatons are in this room. Not very intelligent, but programmed to kill any living thing in sight.
5.  **Blacksmith Statue:** There is a statue of a blacksmith wielding a hammer over an anvil. In the floor there are some chalk pieces. The door on the left is firmly closed and can only be opened with a key. Whatever the adventurers draw with chalk on top of the anvil it will magically be created, made of stone. The only way to open the door is to draw a key on the anvil.
6.  **Spiked Ceiling:** As soon as the adventurers enter this room the spiked ceiling will start to go down and the doors will lock. There is a small hole in the ground where only one of them can fit and avoid the spikes. The trick is that the ceiling will stop before crushing the adventurers while the one in the hole will fall into a pit trap.
7.  **Water Pit:** There is a pit filled with a foot of water on the far end of the room. If any adventurer steps just in front of the door on the water, his foot will be trapped between inverted spikes and the water level will start to rise up to the pit top in about 5 minutes. Additionally, a secret door will open freeing a water monster that will attack the adventurers. There is also a fake wall in this room that leads to room 8.
8.  **Piranha Pit:** Trapdoor to a pit filled with water and very hungry piranhas. About 15 meters tall, it’s very difficult to see the bottom from the trapdoor.
9.  **Spiral Corridor:** A series of corridors going left in a spiral. All are filled with holes in the walls and marks in the ground (look like darts traps and swinging blades) but nothing happens. Only when the adventurers are in room 10 will they start hearing mechanic sounds… the traps are now activated.
10. **The Treasure:** The treasure is a life-size Donkey statue made of solid gold that weighs more than 1000 kgs. Inside, there is a very powerful magic object, but a magic spell makes it impossible to open or break the statue in any way until it is outside of the dungeon. The adventurers must carry the donkey all the way out of the dungeon.

### Map Description for Gameplay
The map is laid out in an isometric view showing a linear, winding path through a stone complex. Imagine the dungeon as a connected series of 10 rectangular chambers:

*   **The Entrance (Room 1):** The adventure begins on the far left side. This is the only entrance.
*   **The Path:** From Room 1, the party moves North into **Room 2**.
*   **The Chain:** From Room 2, they proceed North into **Room 3**.
*   **Winding East:** From Room 3, they travel East through a doorway into **Room 4**. Moving further East, they enter **Room 5**.
*   **The Shift:** From Room 5, the path turns North to enter **Room 6**. From Room 6, the path continues North to **Room 7**.
*   **The Return Path:** From Room 7, the path takes a hard turn West into **Room 8**.
*   **The Spiral:** From Room 8, the path connects to a long, narrow corridor—**Room 9**—which spirals back toward the center of the map.
*   **The Centerpiece:** **Room 10** is the final destination, located in the middle-central area of the dungeon complex.

**Navigation Tip for the GM:** The players must travel from the far left (Room 1) in a zig-zag pattern—north, east, north, then west—to reach the final room in the center of the map. Note that Room 7 contains a "fake wall" that acts as a shortcut directly into Room 8, bypassing the intended flow.
