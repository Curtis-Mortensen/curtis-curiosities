**Lost in the Coldbless Caves**
*by Gregorius21778 | www.gregorius21778.wordpress.com*
*Released as: http://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-sa/3.0*

**Background**
In a cave system far away from the Dwarf settlement of Kar Narruk runs an underground river. The river is known as Coldbless, and its water is an ingredient for local healing potions. Troglodytes have recently been reported in the area, and a Dwarf named Horgast Truebelt has not returned from a journey to gather the water. His wife and family promise a reward to those that return him, or his shiny belt buckle as a proof of his demise.

**Environmental Mechanics**
*   **[E]:** Marks the passage through which the PC will enter the area.
*   **[O]:** Are tunnels and passages to other, unexplored areas.
*   **The River:** It is ice cold and strong. In the north, large rocks allow crossing in relative safety. The water is rich with magical energy; one quart heals minor wounds immediately. After drinking it three times, the effect is lost for three days.
*   **Mushroom Fields (m):** Found on gravel banks. Mostly edible, but house deep cave insects. May trigger a poisonous centipede encounter (doubles on 2d6; bite may leave a character shivery for 1d6 hours).
*   **Crevasses (c):** Deep enough that a fall will hurt but not kill. Wide enough that jumping is a small risk. Climbing down and up is an option but is time-consuming and strenuous.
*   **Ascends/Descends (a):** Not steep enough for climbing gear, but bothersome for unskilled climbers.
*   **Narrows (n):** Impossible for plate armor, heavily packed characters, or trolls. Others may squeeze through with help.

**Random Encounters (d12, check every hour):**
*   [1-2]: 1d6 troglodytes
*   [3]: One giant cave spider
*   [4+]: None.

**Creatures**
*   **Troglodytes:** Larger than Halflings, scrawny, gray, hairless, large black eyes. Feral, early stone-age culture. See heat signatures, 2-in-6 chance to attack on sight. Base camp at [5].
*   **Giant Cave Spiders:** Man-sized with equal-sized legs. Cunning, silent, see heat signatures, walk on walls/ceilings. Will not attack a group, but shadows them to isolate a victim. Two leg attacks, then a bite. Venom is paralyzing.
*   **The Undine:** A hand-sized water pixie. Emerges after the second time the river is disturbed. She appears as a slender nymph made of water. She speaks with a voice like a river over stones. She finds questions "BORING" and likes shiny things. She has Horgast’s belt buckle; she claims it is "MINE!" but might trade it.

**Room Descriptions**
*   **[1] The flat cavern:** Low ceiling (penalties for characters taller than a dwarf). Dotted with stalagmites/rubble. Exits may be missed if not explored thoroughly.
*   **[2] The tilted cave:** Steep ascend to the west, comfortable ceiling. Quartz glitter in the light.
*   **[3] The vast cavern:** A dome-sized space with a central rock formation and a huge sinkhole in the north. A tiny ledge connects to [1]. A troglodyte lookout sits here and will try to flee to [5] to raise the alarm.
*   **[4] The stalagmite forests:** Large caves filled with stalagmites. May lead to an ambush.
*   **[5] The troglodyte lair:** Large, unlit cave with 1d6+6 troglodytes. They rest by the northeast wall. Contains an old pick-ax, 10+2d7 rations of roasted meat, and two large water skins belonging to Horgast.
*   **[6] Unstable areas:** Littered with rubble. Loud noises (like combat) may cause boulders to fall from the ceiling.
*   **[7] The carcass slope:** Used by the troll [T] to dispose of remains. Rats here may shriek and alarm the troll. Horgast’s remains are here, but not his buckle.
*   **[8] The stinking cave:** Reeks of urine and troll. Leads to the lair of a two-headed, three-armed chaos troll [T]. The troll killed Horgast. It has two attacks, is immune to control magic, and regenerates. It is raving mad and talks to itself.

### Map Description for Navigation
To visualize this map, imagine the layout as a vertical path moving from the bottom-left toward the top-right.

*   **The River:** A river flows vertically, splitting the map roughly into a left and right side. There is a path of large rocks in the northern section (near [3]) that acts as a bridge.
*   **The Entrance [E]:** Located at the very bottom-left corner of the map. It leads immediately into Room [1].
*   **The Left Side:** 
    *   Room [1] is at the bottom. 
    *   Moving north (upward) from [1], you reach Room [2].
    *   Further north, past the river, is the large Room [3] (the Vast Cavern), which dominates the top-middle of the map.
*   **The Right Side:** 
    *   Room [5] (the Lair) is tucked into the extreme top-right corner.
    *   Room [7] (the Carcass Slope) is in the middle-right.
    *   Room [8] (the Stinking Cave) and the Troll [T] are in the far right section, connecting back to the rest of the map.
*   **Features:**
    *   **Crevasses [c]:** These are scattered, primarily acting as obstacles between rooms.
    *   **Mushroom Fields [m]:** These grow along the riverbanks, both on the left and right sides of the water.
    *   **Narrows [n]:** These are small chokepoints between certain rooms.
    *   **Stalagmites [4]:** These are clustered in the mid-right and bottom-right areas.
    *   **Unstable Areas [6]:** These act as corridors in the bottom-right and between the central rooms.
