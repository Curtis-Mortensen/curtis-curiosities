ASCENT OF THE ROBOT GOD
Originally a vehicle of interstellar conquest, the robot was heavily injured in battle and crash landed. After eons, the internal defenses and repair mechanisms are mostly still working as intended, but some of the drones have achieved sentience and now have motivations of their own.

**General Rule (Top Right):**
An energy field around the God prevents anything from touching it directly other than at the heel of the right foot. Objects that touch the field are ejected 1d20 x 10 feet away. The only light sources in the God are the panel lights in the cockpit and the drones' eyes. Every location has 1d8 drones. They infest the God. Roll on the reaction and function table each time a named area is entered by the party.

### Map Description for Play
If you are describing this to a player, paint the scene as follows:

The "dungeon" is a massive, derelict humanoid robot laying partially on its side, half-buried in the earth. The aesthetic is "grimy sci-fi"—lots of exposed wiring, metallic corridors, and organic debris like bones.

*   **Entry:** You cannot simply climb onto the robot anywhere you like; the outer skin is protected by an energy field. You must enter through the "heel" of the right foot, which is the only exposed, unprotected entry point.
*   **The Interior:** Once inside, it functions like a cramped, metallic labyrinth. There is very little natural light; visibility is provided only by the flickering panel lights of the machinery and the glowing, mechanical eyes of the resident "drones" (small, insect-like repair bots).
*   **Navigation:** 
    *   From the **Right Leg** entry, you move into the **Pelvis** area, which acts as a staging ground full of debris and remains.
    *   From the center, the **Middle Body** acts as a junction box, connecting the arms, legs, and the ascent to the head.
    *   The **Arms** and **Cannons** act as side-chambers, filled with dangerous, automated weaponry and traps.
    *   The **Cockpit** at the top is the "Boss Room" or objective. It is physically separated from the main body by the **Neck**, which is a high-security checkpoint.
*   **The Environment:** The dungeon is "alive." Because the robot is still attempting to maintain itself, the geometry is hostile—conveyor belts move, metallic tendrils exist to remove "foreign contaminants" (the players), and drones are constantly patrolling, performing maintenance tasks that may or may not include treating the party like trash to be cleared or parts to be salvaged.
