**Setting Details & Rules:**
*   **A fragment of a flying pleasure-palace built by salamanders.**
*   **The salamandroids,** robots in their makers' likeness, still roam the halls, maintaining the palace as best they can.
*   **The wobbegong-men** hunt and forage, using indestructible crystal effigies of the salamanders as currency.
*   **The upside-down hermit,** a human castaway exposed to antigravity particles, believes human flesh is the only way to restore him to normalcy.
*   **Doors** — hinged circles of glass with waterproof rubber gaskets. 1 in 6 chance of being locked.
*   **Translucent Walls** — Light and movement can be discerned in adjacent rooms, but no fine detail.
*   **Breaking the Glass** — Walls, floors and ceilings are alchemically strengthened but will fall to concerted effort. When one breaks, roll 1d6 for every adjacent glass surface. On a 5 or 6, that surface will also shatter. This is recursive.
*   **Electricity** — carried in wires embedded in the glass. Damaged areas usually trip circuit-breakers, but these can be reset.
*   **Salamandroids** — 4 metre long brass and glass constructs. Have heating implements in hands, constrict with tail, can interface with wall ports to control lighting, doors, and communicate with each other. Brain is a radioactive, platinum alloy.
*   **Wobbegong-Men** — 3 ft. high, good camouflage, latch on after biting. Have shaggy beardlike dermal lobes, so most outsiders call them "men" regardless of gender.

**Legend:**
*   **● —— indicates open corridors and "stairs".**
*   **Corridors *only* connect their endpoints.**
*   **Stairs are spiral and switchback ramps** built for salamanders’ serpentine bodies. They pass **through** all intersecting rooms.
*   **Seaglass Rock Gongs** — enchanted by wobbegong-men. Playing them temporarily swaps mind with that of nearest seagull.

### Map Description
The map represents an interconnected series of rooms built into the side of a coastal cliff, partially submerged in the ocean. It uses an abstract, "flowchart" style design rather than a traditional dungeon grid.

*   **Structure:** The layout is divided into two distinct levels. The upper level contains the "palace" rooms (Bedroom, Forge, Bathhouse, etc.), which are depicted as boxes connected by thick black lines (corridors). The stairs are marked by large solid dots (●), signifying they cut through floors vertically.
*   **Geography:** The lower section of the map represents the sea floor and coastline. It includes a "Coral reef" area on the far left, "Rock Pools" on the right, and clearly marked lines for "High tide" and "Low tide."
*   **The "Boiler" and Lower Rooms:** In the bottom-center of the map, there is a lower complex containing the "Boiler" and a cluster of smaller, connected rooms (Shaman's Chambers, Nudibranch Farm, Sleeping Chambers, Nursery). These look more cramped and maze-like compared to the larger rooms on the upper level.
*   **Orientation:** The map is oriented top-down. The rooms are labeled clearly in bold text. The "Eroded balcony" juts out from the upper level, acting as a transition point between the palace and the open air/coastline. The entire map has an organic feel, as if the building is integrated into the rock formations of the island itself.
