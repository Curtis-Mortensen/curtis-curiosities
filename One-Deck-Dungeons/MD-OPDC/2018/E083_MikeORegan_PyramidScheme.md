Abandoned by his friends and overcome by the shadows, Lakam is a powerful undead wight who seeks vengeance on the living and then escape from the pyramid. Play him real mean. For me. Anyone he kills turns into a shadow and joins the others in the cracks in the walls.

The column can be raised back up by turning the winch. It is hard work and takes all a person's strength. It is too stiff for most magical effects to turn. When the column is half way up, both exits are blocked.

### Map Description (For Play)

The map is a cutaway, isometric view of a pyramid. Here is how you would describe it to a player:

*   **The Entrance:** To the far left, there is a staircase that winds down into the pyramid, leading to a small landing area.
*   **The Central Pillar:** Dominating the center of the pyramid is a massive, square-shaped stone column that runs vertically from the very top to the bottom. This column acts as a movable "elevator" or gate.
*   **The Mechanism:** At the very top of the pyramid, there is a statue of the god Anubis. The statue holds a staff that serves as a lever.
*   **The Chambers:** The pyramid has two distinct, horizontal levels that intersect with the central column:
    *   **The Upper Chamber:** This room is accessible from the entry stairs. It sits to the left of the central column. This is where the wight (Lakam) is located.
    *   **The Lower Chamber:** This room is on the right side of the central column. It is "open to the elements," meaning it has a large breach in the outer wall leading to the outside desert. This is the lair of the Giant Scorpions.
*   **The Secret Room:** Above the main structure, accessible only when the central pillar is lowered, is the Burial Chamber containing the sarcophagus.
*   **The Winch:** There is a winch mechanism located at the base of the pillar (or near the column's path) that allows players to physically crank the massive stone column back up.
*   **Physics of the Map:** The central column is a "see-saw" style trap. When it is raised (the starting position), it blocks the path to the bottom chamber but opens the way to the top. When it is lowered, it blocks the path to the top/middle level, but allows access to the top burial chamber. The columns are never both open at the same time; at the midway point of moving the column, both chambers are blocked off, effectively trapping anyone inside.
