### Transcription of "Goblinville"

**Title:** Goblinville
**Author:** Edward Green (edowarsblog.wordpress.com)

**General Description:**
Goblinville is a small goblin community located in the outskirts of a vast ruined city. The goblins scavenge from the ruins, ever vigilant against predatory orcs and opportunistic adventurers. Chief Foz has turned their tiny village into a veritable goblin fortress.

**Goblin Mechanics:**
*   **Male goblins:** Wear leather armor and are armed with a mix of spears, short bows, daggers, and throwing stones. 
*   **Goblin females:** Wear no armor, wielding rusty knives or throwing stones. 
*   **Goblin young:** Just throw stones. 
*   **Wealth:** Proficient scavengers, adult goblins are adorned with small bits of crude jewelry (Warriors = 2d6 GP; Females = 1d6 GP; Young = 0 GP). 

**Village Features:**
*   **Barricades:** Block many entrances. They are crude, easily smashed, but doing so makes lots of noise, quickly alerting the goblins. 
*   **Goblin holes:** Interconnected via a vast network of goblin-sized tunnels, allowing goblins to move quickly and unseen. These tunnels are also frequented by the goblin’s giant rat pets and other "guests." 
*   **Pit traps (10’x10’x10’):** Dug along the outer perimeter. They are easily spotted but not easily circumvented. The pits are staked, and the stakes are coated with a toxic excremental brew. Anyone impaled must make a save vs. poison or succumb to a flesh-eating disease.
*   **Walls:** Crumbling mortar and stone (5–6 feet high). Goblins are adept at scrambling up and running along them, fighting from there by hurling spears, arrows, stones, or feces. If the party climbs the walls without precautions, there is a 2-in-6 chance a sentry will notice and raise the alarm.

**Goblin Tunnel Encounters (Roll 1d6 each turn in tunnels):**
1-2: 2d4 Goblin young (they flee, shrieking)
3-4: 3d4 Giant rats (aggressively attack)
5: A hungry giant spider
6: An irate giant centipede

**Room Key:**
1. **Goblin Sentries:** Two goblin sentries guard the entrance. They are vigilant, if not proficient.
2. **Bachelor’s Quarters:** Up to 1d6+6 uncoupled male goblins sleep in rooms to the left and right of the main entrance, where they can respond quickly to threats.
3. **Inner Guard Post:** Two more goblin sentries stand watch near the south barricade. They are less wary than the outer guards.
4. **Couples Quarters:** Mated goblins live in the middle part of the village. At any given time, each room contains 2d6 males, 3d6 females, and 3d6 young.
5. **Pantry:** Captives are imprisoned here. The goblins have installed crude wooden gates on the north and east entrances. Two goblin sentries stand on the western wall. Currently, two orc scouts are imprisoned here.
6. **Foz’s Harem:** The northeastern portion is reserved for the chieftain and his harem. Chief Foz is attended by six choicest goblin females, all armed and fighting like warriors. They wear jewelry worth 1d6x50 GP each.
*   **Area A (Rubbish Pit):** Along the north wall is a rubbish pit. Giant rat pets make nests here, along with an otyugh hidden in the refuse. Each round spent scrounging yields 1d6x5 GP, but prompts attacks by 1d4 giant rats and the otyugh.
7. **Foz’s Throneroom:** Chief Foz (the ‘Firewalker’) is a massive 3-HD red-hued mutant. He is immune to fire and often walks the fire pit to prove his power. He wears chainmail and wields a +1 mace. Jewel-encrusted chains adorn his shoulders (900 GP). He carries a jug of ‘goblin juice.’ He is protected by four 2-HD goblin bodyguards wearing chainmail, spears, and 1d6x100 GP in jewelry. If attacked, he alerts the harem and nearby goblins.
