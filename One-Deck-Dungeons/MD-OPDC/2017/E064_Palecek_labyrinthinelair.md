### 3. Detailed Map Description for Navigation
This map acts as a "web" of encounters. Imagine the dungeon as a central hub with several sprawling wings.

*   **The Entrance:** Located on the far right side of the map. Entering here leads immediately into a **Chessboard Room**, which holds the **Obsidian Key**. 
*   **The Eastern Wing (Entrance area):** Moving past the Chessboard room, you enter a "Hallway of Traps." This area is densely packed with hazards: a "Crushing Wall Trap," a "Mimic Door," "Golden Arrows," "Molten Gold," and a "Slime Room" filled with mechanical spiders. This area leads toward the center of the map.
*   **The Central Hub:** The map is bisected by a **"Great Chasm."** To cross, adventurers must navigate around it. The chasm is flanked by an "Invisible Hand" trap and a pit filled with snakes.
*   **The Top Wing:** Above the chasm lies an "Alchemy Laboratory" and a "Throne Room." This section is connected back to the start through a series of "Floating Skulls" and "Swinging Pendulum Blades."
*   **The Western Wing:** This area contains the "Lair of Sherma the Custodian" and a "Torture Chamber." There is a vertical section here marked "Climb the Tower," where players can acquire the **Ivory Key**. Above this, a "Maze of Non-Euclidean Geometry" guarded by a "Skull Face Door" serves as a complex obstacle.
*   **The Southern/Bottom Wing:** This area is the "business end" of the dungeon. It holds the **Library of Forbidden Lore** (rotating bookcases, rare books, and book-based monsters). Further south is the **Evil Chapel** and a **Portal to Hell.**
*   **The Final Goal:** In the very bottom-center, you find the **True Vault**. It is separate from the "Fake Tomb and Vault" located further up. The True Vault is clearly marked as requiring **five keys** to open. It contains the "Vast Treasure" and the "Phylactery." 

**Flow Note:** The map is highly non-linear. Many corridors loop back into themselves, and there are several "teleport" zones (the "Maze of Teleport Glyphs") that suggest paths may warp the players to different parts of the dungeon unexpectedly.
