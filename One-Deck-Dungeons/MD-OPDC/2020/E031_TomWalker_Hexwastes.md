#### Generation procedure
At each intersection roll a d6 for the left branch and a d6 for the right branch. Evens: the way is free and clear. 1s: The pathway is broken and impassable; 3s: Creatures; 5s: Beings. Combine multiple Creature and Being results into a single encounter at the intersection.

**Double 6:** instead of foggy emptiness you can just make out in the hex ahead a “floor” about 100 feet down. This glass-like layer is the lid of the Queen Megawasp’s birth hexagon. Any loud or forceful action nearby has a 10% cumulative chance of waking the ravenous Queen [stats as Wyvern].

If ever there seems no way forward then retrace your steps; Creatures and Beings will have moved on and broken pathways may now be inexplicably fixed.

1. **6d6 Stick-slugs** – [as Giant Rats, never fall].
2. **Obsidipus Hex** - [as Grick or Giant Squid, never fall]. Semi-intelligent predator, hunts Stick-slugs and anything else that doesn't pose a threat. Six tentacles, each 30' long, and a powerful hard beak. Once dead its tentacle arms can be stuck to the obsidian and remain in place providing a useful hand-hold.
3. **Pteranodon** – aims to carry off human-sized or smaller prey.

#### The HEXWASTES
Slender obsidian walkways loom precipitously out of the thick fog in a huge hexagonal grid that stretches many days travel hence. They are very slippery, slightly convex, and impervious to mundane damage. Anyone falling has a few moments to scream before they disappear out of sight into the depths of the fog. PCs can move and act in single file on the walkways without penalty; if they move or act two abreast they have a chance of falling [Dex save or similar according to system]. Any PCs hit in melee, or for 4 or more hp of ranged damage, may fall [Dex save]. An adjacent ally can catch a falling ally (automatically) but must drop a held item to do so. Any dropped or thrown items immediately slip off the walkway. Anyone and anything that falls untethered is lost to the void (in the absence of any magical salvation). While PCs are roped together they suffer a penalty to fighting and other actions. If a roped PC falls the next PC(s) on the rope may also be pulled off [Dex or Str save]. Alternatively the next roped PC can jump off the *other* side of the walkway to create a counter-balance. Dangling PCs can be hauled up given time, but to do it quickly is a test of strength.

### Map Description (For Play)
The map serves as a conceptual visual aid rather than a fixed grid. You can describe it to players as follows:

*   **The Structure:** The map displays a massive, skeletal structure made of slender, black lines. These lines form an infinite-looking hexagonal grid. There is no "floor" or "ground"—only these narrow, raised obsidian beams connecting at hexagonal points.
*   **Perspective:** The view is from a high-angle, slightly isometric perspective, looking down into a deep, impenetrable fog. The lines of the grid disappear into the haze in every direction.
*   **The "Birth Hexagon":** Near the center, a group of hexagons is shaded differently or appears slightly more filled in, suggesting a larger or more solid platform compared to the thin lines of the rest of the web. This is the "birth hexagon" of the Queen Megawasp.
*   **The Creatures:** A small, winged insect-like silhouette (the Queen) is drawn near the upper-left of the grid.
*   **For Gameplay:** When using this for a session, you do not need to map the entire area. The map represents a **procedural void.** Simply draw a single point (an intersection) where the players start, and as they choose to move "left" or "right," draw the next connecting lines based on the dice rolls (1, 3, or 5). The map is meant to look empty and fragile, emphasizing that the players are walking on a tightrope over an abyss.
