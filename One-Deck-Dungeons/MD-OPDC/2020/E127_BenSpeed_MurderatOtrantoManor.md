**Title:** Murder at Otranto Manor
**Author:** By Ben Speed

**The Setup:**
You were paid to attend a wedding only to witness the groom, Sir Conrad, gurgle and fall dead at the precipice. The wealthy Baron Manfred Otranto seeks the killer... and something else. What will you do?

**d8 Servants’ Rumours:**
1. The manor is haunted by a dead giant. (False)
2. There’s a hidden passageway somewhere in the manor. (True)
3. Majordomo Osbright recently bought arsenic. (False)
4. House Otranto is famously rich. (True)
5. A servant is secretly in love with a noble. (True)
6. Baron Manfred is a vampire. (False)
7. Manfred is a cruel spendthrift to all but Lady Matilda. (True)
8. A skeletal friar was seen praying in the library. (False)

**What’s Happening:**
*   **A:** Baron Manfred sees Lady Isabella as his last chance for an heir & wants Father Jerome to marry them tonight.
*   **B:** Lady Isabella is trying to escape the manor and seek sanctuary in the nearby church.
*   **C:** Father Jerome is scared of Baron Manfred and will do anything to avoid his wrath.
*   **D:** Theodore is in love with Lady Isabella and (falsely) thinks she’s in love with him too.
*   **E:** Theodore poisoned Sir Conrad’s wine while cleaning his room & left a letter on Isabella’s desk confessing both his love & the murder.

**Character Relationships Table:**
*   **Baron Manfred:** Will do anything for an heir.
*   **Lady Isabella:** Baroness Hippolita is unable to have more children.
*   **Baroness Hippolita:** Matilda assumes the best of everyone’s intentions.
*   **Lady Matilda:** Theodore is in love with Lady Isabella.
*   **Theodore:** Lady Matilda is in love with Theodore.
*   **Father Jerome:** Theodore recently obtained poison.
*   **Majordomo Osbright:** The secret passage to the church is in the library.

**Rules and Mechanics:**
All characters begin in room (1) a few moments after Sir Conrad dies, just after dusk. Amidst the screaming, weeping, and scurrying about in frightened confusion, Baron Manfred bellows louder than all. “Quiet! Guards, take arms at all portals and don’t let anyone leave this floor until we find the murderer! Isabella! Where’s Isabella?” He then heads off, leaving the PCs, two guards, and servants in (1) with Father Jerome administering last rites over the body.

*   **Timers:** Initially, and every five minutes in real time (use a timer), NPCs may be found in different locations. If PCs are in (1) or the hallway when the timer runs out, they might encounter an NPC en route.
*   **Manfred:** Wants to find Lady Isabella and bring her to Father Jerome to be married as soon as possible. He starts in a random room (d10) then methodically moves up in sequence (4, 5, 6, etc.) each interval.
*   **Isabella:** Wants to avoid Manfred, escape, and/or find help. Roll d10 every interval; she stays & hides on 1 or 0.
*   **Theodore:** Starts in room (4) to leave the letter, pretending to clean, then moves down in sequence (7, 6, 5, etc.). If he finds Isabella, he takes the hallway to the passage in room (7), hiding one interval in each room on the way.
*   **Lady Matilda:** Frantically searches for Theodore; d10 each interval, re-roll on 0. If they’re ever in the same room, Theodore is unable to leave on his own.
*   **Baroness Hippolita:** A tearful Baroness Hippolita retires to room (9), offering her jewelry to anyone with a sympathetic ear.
*   **Guards:** There are two guards on patrol in a loop of the hallway and (1); they move towards noise & suspicious motion.
*   **Servants:** There are (d4-1) servants in any room the PCs visit. On a result of 1, it’s Majordomo Osbright.
*   **Difficulty:** If needed, a fight with Manfred should be difficult, Theodore & the guards challenging, and all others easy.
*   **Environment:** In Otranto Manor, all windows are barred and all doors are unlocked. G represents a posted guard.
