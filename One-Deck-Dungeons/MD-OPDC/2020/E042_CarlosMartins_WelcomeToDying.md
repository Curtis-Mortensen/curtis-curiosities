Run this scenario when a Player Character dies. This is a system-agnostic Dungeon. Play it with your favorite game rules. Behold the image and describe the scene as it pleases you... Don’t worry about spatial references - the realm of the dead has its own dimensionality. Be weird! Ask questions and build the lore as you go.

You are Dead. There’s no other way of putting it. You knew this day would come... But not this soon. You hear the calling from the Dark Mistress. She awaits you... Since you were born. Does she want to collect your soul? Or does she have an offer to make? Can you return to the living world?

Because this map is non-spatial, you should treat it as a **flowchart of consciousness**. To guide a player who cannot see the map, describe it as a descent through a shifting, dreamlike landscape.

1.  **The Entry Point (1):** Start at the top, which represents the soul being pulled from the body ("The Pool").
2.  **The Flow:** The map connects via a series of paths that feel like organic veins or cracks in reality. You can describe the path as branching downward.
    *   From **The Pool (1)**, the path immediately branches toward the **Egg Shell (2)** and **Valley of the Wisps (3)**.
    *   **The Tea Party (4)** and **The Vault (5)** act as side-paths or "dead-ends" of experience, hanging off the main descent.

    *   **The Oblivion River (8)** and **Elysian Fields (7)** are areas that threaten to trap the soul on its way down to the center.
    *   **The Center/Bottom (9):** The "Mother" is the focal point at the base of the image. The entire architecture of the page visually funnels the player toward her.
    *   **Peripheral Threats:** The **Behemoth (10)**, **The Hound (11)**, and **Fate’s Web (12)** are clustered off to the side, representing obstacles that exist on the periphery of this afterlife realm.

**DM Tip for Navigation:** When running this, don't ask, "Which direction do you go?" Instead, ask, "What aspect of your death are you confronting next?" and point them to the corresponding number on the map based on their answer.
