**Tomb of Immolation**
*Cryptographer: Michael Harmon | Scribe: Bill Innis*

**Introduction:** The Tomb of Immolation holds an ancient necromancer whose name is lost to the ages. Upon his death, the necromancer’s fanatical servants mummified his remains using a secret, forbidden embalming process, transforming him into an ever-burning undead creature of great power. The entrance to the tomb is in the cellar of a long-forgotten fortress on a hill overlooking the village of Sacred Springs.

**Room Keys:**

1. **Entrance Hall:** Ten alcoves hold upright, elaborately carved stone sarcophagi. The lids open easily like a door to reveal inanimate skeletal remains. Detect magic on a sarcophagus reveals conjuration magic. Stairs are difficult terrain. **Encounter:** Once every 24 hours, after a creature enters the tomb, 5 zombies and 5 skeletons emerge from the sarcophagi. A flaming skeleton in Area 2 will join any combat that occurs here; it wears magical boots of spider climbing. **Treasure:** Each inanimate skeleton has 1d3 pieces of copper jewelry worth 10gp.

2. **Shrine:** Obsidian dais in the middle of the room with a magic circle carved into it. Lava slowly flows from the North door out the East door. Touching the lava causes High fire damage. **Puzzle:** Any creature that stands on the dais and utters the word “Anocharun” causes the North door to shut for ten minutes; the lava drains out in one minute, and covers the floor one minute after the door reopens. **Trap:** Intense heat causes Low fire damage to every creature that starts a turn in this room; this damage continues even when lava is drained. **Secret Door to Area 5:** Easy to find; faint words in Abyssal read “Burn barriers.” The door opens for 10 rounds when damaged by fire. **Locked Door to Area 3:** South door has an Average lock.

3. **Devil’s Deal:** Average lock on the door; seven alcoves encrusted with glowing white crystals hold a Deadly devil. **Encounter:** Nargalux the ice devil sits in the middle of the room, trapped here millennia ago. The crystals are immune to non-good damage and contain Nargalux, absorbing its power to keep the tomb’s magic operating. Nargalux wants to escape and offers a deal to help PCs navigate the tomb in exchange for freedom. **Puzzle:** Nargalux’s deal is for the PCs to destroy the crystals. It knows the command word for the North door in Area 2 but will not reveal it until freed. If freed, it lies, claiming the PCs' souls belong to it, and gives the hint: “It is Anocharun, the master of this tomb, whose name should be uttered rightly, while left unsaid.”

4. **Priests’ Rest:** Short hallway with a floor half covered by lava; take Average fire damage at the start of each turn. The two North alcoves have sarcophagi; the two South sarcophagi have been destroyed by lava. Detect magic reveals abjuration. The Southeast alcove has a hole in the floor. **Puzzle:** Any creature that enters the Northeast alcove’s sarcophagus, closes the lid, and utters “Anocharun” receives fire protection for one hour. **Trap:** Entering the Northwest sarcophagus and uttering “Anocharun” curses the creature so they cannot heal until the curse is removed.

5. **Stairs:** Difficult terrain for Medium or larger creatures. Alcoves contain sarcophagi. A giant, rune-covered stone boulder rolls at 60” per round from 5A to the pit, taking two rounds. **Trap 1:** The boulder teleports from the bottom of the pit back up to 5A in a loop. It automatically hits corporeal creatures in its path for High damage. **Trap 2:** 25’ deep pit with a magic circle that causes the boulder to teleport to the top of the stairs; the heat from Area 2 causes low fire damage to anyone in the pit. **Encounter:** Every alcove has an upright sarcophagus that randomly conjures a ghoul when a creature comes within 10’. Each can conjure undead once per day. This ends if Nargalux is destroyed or leaves Area 3. **Treasure:** Magic weapon and average coins on skeletal remains. **Exit:** The dungeon exit is locked with an Impossible lock; the key is on the mummy in Area 8.

6. **Lava Pool:** A natural cave conduit from the Elemental Plane of Fire. **Encounter:** A fire salamander maintains the conduit. If destroyed, the lava is permanently stopped.

7. **Guardhouse:** Unfurnished room with three alcoves encrusted with deep purple crystals. **Encounter:** 9 flaming skeletons mill about. They attack on sight and investigate noises in Areas 5 and 8. 1d6 destroyed flaming skeletons reform here every dawn until all 9 are present. Anyone not fire-resistant starting a turn within 5’ takes Low fire damage. If Nargalux is destroyed or has escaped Area 3, skeletons do not reform.

8. **Necromancer’s Sanctum:** Obsidian dais with a flaming magic circle and an upright, gem-studded sarcophagus. **Doors:** All three doors into the chamber have Difficult locks; the 4th door has an Average lock with an Average poison dart trap. **Encounter:** Difficult, immolation mummy emerges from the sarcophagus; it is immune to fire and its attacks cause fire. Remaining skeletons from Area 6 enter in 4 rounds. **Trap:** The sarcophagus is an open gate to the Elemental Plane of Fire; entering it is one-way. **Treasure:** Exceptional treasure in the Difficult locked chest and a key to the tomb’s exit in Area 5.

### Map Description
The map depicts a multi-chambered, interconnected dungeon layout:

*   **The Main Path:** You enter at the top left (Area 1), a rectangular hall with ten alcoves. A passage leads south into the Shrine (Area 2). From the Shrine, you can head east into a passage that leads toward the center of the map (Area 5), or south into the Devil’s Deal (Area 3).
*   **The Central Hub (Area 5/5A):** This is the heart of the dungeon. It consists of a large staircase ("5") that runs vertically through the middle of the complex. "5A" is the top of the stairs where the boulder trap begins. The stairs lead down into a 25-foot pit. This area connects to the Secret Passage from the Shrine, the Guardhouse (Area 7), and leads toward the Exit at the top-center.
*   **The Lava Conduit:** Lava flows from the north, passes through the Shrine (Area 2), and feeds into the Priests’ Rest (Area 4) and the Lava Pool (Area 6) near the bottom-left of the map.
*   **The Sanctum (Area 8):** This is a large, circular chamber at the far right of the map. It is isolated, accessible only through the paths branching out past the Guardhouse (Area 7). 
*   **Connectivity:** The map is designed as a partial loop. You can move from the entrance to the shrine, then take a secret path to the central stairs (5). From the stairs, you have to navigate hazards to reach the Guardhouse (7) and eventually the Necromancer's Sanctum (8) at the far eastern side. The final Exit is located at the top-middle, requiring a key found in the final room.
