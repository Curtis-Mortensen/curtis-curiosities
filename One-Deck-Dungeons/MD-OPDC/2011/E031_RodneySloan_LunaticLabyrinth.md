### Map Description for Gameplay
To use this map, you will treat the 25 squares at the bottom of the page as independent, rearrangeable tiles. If you are playing with someone who cannot see the board, here is how the labyrinth is structured:

**1. The Mechanics of the Map:**
*   **The Concept:** This is a modular maze. The dungeon is not fixed; the paths change as characters interact with the environment.
*   **The Tiles:** There are 25 total squares. Each square features stone-paved paths (colored in shades of brown and gold) set against a black background, representing the solid stone walls of the labyrinth. 
*   **The Pieces:**
    *   **The Y-Sections:** There is a "Start" tile and an "End" tile. These are shaped like a "Y" junction.
    *   **Standard Tiles:** These include straight corridors, 90-degree corner turns, T-junctions, 4-way intersections, and dead ends.
    *   **Tokens:** There are five circular tokens included. Three feature footprint icons, and two feature claw-mark icons. These are used to mark where the players are or where monsters/traps are located.

**2. How to Play:**
*   **Initial Setup:** Cut all 25 squares into individual tiles. Place them in a 5x5 grid randomly. Place the "Start" tile in one corner and the "End" tile in the opposite corner.
*   **Hidden Information:** Because you hide the tiles from the players, the dungeon remains a mystery. When a character moves into a new area, you reveal the tile.
*   **Interaction:** When a player uses an "artifact" or "command word" to turn a tile, you physically rotate that square 90 or 180 degrees. This will change where the paths connect, potentially opening new routes or cutting off existing ones, forcing players to navigate a shifting environment.
*   **Boundaries:** If a path leads to the edge of the 5x5 grid, it hits a wall and is considered "closed."

**3. Strategic Advice for the Narrator:**
Since you are the one controlling the map, you can use the tiles to create "loops" or "dead ends" on the fly. If you want to increase the difficulty, rotate tiles when the players aren't looking to make them feel lost in the "Lunatic Labyrinth."
