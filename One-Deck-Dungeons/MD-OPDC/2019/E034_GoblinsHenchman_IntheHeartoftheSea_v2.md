### Description for Gameplay (Map System)

To use this system, you don't need a static map; you need to understand the **"Hex Flower"** structure. Imagine a cluster of 19 hexagons arranged to form one large hexagon (three rows of 3, 4, 5, 4, 3 hexes).

**The Core Mechanics:**
1.  **The Start:** You begin on a specific hex marked "Start."
2.  **The Roll:** Every day, you roll 2D6. 
3.  **The Navigation:** You look at the "Navigation Directions" diagram in the center of the page. It maps the 2D6 results to directions:
    *   **12**: Move to the hex directly above.
    *   **11, 10**: Move to the top-right.
    *   **2, 3**: Move to the bottom-right.
    *   **6, 7**: Move to the hex directly below.
    *   **8, 9**: Move to the bottom-left.
    *   **4, 5**: Move to the top-left.
4.  **Legacy:** The "new hex" you land on becomes your current position for the *next* day’s roll. This creates a "random walk" that mimics moving through weather or sea conditions.
5.  **Boundaries:** If the move would take you off the edge of the grid, "wrap around" to the other side of the flower. If a direction is marked with an "X", that move is impossible; you stay in your current hex.

**The Three Grids:**
*   **Travel & Encounters (Left Grid):** This contains icons for the types of encounters you have. It has a specific "Destination" hex (a flag icon) and "Landfall" hexes. The hexes are populated with silhouettes of ships, sea monsters, islands, and aquatic tridents. If you land on a hex with a creature, you roll to see what it is based on the keys provided.
*   **Cover & Precipitation (Middle Grid):** This grid ranges from sun icons (clear weather) to cloud and rain icons (precipitation). There is a central "Hazard" warning sign in the middle.
*   **Wind & Waves (Right Grid):** This grid is organized by intensity. The "calm" water (0–5 knots) is at the bottom, and as you move upward through the hexes, the wind/waves get progressively rougher, moving from 5–15 up to 55+ knots (represented by icons of increasing wave size and wind swirls). 

**How to play:**
You track your position on all three grids simultaneously. Each day, you roll 2D6 for each grid, move your "token" according to the navigation directions, and read the outcome of the hex you landed on for that grid. This generates a cohesive narrative: for example, you might be in a "Dangerous Creature" hex on the Travel map, under "Storm" conditions on the Precipitation map, with "35–55 knot winds" on the Wind map.
