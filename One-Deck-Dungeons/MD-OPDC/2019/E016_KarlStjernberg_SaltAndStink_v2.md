## Transcription

This document, titled **"Salt & Stink!"**, is a one-page tabletop RPG adventure module designed for first-level characters. Written and illustrated by Karl Stjernberg, it uses a grim, nautical aesthetic featuring undead "Duck-Pirates." The layout is densely packed, featuring hand-drawn black-and-white maps, lists of random encounters, and room-by-room descriptions. The tone is humorous but dark, centered on a beach covered in shipwrecks and a rotting whale corpse repurposed as a pirate lair.

### Transcription of Written Text

#### **Introduction & Beach (Start Here)**
**Salt & Stink!**
Shipwrecked among pirates, parasites & the paranormal inside an undead whale. For 1st level adventurers.

**Start Point A: Trash-filled beach.** 
Everything is covered in mud, barnacles & algae. Two distinct landmarks are visible: a massive mouth-like cave and a small opening at the peak of a mountain of trash.

**Point B: Reeking “Cave Mouth” (Entrance to 1.1)**
An overpowering stench followed by a rhythmic, warm wind. Filthy “stalactites” surround the edges. The “cave” occasionally closes, slowly crushing anything stuck in between.

**Point C: Trash Mountain (Entrance to 2.1)**
Travelling to the top is hard & hazardous; it’s easy to slip & slide down, risking injury. Two surly undead Duck-Pirates guard the entrance. All Ducks in this adventure are armed with sabres & flintlock guns.

**“What do I find on this sad excuse for a beach?” (Roll 1d12)**
*Everyone gets to roll twice. Treat duplicates as “1”.*
1. Rusted weapon. Will break after a couple of uses.
2. Shrimp-filled Leather Armor. Itchy but protective.
3. Letter in a bottle. Contains random 1st level spell.
4. Muddy Backpack. Contains 1d6 torches, flint & tinder.
5. Mast, sticking out of the rubble. On it, 60 feet of damp rope!
6. Flintlock Gun! Great damage, but 1-in-6 risk of exploding.
7. Random potion in small bag. Roll 1d4: 1. Grow Gills! 2. Gain Limited Flight! 3. Turn Invisible! 4. Breathe Fire!
8. Peg-Leg. As club. Contains hidden treasure map!
9. Half Drowned Goblin. Cowardly, friendly, low on HP.
10. Skeleton. A nearby sabre, eye patch, and parrot skeleton. Inside the parrot skull is a small but valuable ruby. Anyone who takes the ruby is haunted by a squawking parrot ghost.
11. Fishing net. + 1d6 dead fish.
12. Oil Lamp. Nothing happens if polished, as the genie inside has drowned. Light turns on/off on command. Endless use.

#### **Inside the Decaying Rot-Whale (Top View)**
**1.1 Inside the Stinking Cave Mouth**
Murky, waist-high water. A skeleton sits grinning on a small island. Searching the water: 1d6: 1. Rancid Eel (bites hard); 2. Poisonous Puffer Fish (nausea); 3. Barnacle Covered Shield (+AC); 4. Slimy Egg Cluster (lure); 5. Helpful Crabling (follows party); 6. Golden Sable (value drops if used in combat).

**1.2 Disgusting, Fleshy Tunnels**
Walls pulsate with dark magic. 1d8 Giant isopods nibble on feet (peacefully, unless attacked).

**1.3 Rubble & Rot**
Roof collapsed. 4 undead duck-pirates are excavating with pickaxes. They ignore anyone if undisturbed.

**1.4 Grumpy Oyster, planning and scheming**
Home to a huge Oyster. She will try to persuade the party to overthrow The Captain (2.6) to prove that spitting out valuable pearls is hard work.

**1.5 Swirling Vortex & Slip-n-Slide**
The vortex sucks the careless into the Acid Pool (1.6) below.

**1.6 (Slow acting) Acid Pool**
Dissolves organic material in hours. The walls are slick. 1d10 valuable pearls at the bottom.

**1.7 Hard Working Parasites**
A collective of 20+ parasitic Lamprey-Folk feed on the whale and make it move. They are hungry and will lure adventurers in for a snack.

**1.8 / 2.8 The Great Barnacle-Guard (Impervious to damage)**
A massive, burly Barnacle-Man blocks the path between chambers. Will summon 1d6 Undead Duck-Pirates to escort people to the Acid Pool (1.6) or Prison Cells (2.6).

---

#### **Trash Mountain (Side View)**
**2.1 Guarded Entrance**
2 Undead Duck-Pirates insist on escorting intruders to the Prison Cells (2.4).

**2.2 Poorly Constructed Tunnel**
In disrepair & prone to collapse!

**2.3 Haunted Cargo Hold**
Home to a depressed poltergeist that lifts a barrel to throw, then puts it back down with a sigh. Can be cheered up.

**2.4 Dilapidated Prison Cells**
Rusty bars. A skeleton is chained to the wall. The Prison Guard Duck with the key sleeps on a chair out of reach.

**2.5 Crew Quarters**
10-ish Ducks complaining, playing cards, arm-wrestling, or sleeping in hammocks.

**2.6 Captain’s Quarters**
Bearded, lich-like Duck. Evil. Needs manpower. Will force adventurers to do tasks.

**"If you want freedom, you’ll have to work for it!" (Roll 1d6)**
1. Cheer up the poltergeist (2.3).
2. Fix the tunnels (2.2).
3. Convince the Oyster (1.4) that life at sea is okay.
4. Make an example out of one of the ducks.
5. Win a drinking contest (ancient rum).
6. "Donate" blood to the Lamprey-Folk (1.7).
*Finish 3 tasks for freedom, or join the crew to loot a nearby town!*

**2.7 Poorly Hidden (but well guarded) Treasure Chamber**
A fat Chest-Mimic guards thousands of gold coins. Obeys only the Captain.

### Map Description for Navigation

The map is split into two distinct mechanical diagrams:

**1. The Rot-Whale (Top View)**
This is a sprawling, organic layout resembling the internal organs of a giant fish. 
*   **Entrance:** Point B (Cave Mouth) leads directly into **1.1**, the central watery chamber.
*   **Pathing:** From **1.1**, there are branching paths leading north to **1.3** (Rubble), east to **1.4** (Oyster), and south to **1.2** (Tunnels).
*   **Hazardous Area:** The eastern/central section contains **1.5** (Vortex) which forces movement down into **1.6** (Acid Pool).
*   **Upper Chambers:** Paths wind toward the upper-right where **1.7** (Parasites) reside, eventually funneling to **1.8** (The Barnacle-Guard), which acts as the transition point to the Trash Mountain diagram.

**2. The Trash Mountain (Side View)**
This is a vertical cross-section showing a stack of ship hulls and debris.
*   **Entrance:** Point C leads into the top entrance (**2.1**).
*   **Vertical Progression:** The map works like a mineshaft. You start at the top (**2.1**) and move down a diagonal tunnel (**2.2**) into the Cargo Hold (**2.3**). 
*   **The Descent:** Beneath that are the Prison Cells (**2.4**) and the Crew Quarters (**2.5**).
*   **The Bottom:** The Captain’s Quarters (**2.6**) and the Treasure Chamber (**2.7**) are at the very base of the pile. 
*   **Connection:** A tunnel labeled **2.8** curves back upward, linking the bottom of the Trash Mountain back to the Barnacle-Guard at **1.8**, allowing for a loop between the two areas.
