### Transcription of Written Text

#### [Header]
**CARAVAN**
*By Michael Calleia*

#### {Background}
“Red Duck” has a valuable & highly flammable cargo to deliver. To avoid trouble, he put together a motley caravan to blend into & for safety in numbers. Needing a bit of muscle in case of highway bandits or other trouble, Red Duck is looking for a group to protect the caravan. But, what intrigue & secrets does this ensemble cast of non-playing characters hold?

#### {Notes for the GM}
The caravan has seven wagons, each with a short description including denizens & in some cases GM information (Secrets & Rumors). In this adventure the action unfolds based on both distance traveled & elapsed time. The party can disrupt any & all events in the timeline—in fact, that’s the point of the adventure, for characters to interact with NPCs & reshape events that would have happened without their intervention. Secrets are generally known only by those on a particular wagon. Rumors can be shared by any NPC. For GM inspiration before running this adventure watch: *Convoy, Smokey & the Bandit* or *Every Which Way but Loose*.

#### {Introduction}
Hearing rumors of opportunities in the North Country, our intrepid adventurers head to the Prancing Centaur to seek a way to get there. The inn-keep mentions Red Duck is in search of a group just like theirs & makes introductions. Red Duck tells the party the trip takes three days by the High Road or five by the Low Road—the current plan is to take the High Road. If the party is hired, the trip begins the next morning.

#### ~Wagon Descriptions~

**~Red Duck~**
Specializes in hauling exotic & rare liquids, spices, dyes & goods that require care. He is a legend among road traders.
*   **Red Duck:** 41 male human.
*   **{Secrets}:** The cargo is Greek/Alchemist’s Fire.
*   **{Rumors}:** Red Duck is trying to avoid paying taxes—truth is, taxes are already settled.

**~Journey-folks’ Wagon~**
Tools, lumber, and supplies for construction.
*   **Denizens:** Barba Laughingseeker (32 female half-orc, leader), Arget Bolge (53 male halfling, cook), Isabel de Bolbee (38 female human, carpenter), Prose Rowith (22 male halfling, carpenter), Nigrate Stonesooth (97 female dwarf, mason), Peronell de Grey (15 female human, miller), Gee D’Arn (28 male human, sheep shearer).

**~The Lady Miath’s Coach~**
An austere, black coach.
*   **Denizens:** Thori Sapphire-braids (155 male dwarf coachman), Lady Miath (41 female human).
*   **{Secrets}:** Lady Miath was replaced by a Doppleganger; the coach floor is littered.
*   **{Rumors}:** The original guards fell ill after carousing.

**~Widow’s Wonder Players~**
Performers for the wealthy.
*   **Denizens:** Ilian “Black Widow” Mayday (half-elf bard), apprentices: Bryce (20 male human), Millicent (20 female human), Loreena (young adult half-elf).
*   **{Secrets}:** Ilian’s husband’s soul is held by Perceval; she is forced to do his bidding/assassinations. Apprentices fear Perceval.

**~Perceval’s Books~**
Traveling used books/scrolls.
*   **Denizen:** Perceval (elderly male elf).
*   **{Secrets}:** Evil magic-user enthralled by a demon. Burned the bridge. Holds the soul of Ilian’s husband. Has a portal mirror. Will kidnap a journey-folk on the 4th night.

**~Tim’s Casks~**
Casks for an abbey.
*   **Denizen:** Winifred Stonesworn (29 female human).
*   **{Secrets}:** A cursed gem is hidden in one of the casks, causing the wagon to break down.

**~The Pig Wagon~**
Pigs headed to market.
*   **Denizen:** Ben Haman (44 male human), dog: Kenworth.
*   **{Secrets}:** Hidden in a “dog house” structure is a wereboar child. A parent is tracking them for a rescue on the full moon.

### Map/Timeline Description for Non-Visual Users
The "Map" is not a traditional grid map of a location, but rather a **linear timeline graphic** located at the very top of the page. 

*   **The Track:** It consists of a long, horizontal horizontal line representing the journey from "Day 1" through "Day 5."
*   **Visual Indicators:** 
    *   There are small markers on the line indicating specific geographic events: a "Bridge Out" icon between Day 2 and Day 3, and a "Road Block" icon at the end of Day 5.
    *   There is a branch in the line showing a "High Road" (top) and a "Low Road" (bottom), indicating the alternate route the caravan is forced to take after the bridge breaks.
*   **Function:** For a player who cannot see the map, it is best described as a "countdown clock." The DM tracks the current day/time on this line. Every time an event is reached, the DM refers to the "Encounters" table (described in the text section above) to see what happens to the caravan. It serves to pace the adventure, ensuring the party is constantly moving toward the "climax" of the 5th day.
