### Transcribed Text

**Title:** Escape from Dead Bird Isle
**Author:** Glynn Seal

**Introduction:**
The players are shipwrecked during a great storm. They awake on a beach, little of use from the ship they travelled on remains. The island is covered in a dense jungle, and dead birds lie on the ground in most places. There is nothing but ocean as far as the eye can see. The players need to figure out how to escape the island. They need to collect all six Godseyes, and find the portal chamber. 
*Hex to Hex distance: 1/2 mile. Half movement through jungle.*

**Dangers:**
The waters around the island have dangerous currents and sharks! The jungle has many large, chitinous insects and snakes. The volcano spews lava and noxious gases.

**Locations:**
*   **A - Black Plateau Temple:** This stepped pyramid temple sits on a 100ft high rocky plateau. Inside is the same as the Bird Temple (Location F), without the secret caves behind the statue. Two Godseyes can be found in the statue.
*   **B - Jungle Temple:** This stepped pyramid is in the heart of the island jungle. Inside is also the same as the Bird Temple (Location F), without the secret caves behind the statue. Only one Godseye can be found in the statue here. The other was stolen by a villager.
*   **C - Volcano:** Still active, the volcano occasionally spews hot lava and poisonous gases (the reason for the dead birds and deserted village).
*   **D - Deserted Village:** A bird-worshipping tribe once lived in the huts of this deserted village. Their skeletons remain. One has a Godseye in a leather pouch.
*   **E - Talonstones:** A series of 30’ tall spires in a ring formation, created by Lava Ants.
*   **F - Island Bird Temple:** See below.
*   **G - Hidden Coracle:** An old coracle and a paddle is hidden in the undergrowth here. Suitable for one person. It has a small hole which can be plugged or used as is, providing the water is bailed out occasionally. A brave paddler could use it to get from the large island to Location F.

**The Island Bird Temple (Inner Map Details):**
*   **1 - Entrance:** The entrance into the stepped pyramid is weathered and crumbling. It could collapse at any point.
*   **2 - Stairway:** Crumbling stone steps descend into the darkness below. Old traps still exist.
*   **3 - Temple:** A temple to the bird god, Corvoth. The room contains four semi-circular niches, each is empty but once contained offerings to the god left by the villagers. The statue is intact and made of stones and mudbricks, and painted black with a gold beak. The two eyes are rubies worth 100 gold coins each. These rubies, called The Godseyes, are required to activate the portal to the mainland. The floor is marked with strange symbols carved into the floor, as well as symbols carved into the entrance archway. These suggest (to someone versed in the arcane arts) that some kind of portal exists somewhere on the island.
*   **Location F Only:** A secret door can be found in the plinth, behind the statue.
*   **4 - Descending Stair:** Behind the secret door, a carved stairway descends down into the cold.
*   **5 - Waterfall Cave:** A large waterfall descends into the cave. A submerged tunnel leads into Location 7. The stream creating the waterfall leads to a hidden cave at the base of the cliffs at the edge of the small island.
*   **6 - Behind the Waterfall:** A chamber that contains a skeletal bird guardian protecting a nest of long-smashed eggshells and some odd bits of loot.
*   **7 - Portal Chamber:** The waterfilled tunnels emerge into the portal chamber. An archway with six recesses is set into one wall of the cave. The Godseyes can be placed here, and the portal will activate.

### Map Description for Navigation

**The Island (Main Map):**
The island is shaped somewhat like a kidney bean, with the northern tip labeled "Shipwrecked." 
*   **North:** The Black Plateau Temple (A) sits on a large cliff/plateau feature.
*   **Central:** The Jungle Temple (B) is located in the dense center of the island, with the active Volcano (C) slightly to its south.
*   **West:** A small cove on the western coast hides the Coracle (G).
*   **Southwest:** The Deserted Village (D) sits on the coast.
*   **Southeast:** The Talonstones (E) are near the cliffs. 
*   **Separate Isle:** Off the southeastern coast, separated by a stretch of water, is the Island Bird Temple (F). Players must find a way to cross this water (likely the Coracle or swimming) to reach this final location.

**The Island Bird Temple (Cutaway Map):**
This is a cross-section showing a descending path. Imagine the entrance is at the top of a pyramid (1), leading down a set of stairs (2) into a central chamber (3). Inside the chamber is a statue. Behind the statue is a hidden passage leading to a long, twisting stone staircase (4) that tunnels deep underground. This leads to a water-filled cavern (5) with a waterfall. To the left of the waterfall (6) is a hidden nest area. Under the water, a tunnel connects the main cave (5) to the final Portal Chamber (7), which has a wall-mounted archway ready to receive the Godseyes.
