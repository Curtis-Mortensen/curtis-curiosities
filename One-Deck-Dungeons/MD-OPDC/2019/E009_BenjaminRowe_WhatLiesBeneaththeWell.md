**The Premise**
The fountain that supplies the water to this small town mysteriously stopped working about a month ago. The town wizard says it’s part of a long-term natural cycle but the mayor is skeptical. The surveyor hired by the mayor to investigate the ancient underground waterway has been down there for six days... far too long... With the promise of gold and saving the town, the players are hired by the mayor to get to the bottom of what’s happening down there and report back.

**Surveyor's Notes**
*   **Note #1 (Day 1):** This waterway is very old yet it seems recently disturbed. I see footprints and the signs of tampering. Doors are locked and I must find the keys... Will set up camp for the night and begin survey tomorrow...
*   **Note #2:** Don't think I am alone down here. Many locked doors. Water seems to source from behind this ornate door... locked but no keyhole... I believe anything lost in the water would wash downstream and through the shaft in this room...
*   **Note #3:** I cannot continue and must leave now. Leaving supplies behind in interest of time. If you are reading this please inform the mayor.
*   **Note #4:** Fire, I believe is the key that unlocks the door to where the water is sourced from... whatever is poisoning the water lies beyond that door... I wonder what causes the poison... perhaps now, I’ll never know....
*   **Note #5:** I agree to immediately stop my survey and to quietly return home without informing the mayor for the sum of 500 gold. -Signed Surveyor, co-signed: ‘Fat Cat’ Bergamot.

**Room/Mechanic Descriptions**
*   **General Hazard:** As long as the water remains poisoned, it does a small amount of poison damage upon contact and again after every 15 seconds of continued contact.
*   **The Plant Monster Room:** The water comes from above ground and falls into this man-made basin. In the center of the room is a large plant-like creature which attacks the players using poison and reaching tentacles/vines. This monster is afraid of fire and weak to fire attacks. If the players light all four torches around the room the monster is weakened as long as the fires remain lit. Once defeated, the water throughout becomes un-poisoned.
*   **The Secret Room:** Behind these vines is a room with a dias and beautiful frescos showing a waterfall and verdant life growing. And a key. A waving flame near the vines or lighting this torch will reveal the room. Vines can also be cut.
*   **The Pump Control Room:** The water is pulled up by an ancient technology that the players don’t need to understand to see that the mechanism has been plugged with the same fabric silks the wizard sells in his shop... Players can clear it easily. And the fountain above ground begins spouting water again. However, if the water is still poisoned (haven’t defeated the plant monster) the townsfolk who immediately begin drinking the water become poisoned.
*   **The Thug Hideout:** The thugs here are lethargic due to the poison and bored but are well fortified and have good supplies. They are using the vines for cover. Players can use fire to their advantage to destroy or repel the vines. The boss of the thugs, “Fat-cat Bergamot,” is sleeping behind a ramshackle desk and only wakes up when the players enter the room. On his desk is Surveyor’s note #5 and in the chest behind him is some loot, Bergamot’s bribe money and the key to the fountain control room. Players may decide what to do with Bergamot but he will insist on being bribed or paid-off or allowed to leave with his own earnings.
*   **The Old Storage Room:** In this old storage room, players find barrels and crates filled with low-grade food and/or consumables as well as (4) matches on top of Surveyor’s note #4.
*   **The Wizard's Stair:** This stair leads to a hatch in the floor behind the counter of the wizard’s shop. If confronted about the plugged up machine, the wizard will reveal that he hired the thugs to prevent people from unplugging the machine. The truth is: He knew the fountain water was poisoned but didn’t know where the poison was coming from, so in order to save the townsfolk from being slowly poisoned, chose to stop the machine until he could find a counter-poison.
*   **The Treasure Pit:** A few days ago, the mayor accidentally dropped the key to the fountain control room where it washed downstream and ended up here. The players may find other loot here including gold coins, monster parts or lost equipment. Players need to come in contact with the poison water or get clever to retrieve these things. If players light the torch on the wall the water temporarily drains and they can access the treasure without braving the poison water.

### Map Description for Navigation
The dungeon is an underground channel consisting of a series of connected stone chambers and water-filled tunnels.

1.  **Entrance:** Players enter from the top-center of the map through an entry point.
2.  **The Spine:** A central hallway runs downwards from the entrance. There is a "Surveyor’s Backpack" near the entrance containing a match and Note #1.
3.  **The Hidden Path:** To the left of the main corridor, behind some vines, is a secret room containing a dias, fresco, and a key.
4.  **The Storage Room:** Branching off to the right side of the main path, you find a small room with crates, barrels, and Note #4 with 4 matches.
5.  **The Thug Hub:** Continuing down the main path, you reach a larger chamber occupied by the thugs. There is a desk on the right side where the boss, "Fat-cat," sleeps. A path leads off from this room to the left, where players can catch the thugs by surprise by sliding down a chute.
6.  **The Pump Room:** Located at the bottom left, this room contains the ancient pump machinery (currently clogged with silk). A staircase leads upward to the wizard's shop.
7.  **The Treasure/Drain Room:** Located on the middle-right side of the map, this chamber contains a torch on the wall. Lighting it drains the poisoned water, allowing safe passage to the treasure.
8.  **The Boss Room:** At the very bottom center of the map is a large, circular chamber with a central basin. This is where the Plant Monster resides. There are four torches located around the room. A locked "ornate" door stands at the back of this room (which requires the key from the vine room or the treasure room).
