### 3. Map Description (for Gameplay)

The tower is a multi-story, cylindrical structure that has fallen into a cavernous, vertical sinkhole. Because the tower has collapsed, the floors are slanted, and the structural integrity is compromised.

**General Navigation:**
Players enter from the surface using a rope attached to an iron spike. Caution: There is a 2-in-6 chance the rope tears free while in use.

**Floor-by-Floor Layout:**
*   **Level 1 (Top):** The roof and upper floor are shattered. It is an open, circular room filled with rubble.
*   **Level 2:** A circular room containing web patterns. It houses one Giant Spider.
*   **Level 3:** Similar to Level 2, but with more extensive webbing. It houses two Giant Spiders.
*   **Level 4:** Split into two distinct areas: a prison cell (containing the Walking Corpse) and an adjacent wizard's laboratory.
*   **Level 5:** A living area featuring a bed or desk, currently occupied by an Exiled Morlock.
*   **Level 6:** A room with a door marked by a barricade. This is where the tower's treasure is stashed.
*   **Level 7:** An empty, circular floor. The wall is crumbling specifically where it makes contact with the jagged side of the sinkhole.
*   **Level 8 (Bottom):** A flooded room containing a single, prominent statue that seems to be "watching" the interior.
*   **Level 9 (Deep):** This is the very bottom of the sinkhole. A dark, jagged opening in the rock leads away from the tower and deep underground into a tunnel system. This serves as the spawn point for the wandering morlock patrols (2d6 morlocks, 2-in-6 chance every 3 turns).

**Spatial Relationship:** 
The central illustration shows the tower's tilt relative to the sinkhole's walls. When moving between floors, consider the incline; the tower is not standing upright, meaning the interior floors are slanted. Rooms on the side leaning against the sinkhole wall may have cracks or holes leading directly to the exterior, while the "upper" side of the tilted floors may be more structurally sound.
