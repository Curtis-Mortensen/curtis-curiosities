### Transcription of Text

**Sepulchre of the Abyss**
This religious site is on the abyssal plain at the bottom of the sea and is rumoured to store vast wealth and secrets. Especially devout cultists once went there to prepare themselves for the transition to their deity’s watery domain… through ritual self-mummification. Treasure hunters can enter this place by stepping through a stone portal likely found in a remote coastal cave or an abandoned beach cabin. Such a gate only functions on specific nights, such as under a New Moon.

**Environmental Description**
The magically carved walls are made of black marble. The only dim light source comes from glowing spongy algae that cover the floor (and produces fresh air). Everything is perpetually wet, cold, & smells like the sea. There is a constant sound of trickling water & ghostly chanting. The outside ocean is extremely cold & the pressure is lethal.

Once for each room, roll to determine its terrain, amount of flooding, & a random monster:

*   **Terrain (1d3):** 1. Flat, mosaic floor (failed save vs enchantment causes nausea/sickness); 2. Uneven and rocky (moderately difficulty terrain); 3. Jagged and broken (very difficult terrain).
*   **Flooding* (1d3):** *Changes every time a room is entered. 1. None (no effect); 2. Low (movement is noisy, +1 extra monster); 3. High (movement is sluggish, +2 extra monsters).
*   **Monster (1d6):** These strange, vicious creatures are translucent & dotted in bioluminescent blues & greens. They are all hungry. 1. Deep Ghoul (hides easily; always surprises); 2. Terror Eel (strong & fast; automatically grapples); 3. Blood Worm (lightning fast paralyzing bite); 4. Black Star (acidic & poisonous tentacle attack); 5. Vampire Squid (envelops & drains endurance); 6. Hell Shrimp (Vorpal pincer strike; armour piercing).

**Treasures**
Each room has a chance in 6 of containing one of these fabulous and weird items (e.g., T:1/6 means that there’s a 1 in 6 chance of finding a treasure):
1. **Amphora:** Restores all lost HP but has a permanent side effect (reverse with sorcery). 1. +7 or 2d6 years older; 2. -7 or 2d6 years younger; 3. Body part becomes scaly (player’s choice); 4. Body part becomes scaly (GM’s choice).
2. **Giant Clam:** Force open to find a weird jewel pendant (minor value). Wearer is initially perceived as friendly by all aquatic humanoids.
3. **Coral Mask:** While worn, you can breathe underwater; vision is poor.
4. **Strange Incense:** Inhaling the smoke counters poison & paralysis.
5. **Ancient Gold Idol:** Offering it at a shrine earns a deity’s good favour for 7 days & 7 nights. Highly valuable to temples & clerics.
6. **Sea Lotus petals:** Ingesting these removes a curse or a disease but causes vivid hallucinations.

**Rooms**
1. **Entry portal:** T:0/6. PCs appear on a circular dais facing an eerie statue of a four-faced humanoid goddess. Good beings feel intense cold & dread. Two small staircases descend on either side.
2. **Antechamber:** T:3/6. Two semi-circular stairs descend to a huge pile of bones (mix of human & fish).
3. **Library:** T:2/6. 100s of waterproof scrolls line the walls; a successful search finds one that answers 1d3 questions (1. Aquatic flora/fauna; 2. Sea gods/demigods; 3. Sunken ruins; 4. Ghost ships/curses; 5. Oceanic monsters; 6. Ports/harbours).
4. **Gallery:** T:1/6. Floor is at a disorienting angle: only slow movement is possible. Studying the mural gives a PC +1 to Wisdom but also a random insanity.
5. **Shrine:** T:4/6. On a coral altar is a bowl with the words: “blood & flesh for insight.” Filling it rewards a vision of a major secret. A grim banner on the wall causes magical fear.
6. **Meditation Chamber:** T:3/6. Five platforms surround a well overflowing with live coral. A mummy sits on one platform; described ominously, but it is harmless… or is it?
7. **Embalming Room:** T:4/6. Dozens of large, empty funerary Urns & a table made of petrified wood. Beneath are 3 desiccated “failed” mummies.
8. **Urn Chamber:** T:0/6. Huge statue of a goddess with eels for limbs; eyes seem to follow intruders. There are 35 sealed Urns; each contains (1. Mummy harmless; 2. Tome; 3. Random Treasure; 4. Mummy lethal; 5. Random Monster; 6. Acid Trap). *If an urn is broken, it sweats green slime. Once 15 are opened, the structure floods/shakes, and all rooms spawn new monsters.*

### Map Description (Accessibility Guide)

The dungeon map is presented as a branching, organic cluster of rooms, designed to be navigated in a "node-based" fashion. Each room is represented by a cluster of squares (1 square = 10 feet).

*   **The Layout:** The dungeon is arranged vertically.
*   **The Start:** **Room 1 (Entry)** is at the very top. It is a circular room with a statue in the center. From this room, two narrow, mirrored staircases lead downward into the rest of the complex.
*   **The Entrance:** The stairs converge into **Room 2 (Antechamber)**, which acts as the main hub.
*   **Branching Paths:**
    *   From Room 2, you can go left to **Room 7 (Embalming Room)**, which is a standalone chamber on the far left.
    *   From Room 2, you can go right into **Room 3 (Library)**.
*   **The Spine:** Moving downward from Room 3, you enter **Room 4 (Gallery)**, which is angled slightly.
*   **The Deep Core:**
    *   Continuing down from the Gallery leads to **Room 5 (Shrine)**, a small room on the right side.
    *   From the Shrine, you move left into the center-most room, **Room 6 (Meditation Chamber)**. This is a larger, central space with five platforms arranged around a circular well.
*   **The End:** Directly below the Meditation Chamber is the final room, **Room 8 (Urn Chamber)**. It is a large, central cavern containing the goddess statue with eel-limbs and the 35 urns.

**Navigation Summary:**
*   You descend from 1 to 2.
*   From 2, you have access to the "west wing" (Room 7) or the "east wing" (Room 3).
*   The east wing path (3 → 4 → 5 → 6) eventually leads to the center and the bottom of the map (Room 8).
