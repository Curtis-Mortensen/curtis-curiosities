### 2. Transcription of Text

#### Adventure Summary
An eccentric wizard named Galiron has summoned adventurers from across the globe to participate in a unique test of skills: braving his deadly gauntlet of traps, puzzles, and monsters. The group that performs most admirably in the gauntlet will be rewarded with riches and glory beyond their imagination! 
Each room in the gauntlet contains a single exit, which can only be reached by completing a challenge. These challenges vary from martial feats to mental quandaries. PCs can bring anything they wish inside the gauntlet with them, but Galiron gives no promise of safety during the competition.

#### Running This One Page Dungeon
Cut along the dotted lines! Put the larger slips (the challenges) in a hat, and the smaller slips (the rewards) in a shoe. Shake well!
The PCs have two hours - real time, not game time - to complete as many challenges as possible, drawn randomly from the hat. Each completed challenge earns the PCs a single point, as well as a reward drawn randomly from the shoe. PCs can compete against other groups at a convention, or can simply play for a good high score. Many of the challenges are open ended and allow for multiple solutions, so GMs should encourage PC creativity and reward ingenious problem-solving.

#### The Challenges
*   **The Taking Tree:** A tall room with walls painted to look like a brambly grove under moonlight. A treant named Falstaff sits on a dais. He wants objects of sentimental value to the PCs, not gold or magic. If given keepsakes, he reveals insights into the PCs' histories and gifts a blue marble (the exit).
*   **The Master of Games:** A room with a giant stone chess set. A mage named Ozlaf plays, but he has no idea how chess works and makes up rules on the fly. Players must either convince him he’s wrong or play along with his delusion to win.
*   **The Pillar of Water:** A room with alabaster tiles and a water pillar. The pillar is a playful water elemental. You can tell it jokes to get it to lift you to the ceiling exit, or block the floor drain to flood the room (which triggers a fight).
*   **The Bell Tower:** A tower with a giant bell. The room fills with screeching bats. Ringing the bell clears the bats for 30 seconds but collapses parts of the stairs. You must climb the rope or stairs to escape.
*   **The Elusive Exit:** A spherical room where gravity pulls to the outside surface. There is a 5-foot hole at the top. The hole is sentient and flees from the party. You must use silence and stealth to catch it.
*   **The Wood Carver:** A room full of wood shavings and an aged goblin in a hammock. The goblin shakes branches, causing wooden carvings to fall and animate as enemies. Defeating him/subduing him grants a wooden bird, which reveals the exit.
*   **The Library:** Shelves of books with single-word titles. Opening a book triggers the effect of the word (e.g., "Fire" shoots flames). You must use books to get through the door, as the "Key" book is missing.
*   **The Noble Duelist:** An empty room with a statue. A sign tells you to stand ten paces away and draw your weapon. The statue awakens and fights. You must duel it solo; if others interfere, you are grappled by spiked chains.

#### The Rewards
*   **Blade of Truth:** Forces enemies to tell the truth when struck.
*   **Faithful Aegis:** A hovering shield that intercepts missiles.
*   **Winding Rope:** A floating hemp rope that obeys verbal commands.
*   **Bugbane Torch:** Everburning torch; instantly kills insects.
*   **Thief’s Bracers:** Eases lock picking and allows carrying heavy loads.
*   **Wizard’s Gloves:** Manipulate small objects up to 20ft away.
*   **Mirrored Vest:** Reflects beams and gazes back at attackers.
*   **Book of Tongues:** Translates foreign languages.
*   **Ring of Inversion:** Inverses elements of spells (Fire to Ice, etc.).
*   **Boots of Sliding:** Makes the user frictionless on command.

### 3. Gameplay Guide (For Non-Visual Play)
If you are playing this module, it is important to understand that this is **not a traditional dungeon crawl** where you walk from room to room through corridors.

*   **The Structure:** This is a **Modular Challenge System.** The "rooms" are separate, independent scenarios. You do not map them physically.
*   **The "Hat" Mechanic:** Because the encounters are drawn from a hat, you can treat this as a series of distinct "mini-adventures." You resolve one scenario, gain your point and reward, and then pull the next "room" from the hat.
*   **Visualizing the Encounters:**
    *   **The Master of Games:** Imagine a chess board where the pieces float and the "DM" (Ozlaf) is confidently making up nonsensical rules.
    *   **The Pillar of Water:** Focus on the auditory cues—the "giggling" of the elemental.
    *   **The Elusive Exit:** This is a puzzle of movement and stealth. Imagine the room as a giant, smooth globe where you can walk on the walls/ceiling.
    *   **The Library:** This is a logic puzzle based on synonyms and associations (e.g., "Fire" vs. "Water," "Hope" vs. "Despair").
*   **The Goal:** You are effectively playing a "Gauntlet" mode. Keep track of how many challenges you survive and what magical gear you collect from the "shoe." The items listed in the "Rewards" section are meant to be used as tools to solve the future challenges you pull from the hat.
