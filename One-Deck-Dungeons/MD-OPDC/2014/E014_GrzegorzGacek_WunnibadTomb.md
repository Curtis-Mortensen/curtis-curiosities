**Wunnibad’s Tomb**
Simple, short adventure for Savage Worlds by Grzegorz Gacek

**Introduction**
Wunnibad is a hero of old, famous for saving the land from a terrible invasion (by undead army, greenskins horde - choose something appropriate to the setting). Some believe that he was deified and worship him as Protector, god of Defence. Entrance to Wunnibad’s Tomb has been recently dug out by a local count, who wants to use it to fill his coffers (by plunder or establishing a pilgrimage site). Unfortunately it is a dangerous place, two workers got killed in an explosion when they entered with a torch, one brave knight took a lamp and was doing well before he mysteriously fell over and something dragged him inside. The count forbade his men from entering and started to look for other ways to explore the tomb. Fortunately a group of adventurers showed up (or volunteered to get out of the count’s dungeon). The tomb is a cursed place, defiled by Wunnibad’s enemy (the unsuccessful invader). Interior is infested with deadly magical intelligent weed which tries to kill everyone who enters.

**Entrance**
Dark, long (60m) stone corridor leading into the hill. Above it is a crude inscription which says: “You are nothing but foul dogs to me, and as such, by the leash, you will come. Fail to obey me, and you will suffer.” The inscription is written in an uncommon language known by one of the PCs. If they don’t possess such skill (or can't read), one of the count’s men will provide translation, if characters ask for it.
Interior of the corridor is covered with strange plants, similar to moss or lichen. At the bottom of the corridor, the vegetation is thinner and stone tiles can be seen. There is a crudely carved line, which starts straight, but zig-zags after a few meters (this is the “leash”).
The corridor is dangerous. One species of moss reacts to open fire by releasing gas which explodes causing 2d6 damage. Another (present only on the ceiling) detects living beings and releases its pollen; PCs entering straightened have to pass a Spirit test at one-third of the corridor length, difficult (-2) at two-thirds, and very difficult (-4) at the end, or fall asleep. Characters not following the carved line will step on a plant which will try to grapple them and cause 2d6 electric shock damage each round. The inscription is the key to the safe passage; if characters crawl inside on all fours following the “leash”, they will be safe.

**Entry room**
Nothing deadly here. There are almost no plants on the floor but walls are almost completely covered in leaves. Vegetation is thickest on the ceiling, which cannot be seen but seems quite high (lowest leaves are 2m from the floor). The wall opposite the entry has a single wooden door (open) and three small windows.

**Statue room**
This chamber is higher than the previous one with the same vegetation. There are two stone feet on the floor, remains of a shattered statue of a warrior. On his shield, the symbol of a tower can be seen, the sign of Wunnibad. Between the broken feet stands the knight who was dragged into the tunnel. He shows no signs of life and seems to be hanging from entangled vines going down from the ceiling. A closer look reveals that they pierce the armor and go into the knight's body. As in the previous room, there are windows on the opposite wall, though in place of the leftmost is a hole. This time the door is closed with a grate. The mechanism to open it is present but doesn't work; PCs may lift the grate and block it or go through the window-hole.
This room holds an ambush. When the party is distracted (by the knight or the attempt to get to the next room), several humanoids hiding in bushes at the ceiling jump down and attack the PCs. They resemble zombies, though like the knight, each is “connected” by the vines to the vegetation. Cutting the vines connecting the monsters to the plant reduces their Fighting skill and Vigor by one die (affecting also Toughness and Parry). Depending on the party’s strength, the knight may also join the fight in a dramatic moment...

**Stairs room**
Vegetation in this chamber grows mostly on the walls. The floor is covered by grappling moss (same as in the entry corridor), and the ceiling is covered by tangled, woody, leafless vines (ideal for attaching a rope to swing across to avoid the moss). Several warrior statues guard the stairs leading to the next chamber; all have a tower carved on their shields. There used to be a door leading to the next chamber but it is destroyed.

**Burial chamber corridor**
Several thick vines hang down from the ceiling; closer examination shows that they are covered in small hooks and sticky slime. The vines are sensitive to contact and quickly wrap around and adhere to a touching object or person and try to raise its prey up. There, the victim is wrapped up in big leaves and digested (2d6 acid damage per round; use Grapple rules to get free). Non-edible objects are dropped. It takes 30 seconds for the vine to unwrap down again.

**Burial chamber**
The heart of the tomb with a sarcophagus completely covered with vegetation; inside is the root of all trouble. When the PCs approach, the lid will crack, thorned tendrils will flail, and the final battle will begin. Stats of the weed’s root should pose a challenge; its tendrils should have a range of 1 or 2, it should be Fearless, and maybe have the Sweep edge. If the party is experienced or really tough, some plant-zombies can descend from the ceiling. Make it memorable! Wunnibad may reward heroes with defense-boosting magical items (e.g., increasing Parry), in line with his aspect.

### Map Description
The map is a linear, top-down dungeon layout spanning a single vertical axis. Imagine moving from the top of the map to the bottom:

1.  **Entrance Corridor:** The top section is a long, narrow, vertical rectangle. A fine, zig-zagging line (the "leash") runs down the center of the corridor floor.
2.  **Entry Room:** The corridor opens into a square chamber.
3.  **Statue Room:** Below the Entry Room is another square chamber. In the center are two stone feet—all that remains of a statue. A figure (the knight) stands between them. The map shows rubble/debris in the lower-right quadrant of this room.
4.  **Stairs Room:** Below the Statue Room is a third square chamber. It is visually distinct because it contains "stairs," represented by horizontal lines crossing the room, and is flanked by symbols representing warrior statues.
5.  **Burial Chamber Corridor:** A narrow, vertical rectangle connects the Stairs Room to the final chamber.
6.  **Burial Chamber:** At the very bottom is the final, small square room. In its center is a large rectangle representing the sarcophagus.

The entire map is overlaid with a subtle grid, and each room is separated by clear wall boundaries, with only the specified doors or holes providing passage.
