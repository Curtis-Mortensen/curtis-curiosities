### Transcription of Written Text

**Title:** A Whale of a Time...
**Intro:** Questionable decisions* were made and now you find yourself in the rapidly closing maw of a giant mechanical sky-whale. As it plummets to the distant ground below, broken and on fire... Speaking of broken, the airship's also seen better days. Days that involved it's envelope being free of holes and properly attached to the rest of it.
* *Footnote:* Involving a local tavern, a friendly airship captain, a conversation on the value of mechanical sky-whale Ambergris and more rum than might be medically advisable.

**The Maw (Start Here):** Full of panicked sky ghost Krill that the mecha sky-whale has scooped up ready to process. Also, a group of player characters are here too, where they've been thrown from their recently swallowed airship...

**The Brain:** Over eager fire fighting water elementals will ensure the PC's are well doused. Opens the maw for two height segments and can trigger a gag reflex. No, it can't turn off the lightning. Does some other things. +/- 1 to the next encounter roll.

**Blow Hole:** Emergency ballast cannon, currently firing out regular bursts of water, could be a way past the lightning field as it'll shoot someone out pretty quick, and quite possibly also knock them unconscious in the process.

**Lightning Fin:** Power collector used to charge the bio matrix spline capacitors, now broken and acting as the source for a very friendly lightning field around the skywhale that's attracted to anything outside of the whale. If making friends with a few million volts of elemental energy doesn't sound fun, this may require some aggressive maintenance before departure.

**Griselda:** "They said I was mad to live in a sky-whale, mad I tell you, who's mad now, eh!?" Griselda is definitely mad as a box of spoons, but maybe she knows the inner secrets of the sky-whale. Good luck with that, you're far more likely to get a story about her cat first... +/- 2 to the next encounter roll.

**Emergency Brake:** This should be incredibly useful, however it's not. The burnt remains of these giant parachutes instead make for a room full of floating cloth scraps, smoke and fire. Maybe an intact spare is in a fireproof store. If only the signs weren't also on fire it would probably be easy to find.

**Ambergris "Store":** Sweet precious Ambergris. Once it's been dried and matured. A rather more pungent, organic, digestive system kind of an odour pervades the air currently. Like an outhouse in summer, after a festival. Breathing should be kept to a minimum.

**Getting Out!:** How to get out of this alive. Disabling the friendly lightning field might make life easier. Dislodging your vessel from the maw could be handy to open first. Some might say an airship vessel is useful, but at this stage that might be a luxury too far. Skywhale Ambergris would be spiffy too, as may well make the trip worthwhile.

**Beast Belly:** Ghost-krill are sucked in to giant vents and turned in to the fuel the mech whale uses, well normally they would, but there is the crashed wreck of an airship jammed in here instead. The wreck is being dismantled by a gang of smaller mecha robo antibodies. It looks familiar...

**Engine Halls:** Unnecessary amounts of dangerous moving parts: CHECK. Ghost krill tenderising auto hammers: CHECK. It'll get easier once you know the timing?

**Enemies:**
* **SWAB's (Sky Whale Anti-Bodies):** Anti whose body? The PC's who just crashed into their Skywhale for one. SWABs come in 3 sizes with lots of sharp, nasty tools.
* **Ghost Krill:** The size of a small bat and made of eerie green ectoplasm, these can choke if inhaled. Their screeching whale tests the soundest mind to beakers.
* **Gelatinous Tube:** Like a gelatinous cube, but a cylinder. One that also happens to produce a clear oily coating. Of course it's poisonous. And slippery. They also roll...

**Encounters (D10):**
1. Final Finfare - Huge SWAB + reroll and add any negative result.
2. This'll whaley hurt - Many medium/small SWABs.
3. You'll be here a whale - Encounter Griselda and lose a turn. See NPC above.
4. Narwhal get it - The Gelatinous Tubes are whaley pleased to see you.
5. There's no whale it'll hold - Collapsing structure causes extra problems.
6. That's a bit cruwhale - Ghost Krill swarm attacks!
7. I don't feel whale - Smoke and fires are bad for breathing, vision and hangovers.
8. Quit whalest you're after - Some small SWABs.
9. They've run out of fuwhale - No encounter, enjoy.
10. Ride the monowhale - A shortcut! Move one space.

**Height Tracker:** 1 (Falling), 2 (Still Falling), 3 (Falling Faster), 4 (Turbulence), 5 (Nose Down), 6 (Flipping), 7 (Spinning), 8 (Petunia Bowl), 9 (Round, Ground).

### Map Description for Players (Non-Visual)
The dungeon is represented as the internal layout of a massive, mechanical whale-shaped vessel in mid-air. 

*   **The Maw (Entrance):** You start at the far left of the map, inside the whale's open, mechanical jaws. Your wrecked airship is lodged here. 
*   **The Layout:** Moving from left to right, the ship is divided into functional "rooms" linked by corridors:
    *   **The Beast Belly:** A large central storage area where your ship wreck is located. It is filled with conveyor-like vents.
    *   **The Engine Halls:** Located centrally, a maze of moving pistons and heavy machinery.
    *   **The Brain:** Positioned near the upper front of the whale. It is a control room staffed by water elementals.
    *   **The Ambergris "Store":** A pungent, rear-located room filled with stored digestive fuel.
    *   **The Blow Hole:** A vertical shaft leading toward the top of the ship, acting as a pressurized exit cannon.
    *   **Lightning Fin:** Located along the dorsal ridge (the top of the whale), surrounded by a dangerous electrical field.
    *   **Emergency Brake:** A room located near the rear, currently a burning, obstructed hazard.
    *   **Griselda’s Location:** Tucked away in a small, isolated compartment, acting as an unpredictable NPC encounter.
*   **The Height Tracker:** On the right side of your play area, keep a counter. You start at stage 1 ("Falling") and move toward stage 9 ("Round, Ground"). Every time the party changes rooms or triggers specific events, you must move up the chart; the higher the number, the more chaotic and physically difficult the environment becomes as the whale continues its descent to the ground.
