### Transcription of Text

**THE DEATHLESS CYST**
*By Luca Coppola, Simone Di Francesco and Lorenzo Dionisi*

The Deathless Cyst is the greatest achievement of Parnassus, the Biomancer. It is an endless soul receptacle, that allowed him to become immortal (at a price). The Cyst often captures and respawns the souls of many unlucky ones, forcing them to be reborn in it when they die. It is meant to be used as a "you wake up in this goo" event after a TPK, but remember: nothing truly dies here. Any time something dies, its soul is captured back into the Cyst, which spawns a new body for it (with a 1-in-6 possibility of a random mutation; this mutations stack and every time one is rolled more than once reduce a random Ability Score by 1; when ability scores reach 0 players degenerate in a twisted shadow of their former selves). They are then reborn 1d3x10 minutes afterwards, naked. Many souls were captured over the years, which resulted in... peculiar degenerate creatures roaming this goey place. Can you escape the Deathless Cyst of Doctor Parnassus and its terrible marvels, or will you become one of the deformed monstrosities that stalk its bowels?

**CREATURES:**
*   **Unicorns:** flaccid, obese and pony-sized, the Unicorns of the Cyst are what remains of much nobler creatures trapped by it. They feed off nutrients in the ground, biting off small pieces of flesh that regenerate almost immediately. When hit, they release small toxic clouds, very dangerous to predators around. Their horns comes easily off their corpses, and can be opened like a scroll. They contain 1d4 spell levels worth of a single random spell.
*   **Handmaidens:** giant bees with human hands for feet. Big as a human torso. They secern a sprinkly blue dust from their mouths, that lures their prey. Mostly spend their time grasping the ceiling and exchanging nutrients with the Cyst. Their barbed stingers ignore armor when they drop from above to kill their prey, which they eat and feed the Cyst with.
*   **Silent Watchers:** eyes with buglike legs, they scuttle all around the Cyst and give off light as a torch. They are as big as a Lantern, and don't need to be fueled. Can walk on the ceiling and walls. Feed by licking where they walk. Docile, can be killed by just squeezing too hard. When killed, they contain 1d4-1 small gems, which explode when dropped, stunning all those within 10 feet. In any room, there are d12-6 of them around.

**MUTATIONS:**
*   **Crabby Hands:** they work like a Heavy Weapon. You can't manipulate anything precisely anymore.
*   **Skinless Scalp:** you can sense anything moving 10 feet around you. You take double elemental damage.
*   **Acidic Soul:** you can vomit your own soul, which you control and acts as a green slime. You die if you're not back to your body in 1 hour.
*   **Iron Hair:** your body is covered in thick hair, that acts as Leather Armor. It instantly grows back if burned or cut. You can't wear any armor at all, or any clothes for that matter.
*   **Electrical Heart:** your heart produces and stores electricity. You can use it to attack at will by just touching any enemy (damage like a standard weapon), but you receive half the damage you inflict.
*   **Octofeet:** your legs now fork off into 8 long, thin and extremely movable finger-like appendages. They work like Thieve's Tool like a charme, and allow climbing at 1/2 your normal speed. However they reduce your land speed by half and make running impossible.
*   **Healthy Eater:** your ribcage becomes a giant mouth, filled with sharp teeth. You can swallow whole creatures smaller than you that you grapple, and be fed for a week. You can't eat normal food anymore.

### Map Description for Gameplay
The map is a singular, continuous, organic structure. For a blind or text-based player, think of the dungeon as a **living, internal organ system** rather than a standard grid-based stone dungeon. The connections between rooms are "sphincters" or "mouths" that open and close based on proximity.

*   **The Biomancer’s Lair (Top-Left):** Access is only possible without Cyst Juice. It is entered via a giant uterus-like structure hanging from the sky (ceiling).
*   **The Spawning Pool (Top-Center):** Where players respawn. Contains "Birthing Pods" (housing souls).
*   **The Hekatian Bonecave (Top-Right):** A torture chamber featuring bone tumors that hang from spikes on the walls. If you are thrown into the walls, there is a chance of taking damage or becoming stuck.
*   **The Descent (Mid-Left):** A long, muscular connective tube. It connects rooms and is a primary hunting ground for monsters. It is muscular enough that it shifts/trembles every 30 minutes.
*   **The Arena (Center):** A large, wide, central chamber. It is deaf and reacts to light (not sound). It is completely immune to conventional damage.
*   **The Flesh Bible (Mid-Right):** A library of living meat, where books are grafted into the anatomy of the room. It contains a map of the Cyst.
*   **The Nest (Bottom-Left):** Filled with acidic "Nestlings." It is dangerous and highly volatile; the Nestlings explode if they sense anyone nearby.
*   **The Leathery (Bottom-Center):** A pit filled with skeletons and hanging skin strings. It is a hazardous zone where players must save or be pulled in.
*   **Parnassus, Bane of the Unicorns (Bottom-Right):** The location of the creator. It is a stomach-like area where Parnassus is digested by the Cyst’s own juices while he waits for his next victim.

**Navigation Note:** Movement between these areas should be described as moving through fleshy tubes, past muscular valves, and into cavernous, organic pockets. Because the dungeon is "alive," you can describe the floor pulsing or the walls tightening as the players move between specific chambers.
