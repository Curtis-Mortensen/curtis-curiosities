**Title:** The Faerie Market
**By:** Leslie Furlong

**Introduction:**
Your search for young Kath, the Minister's gifted daughter, has led you to this nowhere place between places, the moonless fair that beckons the creeps, the shadows, the forgotten. Shielded from their scrutiny by a powerful illusion, you move among them, hoping to soon find some sign of the girl, because in a few hours the market disappears with the dawn, and with it go all of it’s secrets.

**[Location Boxes]**

*   **The Spirit Talker:** The Gray Lady, Old Missus Inbetween, Ghostcaller... Not every weirdling and spirit makes it to the market. If you wish to contact one of those beings, or maybe your dear old Gran, the Spirit Talker can make it happen, as always, for a price.
*   **The Main Gate:** Entering the fair is a straightforward process, just show your invitation to the guards and walk right in. I mean, you do have an invitation, don't you?
*   **Bullington and Beefe, Cartographers:** These narrow-faced gents seem to know where you are going even when you don't. The route is never an easy one, but what worth doing is? Good people to speak to if you're a little girl lost, trying to find her Gran.
*   **The Fortune Seller:** For the right price, the fortune seller will give you a glimpse of the future. It is always a troubling one, perilous and painful. Best not to ask about yourself, then.
*   **The Marketplace:** The oldest part of this fair is this warren of blankets and carts at its heart. Here traders of all manner of goods, tangible and otherwise, conduct their business, and while coins from the daylight kingdoms are accepted, it is barter that is truly valued. Tell me, what would you trade to speak to your beloved Gran?
*   **Sneere's Auctioneers:** The keen-eyed Mortimer Sneere and his company of appraisers know the value of everything, from a dusty chalice to a musty book to a cherished memory. Bargain hunters might find something among sellers in the Marketplace, but those with a desire for the unique will show up at the bottom of every hour to see what treasure Sneere has unearthed.
*   **The Stage:** Some say that the voices of performers carry on beyond the market into the Never-Never, so it is important to be in top form, lest those listening be displeased. Of course, some say the opposite... Hopefuls must first see the Emcee, Macklin Drax, before taking to the stage.
*   **The Hole:** Outside the Main Gate, scruffy youngsters mill about, watching, looking for those without an invitation. “Want in, don’ya?” they say, with a wink. For a price the urchins will show you a way in. “Just stay clear of the guardfolk,” they tell you. “No skin off my nose, but they find you without an invite an’ yer done.”
*   **Academy Recruiters:** The doll-like young ladies with onyx eyes sit patiently, hands folded upon their laps. "Would you like to join us at our school?" they ask passersby. Occasionally they are more insistent, especially when taken by a young, clever thing. “Have you seen her?” They might ask. “The Matron would be most welcoming.”
*   **The Square Circle:** Do you have a grievance to settle? A point you wish to make? Those with martial skill (or at least access to it) are encouraged to make use of this arena. The contest need not be lethal, but the outcome is binding. One-Eyed Fred at the Den covers most wagers.
*   **The Den:** The lucky and the desperate go to the den, yearning for fortune’s caress. From a quick hand of Devil’s Lash to a round of Daggerdeep, One-Eyed Fred provides tables for all games of chance. When caught, cheaters are given one last wager. Win, no harm done, play on. Lose, and well... lose.

### Description of the Map for Play
The map is an "event map"—it is not a grid for tactical movement, but a conceptual layout of the market's layout that you can use to describe the scene to your players. Imagine yourself standing in the center of a circular clearing under a moonless sky.

*   **The Center:** The **Marketplace** is the heart of the fair. It is a dense, chaotic mess of blankets, carts, and stalls packed with strange, glowing items and odd traders.
*   **The Perimeter:** As you stand in the center, you can look out toward the edge of the market in different directions:
    *   **Directly Ahead (North):** You see the **Main Gate**, the primary entrance where guards stand watch, filtering the arrivals.
    *   **Slightly to the left (North-West):** You find the **Spirit Talker**, sitting in a quiet, dark spot, surrounded by swirling ghostly vapors.
    *   **Far Left (West):** You see the **Fortune Seller**, sitting in a small, ominous booth with a crystal ball that seems to swallow the light.
    *   **Bottom Left (South-West):** This is the **Stage**, a wooden platform where a performer is currently holding the attention of a shadowy crowd.
    *   **The very edge (South-West exterior):** The **Hole** is located just outside the gate, where the street urchins wait in the shadows of the outer wall.
    *   **Bottom Center (South):** This is **The Den**, a rowdy, dimly lit gambling area. Nearby, you see the **Academy Recruiters**—a group of girls with terrifyingly still, doll-like expressions, watching the crowd.
    *   **Bottom Right (South-East):** This is the **Square Circle**, a rope-bounded arena where people are currently engaged in a heated, non-lethal brawl.
    *   **Far Right (East):** You find **Sneere’s Auctioneers**, a collection of tables displaying bizarre, valuable artifacts being appraised by cold-eyed men.
    *   **Slightly to the right (North-East):** You see the tent of **Bullington and Beefe, Cartographers**, who have maps laid out that seem to shift and change as you look at them.

**Navigating the area:** The market is a "warren," meaning the paths aren't straight. It feels cramped and claustrophobic. If you are describing this to someone, emphasize that the edges of the map are constantly shifting, and the further you walk from the center, the more the market seems to dissolve into the dark, formless "Never-Never."
