**THE BURNING LAIR**
*A one page Dungeon for 6-8 characters, levels 9+*

**The Story so far...** The great dragon, Astrellax, wages a terrible assault on the surrounding lands. His foul magics have twisted and corrupted many creatures and he has bound them to his service. He demands tribute and those cities that fail to pay he ravages or sends his minions to destroy. The King has called for a group of heroes to stop the dragon, offering lands, wealth, and titles to any who can.

The volcano is difficult to reach overland. There is little normal wildlife in this broken land, but monsters and undead rove nearby in packs. Even the elements themselves have been broken to Astrellax’s will; air and earth elementals patrol the upper reaches of his lair, denying easy access to climbers or flyers. The characters must find a way into his lair using one of the various caves they can see upon approach.

**New Monsters:**
*   **Blooded:** Creatures warped by drinking Astrellax’s potent blood. They are stronger than normal creatures of their type, receive +1 to saves versus fire, and only take half damage from magical flame (none from mundane fires).
*   **Blooded Kobolds:** Red-skinned Kobolds, larger than normal. Fight as Gnolls.
*   **Blooded Trolls:** Red-skinned horrors as tough as hill giants. They use normal claws and bite (+ giant strength) and are nearly immune to fire!

**Wandering Monsters (Roll every 2 Turns, 1 on a d6):**
1. 2d6 Blooded Kobolds
2. 1d6 Blooded Kobolds, plus 1d6 Hellhound ‘pets’
3. 1d3 Blooded Trolls
4. Steam Vent-save or take 5d6 damage, d4 PCs.

*Note: All cave tunnels slope upwards, allowing the PCs to move up the map. Distances should be to the GM’s taste.*

**Cave A**
*   **A1:** The entrance is littered with gnawed bones.
*   **A2:** This large cave has d3 Blooded Trolls eating a captured adventurer. The adventurer carries some gems and a magic weapon.
*   **A3 (Nursery):** d6+1 young Blooded Trolls (stats as normal trolls + fire resistance).
*   **A4 (Sleep chamber):** Filthy chamber with beds of decaying vegetation. No trolls currently; a dead adventurer lies here with 3 potions and a magic ring.
*   **A5:** The Blooded Troll Chieftess lives here with four male concubines. She fights as a frost giant and casts spells as an 8th level wizard. The concubines are normal. She has 5 pieces of jewelry, a wand, and two miscellaneous magic items.

**Cave B**
*   **B1:** This tunnel has no tracks indicating entry.
*   **B2:** Valuable gems glitter in the walls. If pried free, d6 earth elementals attack.
*   **B3:** This large chamber is very warm. 25% chance of Steam Vent eruption (see wandering monsters) every turn spent here.
*   **B4:** Fiery vents and 5 salamanders. Treasure normal for their type.
*   **B5:** Large pool of black, viscous fluid. It is a double-strength Black Pudding that attacks anything that approaches.
*   **B6:** Magical pool chamber. A pool of warm water; if drunk, it has a random effect on the drinker (GM’s choice, half good/half bad). Effects are permanent unless powerful magic is used.

**Cave C**
*   **C1:** Many tracks going in and out.
*   **C2 (Guard room):** 10 Blooded Kobolds on lookout.
*   **C3 (Guard room 2):** 10 more Blooded Kobolds who assist C2 if they hear fighting. Each carries 2 flasks of oil and torches to throw.
*   **C4 (Supply room):** Stolen goods and supplies. Contains a magic spear and a cursed spear.
*   **C5 (Prisoners):** Cages with 3d6 prisoners (normal people, merchants, or potential PC replacements). If PCs, their gear may be in C4. 6 Blooded Kobolds and 3 hellhounds guard this room.

**Cave D**
*   **D1:** Many tracks going in and out.
*   **D2 (Guard room):** 10 Blooded Kobolds.
*   **D3 (Kennel):** 8 hell hounds and 6 Blooded Kobolds. The hound master is double strength and wears a ring that controls animals.
*   **D4 (Living chamber):** 25 Blooded Kobolds and 3 Blooded Trolls. One double-strength sub-chief is here with a magic sword, magic armor, and jewelry.
*   **D5:** Supply room (food, weapons, stolen goods).
*   **D6 (Witch lair):** The dragon shaman lives here. A triple-strength Blooded Kobold who casts spells as a 6th level wizard. A bound fire elemental is in a brazier. He has scrolls, d6 potions, and 3 valuable gems.
*   **D7 (Chief’s Lair):** The Blooded Kobold Chief has wings, flies, has a 6d6 breath weapon, and is as tough as a hill giant. He wears a magic ring, cloak, and carries a powerful magic spear. There are 15 Blooded Kobolds and 3 sub-chiefs. Treasure is in a locked chest with a poison cloud trap (save or die within 10’). If losing, they try to run to the shaft to warn Astrellax (3 rounds).

**The Shaft**
Several hundred feet deep. A fall is lethal without magic. Climbing is time-consuming but possible.

**Astrellax**
Huge, ancient Dragon. If warned, he creates an illusion of himself on his hoard while flying invisibly overhead, using ventriloquism to goad PCs into wasting magical resources. His trove is as vast as the GM desires. If losing, he attempts to escape to brood on revenge.

### Map Description for Navigation
The map is a top-down view of a complex, sprawling cave system, oriented so that multiple cave mouths at the bottom and sides lead deeper into the mountain, converging toward a central point.

*   **Connectivity:** The map is divided into four distinct zones (A, B, C, D) which are not directly connected to each other, but all lead towards the center of the map where a vertical "Shaft" is located.
*   **Cave A (Top Left):** A sprawling, branching network of five small, interconnected chambers. It starts at the surface (A1) and winds deep into the mountain, ending at the Chieftess's chamber (A5).
*   **Cave B (Bottom Left):** A mostly linear series of six rooms (B1–B6) that wrap along the left and bottom perimeter of the dungeon.
*   **Cave C (Bottom Right):** A cluster of five rooms (C1–C5) located in the bottom-right corner. It is a dense, interconnected area of guard rooms and holding pens.
*   **Cave D (Right side):** A linear path of seven chambers (D1–D7) that runs along the right side of the map. It is the most heavily fortified, leading directly into the central shaft (D7 is immediately adjacent to the Shaft).
*   **The Shaft:** Located near the center-top of the map, this is a large, circular void space. It acts as the "boss room" and the primary vertical access point for the dragon, Astrellax. You can visualize this as a chimney cutting through the center of the dungeon; the outer caves represent the various entry points used to reach this vertical chasm.
