**[Header]**
**Karsthaven: The Hidden Temple of Orena**

**[Introduction]**
Known from legends, but long lost, the Hidden Temple of Orena was of ancient times carved from the limestone deep in the heart of a small dome-pit high in the Snowy Mountains. Access to the Temple was through a narrow cave passage until a small earthquake filled the entrance with breakdown rocks. Recently rediscovered, the cave entrance passage has finally been cleared of the breakdown and opened, but none have dared venture in, yet.

### Map Description for Play
The dungeon is organized around a central, circular nexus called the **Dome-Pit**. Imagine this as the hub of a clock. You enter from the "Entrance Passage" at the North-West, which spills you out onto a ledge roughly halfway up the wall of this 300-foot vertical shaft. 

From this central point, various rooms radiate outward like the numbers on a clock face:

*   **The North Section:** Directly accessible from the ledge at the top is the **Priestess Quarters** (North-West). Moving clockwise along the top edge, you find the **Bath**, and then the **Acolytes’ Chamber** (North-East).
*   **The Center Section:** Moving further clockwise towards the eastern side of the complex, you find the **Refectory** and the **Common Room**.
*   **The South Section:** At the bottom-right (South-East) lies the **Cave of Orange Crystal** and the entrance to the **Waterfall Cave**. The Waterfall Cave leads further south into the **Pooka’s Grotto**.
*   **The West Section:** Returning to the bottom-left (South-West), you have the **Crypt Cave** and the **Storage Cave**.
*   **The Interior:** Situated in the center of the complex, wedged between the Library (West) and the Refectory (East), is the **Chapel**.

The map implies that movement between these rooms involves navigating the perimeter of the Dome-Pit. The "Dome-Pit" itself is a massive void. Players are essentially on a vertical cliff face with cave entrances dotted along the stone walls at different heights, connected by rotted, dangerous wooden walkways and stairs.
