**Title:** The Heist
*An adventure for LVL 1 characters. Try to steal the “Golden Egg”, a 10,000 gold coins masterpiece.*

**General Information/Fluff:**
*   **The fortress:** Far away from the civilized world, the unique goal of this small fortress is to hoard the shiny things that the crowmen find outside. Maybe they are a cult to the golden god. Anyway your only desire is to loot such a profitable place.
*   **Dungeon facts:** Floor - sand. Walls - stone bricks. Ceiling - 2.5 meters tall. Stone bricks. Illumination - only the guard rooms and dormitory. Crowmen always carry a torch.
*   **The golden egg:** The objective is to steal this thing. It’s worth 10,000 gold coins and can be opened: inside you’ll find three magic rings and one amulet. The GM decides the special powers of these items.

**Room & Trap Descriptions:**
*   **Start Here!:** Obvious main entrance.
*   **Or Here!:** Less obvious backdoor.
*   **Tripwire alarm:** (Located near the backdoor).
*   **4 meters open pit:** (Found in the initial hallway).
*   **Pit trap:** (Found in the initial hallway).
*   **Living statues:** If the players step in the center square, they will attack. LVL 2 monster stats.
*   **A dog is locked here:** Very hungry. LVL 2 monster stats.
*   **Poison darts trap:** (Near the main entrance).
*   **Guard room:** 4 crowmen here. Communicates with 2nd guard room through a small hole in the wall. Crowmen can alert between rooms.
*   **2nd Guard Room:** 6 Crowmen here.
*   **Crowmen dorm:** 1d6 crowmen here. There are weapons, food, and some tools.
*   **Fake wall:** (Secret door).
*   **Three doors:** Left door reads “Dexterity”. Center door reads “Strength”. Right door reads “Mind”. Left door is decorated with demons, right door with snakes. Stone inscriptions are intelligible. Between the doors there is a sign with this text: “One of these doors has a trap. Choose wisely and quick, this text introduced an enchantment into your head and will kill you in 10 minutes if you don’t decide.” Neither has a trap, but the enchantment is real.
*   **“The nest” room:** Here lives the man-crow. It’s an horrendous giant bird with an human head. He eats people, and happens to like birdseeds too. LVL 3 stats.
*   **10 meters pit:** Very sliding walls.
*   **2 meters of water:** With aggressive Piranhas inside.
*   **Treasure room:** There are two chests with nice goodies at discretion by the GM. The golden egg lies on the altar. If touched, the water level will increase until flood the entire dungeon in 30 minutes.

### Map Description for Navigation
This dungeon is presented in an isometric view, meaning the layout is three-dimensional and viewed from an angle. To visualize or play this map, consider it as a series of connected stone chambers and corridors built into a hill:

1.  **The Entrances:**
    *   **The Main Entrance (Northwest):** This leads directly into a hallway filled with hazards. You enter near a "Poison darts trap." Moving forward, you pass a square containing "Living statues" and a "Pit trap."
    *   **The Backdoor (South):** This is a small, shadowed tunnel opening that leads to a "Tripwire alarm" before connecting to the central guard areas.

2.  **The Central Complex:**
    *   The heart of the dungeon is a network of small, connected rooms. There is a **Guard Room** (4 crowmen) which has a small hole in the wall allowing it to communicate with the **2nd Guard Room** (6 crowmen). A **Crowmen Dorm** is located nearby, containing supplies.

3.  **The Northern Sector ("The Nest"):**
    *   Located at the top-most part of the map is "The Nest." It is a circular, open-air room containing a large, woven bird nest. This is the lair of the "man-crow."

4.  **The Riddle Doors:**
    *   Central to the hallway layout are three doors side-by-side. The left door (Dexterity) has demon carvings, the center is marked Strength, and the right (Mind) has snake carvings. Between them is the sign warning of a 10-minute death enchantment.

5.  **The Final Chamber (Southeast):**
    *   Further into the dungeon, past a "10 meters pit" with sliding walls, lies the Treasure Room. 
    *   This is a sunken, circular chamber filled with "2 meters of water" occupied by Piranhas. 
    *   An altar sits in the center of the water, holding the "Golden Egg." The architecture shows the egg is surrounded by the water, which will rise and flood the dungeon once the egg is touched. There are two treasure chests sitting on the dry floor perimeter of this room.
