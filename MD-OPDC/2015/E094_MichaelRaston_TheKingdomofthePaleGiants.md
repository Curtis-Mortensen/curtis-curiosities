The Kingdom of the Pale Giants, Michael Raston.
The city's sewage system always collects and funnels towards a great underground river. When the moulding stone and rotting iron of the sewers fade away, the river is housed within a looming whitestone cavern. Within these caverns the Pale giants live. Here they have hewn the whitestone to construct a vast fortress, embedded in the earth. In the darkness, the polluted river warping and maddening them, the abhorrence of their civilization grows. Rumors of naked, pale giants stalking the sewers, missing women throughout the city and panicked pygmies appearing from nowhere and gibbering of an evil kingdom beneath the ground may spark adventurer's interest in venturing here.

### Map Description for Gameplay
The map depicts an underground fortress centered around a large, polluted river that flows from the West to the East. 

*   **The River:** This is the primary feature of the dungeon. It has two "scum-covered bays" on the South side, which contain piles of dead adventurers and treasures. The North shore is rocky, with "whitestone cliffs" and small, makeshift bone boats.
*   **The Northern Fortress:** On the North side of the river, the Pale Giants have carved a massive structure into the stone.
    *   **The Entrance:** Access is gained through the river or the "Jagged Rock Shore" to the West. 
    *   **The Interior:** A series of interconnected stone chambers. The **"Barrel Vaulted Hall"** serves as a central hub with sleeping areas for Kingsmen and storage. There is a specific room labeled as the **"King's Throne Room,"** containing the King, his throne, and scattered loot.
    *   **Features:** The area is dotted with pillars, bonfire rooms, and small windows looking out toward the river.
*   **The Southern/Eastern Area:** The South and East sections of the dungeon are more "ruined" and "crumbling."
    *   **Living Quarters:** The South contains slave pens and communal living areas for the pygmies.
    *   **Infrastructure:** A network of large sewage pipes feeds into the dungeon here. There is also a "Secret Passage" leading from the far bottom-right, marked by ancient murals of a better, forgotten era, which the pygmy shamans use for secret meetings.
*   **Navigation:** The dungeon is interconnected via numerous heavy, 14-foot-tall stone doors (labeled D1–D6) and several deadly traps (T1–T4) that include hidden scythes, pit traps with spikes, and explosive grates.
*   **The Exits:** There is an "Upriver" exit toward the city sewers, a "Downriver" exit, and specific tunnels leading to the "Fetid Caverns" and "Horned Rat Dog Warrens."
