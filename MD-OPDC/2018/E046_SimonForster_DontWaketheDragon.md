### 3. Map Description for Gameplay
The map depicts a winding cave complex carved into a mountain. The entrance is located at the bottom center.

*   **The Main Path:** Upon entering the cave, there is a wide, main artery that leads generally northward toward the Dragon’s Lair.
*   **The Southern Section (Area 1 & 2):** Immediately upon entering, you encounter "Area 1," a large cavern filled with crunching, waist-deep piles of bones. To the east of this, the path leads to "Area 2," a grotto containing a shallow stream of musky, smelly water that is prone to splashing.
*   **The Western Branch (Area 3 & Mushroom Farm):** From the entrance area, you can head west into a large, circular chamber which acts as a "Mushroom Farm." To the northwest of this is "Area 3," the leper colony, which is heavily fortified with spiked barricades.
*   **The Eastern Branch (Area 4 & 5):** If you head east from the central bone cavern, you find "Area 4," the den of the mountain wolves. Continuing north through this passage leads you to "Area 5," a dangerous chasm that requires a difficult crossing and is prone to falling rocks.
*   **The Dragon's Lair (Area 6):** Located at the very top of the map, this is the destination. It is a large, egg-shaped chamber where the dragon rests on her hoard. Access to the lair is gained by heading north through either the mushroom farm passage (left) or the chasm/wolf den passage (right).

**Game Mechanic Summary:**
The "Pokes" list functions as a "noise meter." Every time the players navigate through one of the numbered locations, they must use stealth or cleverness to avoid triggering the "Poke" associated with that location. If they trigger 5 "Pokes" total, the dragon wakes up and the combat begins. The map is designed so that players have multiple paths to the dragon, allowing them to choose which "Pokes" (noise risks) they are willing to attempt based on their party's strengths (e.g., a Rogue might easily bypass the bones, while a heavily armored Fighter might struggle).
