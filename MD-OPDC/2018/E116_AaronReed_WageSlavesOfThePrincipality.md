### Text Transcription

**Header:**
Far out to sea, a huge, blindfolded turtle swims forlornly, a village on its back. The five residents wave cheerily. But be wary, sailors, lest ye end up... **Wage Slaves of the Principality**

**The Laws (The Scroll):**
* Provisions in the form of Fish are to be caught by Labour.
* Fish may be sold to a licensed Tender at a rate of ½ day’s catch for 1 day’s nourishing rain-water.
* All barrels, buckets, and other devices for catching of rainwater may only be owned and operated by said Tender, for the safety and comfort of citizens.
* The Tender may rent properties to Labour for a fee of ½ day’s catch per rental day.
* The Mayor shall collect a daily tax of ½ day’s catch in exchange for services rendered to the Principality (passing & enforcing of laws, appeasement & administration of the Turtle, &c., &c.)
* Given Labour at present overspends their means, the Mayor generously offers a reasonable rate of compounded interest on their increasing debts.

**Daily Fees:** ½ + ½ + ½ = 1½ day’s work.

**Numbered Points:**
1. **The Dungeon.** Cage chained half-water line at lip of turtleshell.
2. **Shoddy row-houses** where Labour (3 souls in rags) live and fish. Worried sick about mounting debt. Crabs nibble their toes. They have forgotten their names. Would re-elect the Mayor.
3. **The Lender** (a miserly, officious man) collects rent and dispenses rain. His roof is lined in sloshing buckets.
4. **The Turtle**, confused & restless, won’t dive while blindfolded. Despite contrary claims, no one has any control over it.
5. **The Mayor’s ridiculous mansion**, stuffed with reeking barrels of rotting fish he treats like treasure. A right bastard, grandiose & entitled. Stole a Wand of Compulsion & Stick of Punishment to claim power. Uses both constantly. Will sell out his citizens in a heartbeat to save his skin.

**Setups:**
* A prisoner must be rescued
* Saved from a shipwreck (for a price)
* The Turtle knows an important clue
* The paladin takes an interest in the proletariat
* Rumors of hidden treasures were inaccurate

**Outcomes:**
* Revolution! (a new ruler will surely do better)
* Conquest! (floating home base)
* Wealth Redistribution! (everyone gets a wand)
* The Turtle Has Had Enough

### Map Description for Play (Accessibility)

To visualize this map for gameplay, imagine a circular, organic terrain that is constantly in motion.

*   **The Environment:** The entire "dungeon" is the back of a colossal sea turtle. The turtle is blindfolded with a thick, heavy cloth wrapped around its head (Position 4). It is constantly swimming; the water around the shell is choppy and dark. 
*   **The Layout:**
    *   **The Bottom (The Docks/Dungeon):** At the very edge of the shell, where it meets the water line, is **The Dungeon (1)**. It is a hanging cage, constantly battered by waves and spray.
    *   **The Residential Zone:** Just above the water line, moving toward the center of the shell, are the **Shoddy Row-houses (2)**. These are cramped, decaying buildings where the population lives in poverty.
    *   **The Administrative Zone:** Towards the center, near the highest point of the shell, sits **The Lender’s House (3)**. It is a more rigid, distinct building, easily identified by the many buckets and rain-catching apparatuses covering its roof. 
    *   **The Seat of Power:** At the highest, most stable peak of the shell is **The Mayor’s Mansion (5)**. It is a grandiose, slightly ostentatious structure that dominates the skyline of the village. It looks out-of-place compared to the crumbling shacks of the laborers.
    *   **The Navigation:** The "Front" of the dungeon is the turtle's head and neck, which are stretched out in front of the shell. The blindfold makes the turtle appear distressed and directionless.

**Spatial Note for the DM:** This is a "vertical" dungeon. The players are at the bottom (dungeon level) and must move upward through the residential area toward the Mayor’s mansion to reach the "boss" or center of political power. The environment is unstable due to the swimming motion of the turtle.
