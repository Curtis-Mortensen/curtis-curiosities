**Overview**
A long-abandoned hunting castle known to den a group of Kobolds now festers with unknown creatures. Locals are concerned by the Feral Rats that come from the dilapidated lodge, but as players investigate they find themselves in the trap of the Tunneling Black Maw.

**Recurring Mechanics**
*   **F) Ferality Traps:** Small gas-filled sacks which cause weak-willed creatures to attack until exhaustion.
*   **S) Spike Traps:** Tripwire and pressure plates that launch spines or needles; intended for vermin but still harmful.
*   **P) Paralysis Traps:** Actually Black Maw larvae encased in a toxin which numbs and immobilizes on contact.
*   **Collapsing Terrain:** Almost all flooring is rotted or crumbling; all heavy actions have a chance of causing players to collapse into the level below, battering and possibly burying them.

**Detail of Map Sections**
*   **A) Entrance Hall:** Only rubble in this once grand feasting hall.

*   **C) Sinking Pit:** This gaping chasm contains deadly falls, slips, and a Female Black Maw far below.
*   **D) Bunk Rooms & Balcony:** The rickety railing overlooks the sundered hall; rooms are bare but for decrepit cots.

**Tunneling Black Maw**
*   **Tunnels:** True to its name, the Male Black Maw digs paths to hunt and travel by. They are smooth-walled, only as wide as necessary, and are prone to collapse.
*   **Trapping & Tracking:** Male Black Maws place traps that exhaust prey in areas of high traffic and hunt by ambush or tracking blood until the prey is no longer a direct threat.
*   **Male & Female:** Female Black Maws have smaller claws and larger jaws. They stay in softer sand pits digging deeper into the earth and producing eggs while the male lures & drags prey into the pits.
*   **Brood & Life Cycle:** Once the female Black Maw lays a clutch of eggs, the male stashes them in a safe location where prey will be plentiful; they molt & grow after feeding.

### Map Description for Gameplay

The map depicts two distinct levels layered on top of one another: the upper **Lodge** (a ruined castle) and the **Underground Tunnels** beneath it.

**1. The Upper Lodge (The Building):**
*   **Layout:** The lodge is a structured, rectangular footprint of rooms.
*   **Key Landmarks:** The entrance (A) is at the bottom of the structure. Moving clockwise, you have the ruined kitchen (I), the cellar access (J), and the larder (K). The upper-left corner contains the bunk rooms (D) and the main tower (B). The interior is largely open, marked by "Sinking Pit" (C) in the center, which acts as a major hazard.
*   **Navigation:** The building is interconnected by doorways and halls, but it is heavily restricted by "Collapsing Terrain" (marked by rough, broken lines). 

**2. The Underground Tunnels (The Den):**
*   **Layout:** Located visually below the lodge on the map, this area is organic and cavernous, consisting of sprawling, winding corridors (O) rather than squared-off rooms. 
*   **Key Landmarks:** The "Abattoir" (M) serves as a hub for the displaced kobolds. The tunnels spiral out toward the "Kobold Den" (N) and terminate at the far right in a path leading "To the Underhive" (R).
*   **Hazards:** The tunnel section is littered with "Minor Pits" (P) where the Female Black Maw resides, often surrounded by ambush points.

**3. Visual Markers (Legend):**
To help players navigate the traps mentioned in the key, the map uses distinct colored/lettered circles overlaid on the layout:
*   **F (Orange circles):** These mark **Ferality Traps**, found near doors and transition points in the Lodge, and at the entrance to the tunnel systems.
*   **S (Green circles):** These mark **Spike Traps**, found along main corridors and the cellar passage.
*   **P (Purple circles):** These mark **Paralysis Traps** (and the Black Maw pits), concentrated heavily in the underground tunnel system to represent where the larvae are active.

**Gameplay Note:** The connection between the two levels is strictly controlled. Players can only move between the Lodge and the Tunnels via the **Chimney Ladder (F)** in the top-middle or the **Cellar Stair (J)** in the bottom-right. The Lodge is structured for defensive, room-to-room combat, while the Tunnels are designed for ambush-style gameplay and tracking.
