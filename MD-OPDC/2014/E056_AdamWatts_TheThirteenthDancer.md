**[Top Left / Intro]**
A giant statue stands in the wilderness, hollowed out and occupied by strange creatures and their stranger prisoner.

**[Numbered Locations]**
1. **The Head:** There is a mechanism in the head of the statue that helps keep the Whisper Man in the chamber below contained. Three crystal spheres orbit the stairwell. Disturbing them will result in delusions and prophecy.

2. **The Heart:** There is a creature known as a Whisper Man chained in the center of the large circular chamber at the heart of the statue. It is a servant of the demon Ivodrol, Devourer of Words, and constantly whispers prophecy and dark secrets. Looking at the Whisperer will let cause other Whisper Men to see you, and they will begin hunting you in your dreams. The room outside the cell contains crates of rare incense worth 3000 gold, and a blackbird mage.

3. **Meditation Circle:** An open shaft from the Whisper Chamber above opens above a meditation circle on this floor, where faint echoes of the Whisper Man’s ramblings can be heard. The shaft is curved so those meditating on its words do not see the Man itself and become cursed. To the west of the meditation circle is a library where the records of what the Whisper Man has said are kept. A librarian and three assistants will flee out the balcony to the east if threatened and glide away.

4. **The Throne Room:** This room is, essentially, a large stairwell leading up to a throne, upon which sits the high priest of the blackbird cult. This creature has feathers of gold implanted in its back, worth 2500 gold if retrieved whole, which give it AC of chain. It also wields a staff that makes things catch fire if hit, and is guarded by three well-armed minions. The door to the south leads under the stairs, to a ladder on the outside of the statue that leads to a blackbird sniper post.

5. **Living Quarters:** These rooms are where most of the blackbirds live. There are eight armed birds here. A trapdoor in the center of the center-left room can be activated by lever to drop the unwary. The small room to the north of the eastern large room is behind a secret door, and contains an insane animate skeleton with a magic sword that has been locked in there for centuries. The blackbirds’ rooms have some minor personal treasures and five embroidered silk rugs worth 600 gold.

6. **The Kitchen/Storage:** This area was once a kitchen, and is now used to store food and barrels of fresh water. There is a pit in the central room that drops down to the previous level. Two blackbirds guard the door to the east, keeping an eye on the wilderness.

7. **Main Entry Hall:** The main entry hall. The support pillars here are essential to keeping the statue upright. The main door is guarded by a spear-toting blackbird with an attack raptor on a leash.

**[Random Whisper Man Prophecies]**
*If the players investigate the library, roll:*
01. You will speak to the beast, and when the beast speaks back you will know him as rock knows stone, for he is Ivodrol, Devourer of Words.
02. When the Red King falls, so too does the Queen of Smoke.
03. The orphan’s fear is unfounded! She will never see the knife.
04. See the demon in his eye? See the demon in his smile? Hear the angel on his breath?
05. Dark blades cut dark wings and shining chain alike. Can the birds not see?
06. The Jester dances with his dust, blood in his hands.

**[Blackbirds]**
Crow- and magpie-men live in the statue, collecting shiny things from land-bound travellers and villages below. Much of what they have stolen is trash, but there are riches to be found in the blackbirds’ hoard, among the rubbish. Blackbirds can fly, or at least glide slowly, but they can only do so when wearing light armor and not holding any item larger than a knife.

### Map Layout for Non-Visual Players

The dungeon is designed as a vertical structure inside a giant hollow statue. To navigate this, imagine the statue as a multi-story building.

*   **Overall Structure:** The statue has two levels depicted. The "upper" section contains the head and heart, while the "lower" section contains the throne room, living quarters, and entry. 
*   **The Head (Area 1):** This is the highest point. It is a compact, circular room. It connects downwards to the rest of the structure.
*   **The Heart/Chamber (Area 2):** Below the head is a large circular chamber. The Whisper Man is in the center, chained. It serves as the primary "boss" or puzzle area.
*   **Meditation & Library (Area 3):** Located directly below the Heart. There is a shaft connecting Area 2 down to Area 3. The library is to the west of the meditation circle.
*   **The Stairwell/Throne (Area 4):** This is a significant junction point. It is a large room dominated by a grand staircase leading to the high priest's throne. This room contains the main exit/entrance to the exterior ladder (leading to the sniper post).
*   **The Living Quarters (Area 5):** A series of connected rooms adjacent to the throne room. It is a residential block with a central trapdoor and a hidden room to the north.
*   **Kitchen & Storage (Area 6):** Located near the living quarters, this area connects to the lower levels and has a pit that leads back to the previous floor.
*   **Entry Hall (Area 7):** This is the ground level. It features large support pillars essential for the statue’s stability. The main entrance is here, guarded by the spear-wielder and the raptor.

**Navigation:** 
*   **Labels A, B, C, D:** These represent transition points. When you move through a door or trapdoor labeled "A" on one floor, you emerge at the "A" on the corresponding floor. 
*   **The Red Lines:** Faded lines on the map indicate connections (stairs, shafts, or ladders) that move characters between the upper and lower levels. If you are in the Entry Hall (7), you must ascend through the Kitchen (6) or the Throne Room (4) to reach the upper meditation and heart chambers.
