### Part 1: Full Transcription

**Title:** The Devil’s Pitchfork
**Premise:** The PCs have been trapped in Hell for longer than they can remember. A rumor is heard of a possible escape: The Devil’s Pitchfork. It might be the only way home. It also might be safer to stay in Hell! Intended for 1E AD&D, but can be adapted to other systems. © 2014 Darren Bellisle. 

#### Room Descriptions

**I. Bealach, The Entryway**
Massive doors open into a cathedral-arched hallway. Just inside the doors is a giant rotting scarecrow (AC 4, MV 9’’, HD 8, 2 att. [2d6 each], size L) with a “some go this way” sign under its right arm (pointing to II) and a “some go that way” sign under its left arm (pointing to VI). It holds a large brass key in its right hand (the only thing that can open the hatch to II), and an identical key of brass in its left (the only thing that can open the hatch to VI). The keys can be removed easily, but any attempt to damage the scarecrow will cause it to attack. A third key, forged of arjale, is hidden inside the scarecrow’s head—it is the only thing that will open the hatch leading from X to XI.
*(Doors are 20' x 8' x 1'; not locked, but a combined STR of 55 is required to pull one open.)*

**II. Aingeal Tuiteamach, The Chamber of the Fallen**
The walls are a mosaic of white angels and black demons. A wide spiral staircase with no handrails runs up the center of the room to a ladder below a hatch in the southwest corner of the ceiling (leading to III). If anyone reaches the halfway point of the stairs (or otherwise gets to a height of 70' above the floor), all the PCs are attacked—by eight of the angels (erinyes in disguise), who detach themselves from the walls and fly screeching at the intruders.

**III. Ioma-Ghaoth, The Chamber of Chaos**
A roaring whirlwind fills this entire chamber. The upper part of the room is completely obscured by a spiraling mass of black smoke; 100' above the floor, the cyclone begins tapering to a point, which terminates 5' above the floor. This uncontrollable whirlwind will pull any creatures (man-sized or smaller) within 10' of its perimeter off their feet and send them tumbling into the air. There are long iron spikes sticking out of the walls in the upper part of the room. The ceiling has handholds and a hatch leading to IV.

**IV. Dubh Uisge, The Chamber of the Deep**
This chamber is filled with rank, stagnant water, with bits of glowing seaweed and kelp floating around. A faint circle of light is visible above. The water does not pour out of the hatch into III when it is opened; it is magically confined to the chamber. An eye of the deep floats about in the darkness. The chamber has a 10' wide circular opening at the top, which emerges into V.

**V. Faram Toisgeal, The Brass Chamber**
A dark, 10' wide pool of water is in the center of this teardrop-shaped room. The walls are polished brass. A 20' tall gong stands on the west side of the room; an iron golem (4d12 damage) stands guard. The gong requires three people with STR 16+ to ring. Once struck, a circular portal (20' diameter) will open in the eastern wall, lining up with the tunnel in X. The noise lasts 10 rounds, making speech impossible.

**VI. Ioma-Shlighe Suthainn, The Chamber of the Lost**
Lights and shadows move off innumerable planes of force. Treat the room as a permanent *maze* spell, affecting everyone. It takes sixty times longer than normal to find an exit. For PCs with an INT < 8, the player must make a WIS check every 4 hours. A failed roll means the PC goes insane (melancholia, paranoia, etc.). When an exit is found, roll a die: odd = door back out into I; even = hatch leading up to VII.

**VII. Sàr-Shealladh, The Chamber of Falsehood**
A wooded glade with waterfalls and sea breezes. Five adventurers greet the PCs, claiming they failed to escape Hell and urging the PCs to give up and stay. This is actually a pack of five bearded devils. If defeated, the illusion reveals a nightmare of rotting trees and thorn bushes. The hatch to VIII is on the ceiling.

**VIII. Ifrinn, The Chamber of Flames**
A vertical tunnel of roaring flames. Being inside the flames causes 6d6 damage/round; inside the 10' "core" causes 4d6 damage/round. Cold/ice-based spells beat back the flames for one round. The hatch to IX is on the ceiling.

**IX. Faram Deas-Làmh, The Bronze Chamber**
Bladed pendulums swing in a confounding pattern. Treat as a permanent *blade barrier* spell, doing 8d8 damage every round (DEX check for half damage). The longest pendulum is a "hammer" that can sound the 20' gong located here. Pushing the gong on its rail to the east allows the pendulum to strike it. Striking it opens a circular portal in the western wall.

**X. Staran, The Crossroad**
Two 20' diameter tunnels intersect within the central pillar. One runs west-to-east (aligning with V and IX); the other is a vertical shaft leading down (to a lethal 200' fall) and up to XI. Rough wooden beams criss-cross the shaft; the 6th and 10th beams collapse if >50 lbs are placed on them. A trio of shadows lurks here. The hatch to XI is locked (requires the arjale key).

**XI. Mullach, The Chamber of Ways**
The entire Pitchfork operates like a tuning fork: the central pillar (X and XI) is “out of phase” unless both gongs (in V and IX) are ringing simultaneously. Arched stone doorways ring the perimeter. One has a grim reaper image—the “death’s gate.” One willing PC entering the gate is transported to safety; others can then pass. Unwilling PCs forced through will also be transported, but the gate then closes forever, trapping the remaining PCs.

### Part 2: Map Description

The map is a highly stylized, vertical cross-section of a tower called "The Devil's Pitchfork." Its layout resembles a **tuning fork** standing upright.

1.  **The Base (The Entryway):** At the very bottom is **Room I**, the entrance hall. It acts as the anchor for the entire structure.
2.  **The Central Pillar:** Rising vertically from the bottom is a central column. This column contains **Room II** (bottom), **Room X** (the middle crossroad), and **Room XI** (the top summit).
3.  **The Two Prongs:** From the central pillar, two large arms branch out horizontally, then curve upward, mirroring each other:
    *   **The Left Prong:** Consists of **Room IV** (bottom) and **Room V** (top). These are teardrop-shaped chambers. Room V contains the large gong.
    *   **The Right Prong:** Consists of **Room VIII** (bottom) and **Room IX** (top). Room IX contains the bladed pendulums and the second gong.
4.  **The "Weird" Chambers:** Rooms **III, VI, and VII** appear to be side-chambers or "pockets" connected via hatches to the primary vertical or horizontal structures, representing the chaotic or illusory nature of the tower.
5.  **Connections:** The map is dominated by verticality. Players move through hatchways in the ceilings or floors, spiral staircases, and horizontal tunnels that align only when the gongs (in V and IX) are struck to shift the "central pillar" into phase.
6.  **Scale:** A scale marker on the right indicates that the vertical distance from the bottom to the top is significant, with a note that falling down the central shaft (Room X) results in a 200-foot plummet.
