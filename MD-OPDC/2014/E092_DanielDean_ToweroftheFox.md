### Accessibility Description of the Map
This is not a spatial map (like a grid of rooms), but rather a **procedural narrative engine**. To play this, you do not need to look at a floor plan; you need the text descriptions.

1.  **The "Map" is the Tower:** The dungeon exists as a series of abstract "worlds" found inside tapestries. When the players enter the tower, they are not moving through corridors, but jumping into magical paintings.
2.  **The Mechanics:**
    *   **The Entrance:** A gatehouse in a forest. It is guarded by a "Tower of Etiquette" which requires specific social or magical interaction to enter.
    *   **The Rooms:** Every time a player enters a "room" or follows the fox into a tapestry, the DM rolls a 1d20. The result determines the setting of that room (the list of 20 provided in the text).
    *   **The Goal:** The game is a "ticking clock" scenario. The players must catch the fox, return it to the top of the tower, and stop the "horrible things" (Ghouls, Demons, etc.) from leaking out of the tapestries.
3.  **Visualization:** If you are playing, imagine the "Tower" as a central hub (the top of the tower where the Loom is) surrounded by infinite shifting portals (the tapestries). The art on the page—depicting knights and mythological beasts—serves to set the mood for these tapestry worlds. If you are describing this to someone, describe it as a **"portal-hopping fantasy hunt,"** where the geography changes every time the players step through a tapestry.
