**THE SHATTERED TEMPLE**
*An otherworldly, tile-based, reusable, randomly generated dungeon.*

The temple of Azuria was located in another dimension, reachable through a system of ancient portals. No one knows why the Stone God shattered the temple or what happened with the priests. Today only three different portals lead to this nearly abandoned realm. Many adventurers enter the Shattered Temple in search of secrets and ancient treasures. For some, this pale realm becomes a graveyard. For it is a capricious place, constantly shifting and changing, roamed by many a creature. Floating shards of the Temple became a territory for angry spirits and beasts, and home for more than one great unspoken evil... Will you dare enter the Shattered Temple?

#### HOW TO USE THE SHATTERED TEMPLE
0) Stick this sheet onto some cardboard.
1) Cut out all the tiles.
2) Place green tile with portal (marked with S) on the table. Remove the red tile (marked with the Boss icon). Shuffle the remaining 11 tiles.
3) Players always start on the green tile.
4) Players can only move where the shining arrows point from the tile they are on.
5) "Build" the dungeon by placing tiles as players explore the realm.
6) When players are exploring a tile, roll 1d6 for each icon using the tables provided.
7) Use the red tile with the Boss icon whenever you think it’s time.

### Dungeon Tile Map Description
The dungeon consists of 12 distinct square tiles arranged in a 4x3 grid. Each tile contains a piece of floating stone terrain. 

**Navigation Key:**
Each tile has edges. Some edges are flat (meaning no path), while others feature white arrows pointing outward. To explore, players must match the arrows on their current tile to an edge of a new, face-down tile. If the arrows align, the "path" is open.

**Visual Guide to Tiles:**
*   **The Start Tile (S):** Located at the far left of the middle row. It is a green-tinted stone platform with a large stone archway (the portal).
*   **The Boss Tile:** Located at the far right of the bottom row. It features a red background, a large demonic-looking circular gate, and the Boss Icon (a stylized head with a red circle).
*   **Intermediate Tiles:** These feature various terrain features:
    *   **Architecture:** Several tiles feature ruined arches, broken stone columns, or stone stairs leading to nowhere.
    *   **Iconography:** Tiles are marked with small, distinct symbols that tell you which table to roll on:
        *   **Skull:** Monster table.
        *   **Water Drop:** Liquid table.
        *   **Crystal/Diamond:** Crystal table.
        *   **Flower:** Plant table.
        *   **Backpack/Scroll:** Remains table.
        *   **Question Mark:** Riddle table.
    *   **Specialty tiles:** 
        *   One tile features a large stone "skull face" carved into a wall.
        *   One tile features a cluster of glowing floating crystals.
        *   One tile features a lone, wispy tree.

**Gameplay Note:** Because these tiles are cut out and shuffled, the "dungeon" is never the same twice. The "light level" gauge at the bottom (100%, 50%, 20%, 0%) implies that as players move deeper into the dungeon (or as time passes), the challenge level or environmental difficulty increases.
