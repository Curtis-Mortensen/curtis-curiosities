#### Background
Count Arpad of Thistleshire is trying to secure a peace accord with the neighboring lizardfolk of Lake Ervad. The tribe of lizardfolk have been raiding the county which borders the lake. The Count’s last two emissaries, Otto and Horthy, have disappeared, and he would rather not risk his own men, so he is seeking adventurers to secure the accord. Arpad offers the PCs a jeweled sword (worth 500 gp) as payment once the treaty has been signed. Unknown to him, his niece, Zasa, an evil cleric, is leading the raids in the county with a following of brigands and enslaved lizardfolk. Her forces occupy the abandoned Cove Fort and she uses the water from the Baleful Spring to control the lizardfolk. The lizardfolk tribe has been trying to rescue their enslaved kin but have failed. The lizardfolk chieftain, Kizar, tells the PCs that Zasa has enslaved his people and will sign the treaty if the PCs free the lizardfolk and defeat Zasa.

#### Room Key: The Cove Fort
The fort is built of volcanic rock from the Bleak Mountains by the Knights of the Golden Sword to guard the north shore of Lake Ervad and was abandoned when the order fell.

*   **1. Courtyard:** The entry double doors are guarded by two lizardfolk and their dwarven sergeant. The group has poor discipline and are dicing most of the time. They will invite the PCs to play, thinking them either sailors or new recruits. Once inside, the courtyard is gravel-strewn and has a roofed open stable. A wooden platform is where Zasa gives speeches. Five sailors are hauling crates across gravel unsuccessfully towards the dock.
*   **2. Barracks:** The main sleep area for the brigands. Twelve bunk beds with six chests line the wall. Four brigands are asleep. A tapestry hangs on the wall showing knights doing battle by a lake; one wields a golden sword. If examined for 6 rounds by a Lawful PC, the sword glows softly and appears in their hand. It is a +1 weapon that glows in the presence of Chaotic alignment.
*   **3. Hall:** Has a fireplace and two large tables. 1d4 brigands and two sailors are present. Emissaries Otto and Horthy are bound in ropes in the corner. A spiral staircase leads up. If detected, a brigand will drop an iron chandelier (DEX check, 2d6 damage).
*   **4. Distillery:** Zasa distills water from the spring here. A tunnel connects to the barrel house. Three nixie skeletons are in the copper still and will pound on the inside. Imbibing the concentrated water triggers the *Charm Person* spell effect (3rd level magic user), with the first person the drinker interacts with being the default caster.
*   **5. Chapel:** A chapel to the dark goddess with an ebony statue of a woman, an altar, a holy book, and votive candles.
*   **6. Zasa’s Chamber:** Well-appointed. Contains an iron brazier and a "Grimacing Chest" (save vs. Spell or be fascinated and trapped in an extra-dimensional space). Hidden under the mattress: 200 sp, 1000 gp, 8 gems, and elven jewelry worth 200 gp.
*   **The Ship:** The *Dark Pilgrim* is a cog captained by Vaar, an elf, which transports spoils to Vicewick.
*   **7. Main Deck:** Contains trade goods (1473 gp value) and a pet baboon named Joko.
*   **8. Forecastle:** Two heavy crossbows mounted here. Three sailors drink grog and will fire on strangers after a warning.
*   **9. Stern Castle:** Contains the tiller, chart table, and a rigging box holding a crab spider.
*   **10. Cabin:** Contains six stacked hammocks, chests (with trapped treasure and coins), and hanging food. Captain Vaar (3rd-level elf fighter) is here.
*   **11. Baleful Spring:** Bubbling sulfurous water. Zasa uses a bucket on a rope to gather it.
    *   *Spring Effects (1d6):* 1. Change color for 1d6+1 days; 2. Amnesia for 1d8+1 rounds; 3. Babble nonsense for 1d4+1 rounds; 4. Become incandescent for 1d12+1 rounds; 5. Charm Person spell (4th level MU); 6. Nothing.
*   **12. Barrel House:** Stores the spring water, connected by an earthen tunnel to the distillery.
*   **13. Huts:** Squalid huts for the enslaved lizardfolk.
