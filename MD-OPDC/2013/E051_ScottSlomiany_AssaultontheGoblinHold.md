### Part 2: Play Guide for Non-Visual Players

#### The Core Mechanic: "The Dice Fight"
This game is a combat simulator using dice as your health and attack power.
1.  **Your Resources:** You begin with **5 blue dice** (representing your Hero) and **3 red dice** (representing the Enemy/Trap).
2.  **How to Fight:** When you encounter an enemy or obstacle:
    *   Roll your dice.
    *   **Matching Numbers:** If a blue die and a red die show the same number, you have scored a "hit" on that enemy.
    *   **Enemy Hits:** If the obstacle (Red) rolls a 6, they hit you. If you roll a 4, 5, or 6 on a blue die, you lose that die.
    *   **Damage:** Every time you score a hit, the enemy loses one of their dice. Every time they hit you, you lose one of your dice.
    *   **Victory/Defeat:** If the enemy runs out of dice, they are defeated, and you move forward. If you run out of dice, the game ends (you lose).

#### Navigation
The dungeon is non-linear. You "traverse" it by moving from numbered section to numbered section, usually indicated by arrows or instructions at the end of the text blocks.

*   **Setup:** You start at **4Q** (The Great Hall).
*   **Exploration:** From 4Q, you can descend the stairs into **2B** (Squadron of Goblins).
*   **The Goal:** You are trying to find and rescue the "infant." You must fight through the various rooms (like 3S, 5H, 5W) to reach **3B**, where the infant is located.
*   **Progression:** Most rooms require you to "rip the tab" (or conceptually mark the area as complete) once you have won the dice combat for that area. This allows you to advance to the next connected section of the map.
*   **The Boss:** Once you have the infant, you eventually navigate to **7** to face the Goblin King. The final mechanic involves using the King's head to intimidate the remaining goblins (Section 15), effectively ending the combat through roleplay/mechanics rather than just rolling dice.

#### Tips for Play
*   **Keep track of your dice:** You need a physical set of 5 blue dice and 3 red dice to play. 
*   **Track "Rips":** Since this is a physical paper game, keep a notepad to track which sections you have "ripped" (cleared), so you know which paths are now safe to travel or which doors have been unlocked.
