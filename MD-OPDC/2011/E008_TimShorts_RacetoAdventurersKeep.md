**Race to Adventurer’s Keep by Tim Shorts**

**Current Situation**
The players were training with an owlbear in heat, when Captain Tenacious had a mage cast sleep on all the trainees. The limp bodies of the trainees were transported to various parts of the massive forest that surrounds Adventurer's Keep. The players awaken wearing only their skivvies and a note in their hand. *Time for graduation meat shields. Return to the keep as quickly as you can. The first to return gets a large monetary hug from me, Captain Tenacious. Should there be a tie the one who returns with the most gifts for me shall win.*

For those who fail, means that you have died. Please die where we can recover your body so a resurrection may be attempted. Avoid being eaten as it will make the process more difficult. And frankly we don’t have the time.
Good luck you sorry little bastards. See you at Adventurer’s Keep!

**GM Notes**
This adventure needs no map so stop looking for it. GMs will need to be quick and clever. This entire adventure is run on a series of random encounters. This can be run solo or with a group.

The base time to return to the keep is three hours. So a minimum of three encounters will be rolled for each player. Roll the encounter and the player interacts with the situation.

Because this is a timed adventure it is critical the GM keep track of each player’s time. It is also critical that the GM keep track of item obtained and calculate the value in case of a tie. Some items values are listed while simple items like a dagger will not.

### Description of the "Map"
The adventure intentionally **does not have a physical map.** Instead, the "map" is represented by a **time-based mechanical loop**.

*   **The Goal:** The players are lost in a forest and must "return to the keep."
*   **The System:** This is a procedural exploration game. The "map" is effectively a deck of 50 potential events. Every turn, the GM rolls to determine what the player encounters.
*   **Progression:** Rather than moving a token on a grid, players move through "time." The base goal is to survive and navigate for three hours. 
*   **Obstacles:** Many encounters (such as 3, 10, 16, 27, 30, 37, 38, 44, and 49) explicitly state "no progress is made this turn." This acts as a mechanical "wall" or "delay" on the map, forcing the player to waste time.
*   **Shortcuts:** Some encounters (like 50) allow the player to "reduce time," acting as a mechanical fast-forward button through the forest.
*   **Strategic Play:** Because the GM is tracking the time for each individual player, the "map" is a race. A player's choice to engage with side-items (like the skull in 31, or the map in 11) acts as a strategic risk-vs-reward calculation that dictates how much time they spend before reaching the finish line.
