### 3. Map Description for Players
The map is a 10-column by 8-row grid of hexagons. To navigate, imagine the grid coordinates like a map: the first two digits are the column (01 to 10 from left to right) and the last two digits are the row (01 to 08 from top to bottom).

*   **Topography:** The map is split diagonally. The top-left region represents the **Highlands** (mountains), while the bottom-right region represents the **Lowlands** (jungle and wetlands).
*   **Dividing Line:** A massive, impassable cliff known as "The Drop" runs like a jagged scar through the center of the map, separating the high ground from the low ground.
*   **Major Landmark:** "The Fin" is a large, distinctive red sandstone ridge that cuts across several hexes in the middle-east section of the map (roughly columns 07 to 09). It is impossible to climb over and serves as a primary navigation point.
*   **Water:** There is a caldera lake in the western section (near 0108) and river systems traversing the central jungle areas.
*   **Features:** Within the hexes, you will find a variety of icons and markings. Some hexes contain symbols for caves, hills, or dense jungle, while others are noted as having specific hazards like toxic smoke (volcanic areas) or ruins.

If you are a player, think of the hex grid as your "board." Moving from one hex to an adjacent one takes time. Using the hex coordinates (e.g., 0506) allows you and the referee to track your exact location in this dangerous, monster-filled wilderness.
