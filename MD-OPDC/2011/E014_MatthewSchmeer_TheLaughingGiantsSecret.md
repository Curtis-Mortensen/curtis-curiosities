The western hills of Vlarkenden are some of the most beautiful untamed lands in all the realms. **About the Inn**
No one knows or remembers when The Inn of the Laughing Giant first appeared or how long it has existed in the valley. The Inn is tainted by powerful residual magic (detect magic spells & magic items make everything, including PCs, faintly glow) which affects every living thing that stays too long, until the point that escape is futile and the characters must remain in the inn forever or risk dying upon setting foot off the property. The magic blanketing the inn is so powerful that PCs will permanently lose 5% of accumulated XP for each night spent at the inn if they do not settle their tab before dawn of each day. Hogarth, the halfing-giant proprietor, cannot leave the inn’s property, else he will immediately turn to dust. Other NPCs—including Sarae & Bükan, serving wenches, Sir Norris, the bouncer, Gwark Nithal, a petty thief, Tenk Ekralc, a wizened ranger, and Bob, the tavern drunk—suffer from the same malady. Hogarth, driven mad by knowledge of his fate, often kills his guests, using their blood to brew his ale, and serving their flesh to unsuspecting patrons. None of the other NPCs know this.

### **Map Description (For Non-Sighted Players)**

The maps are organized by floor level, laid out on a grid.

**1. Ground Level:** 
This is a large, rambling structure. The main entrance leads into a large, open tavern (Room 1) taking up the center of the building. Directly below this is the Bar (Room 2) and kitchen areas (3, 4, 6, 7). Radiating out from the tavern are several small, rectangular guest rooms (8 through 15) arranged along a hallway. To the right of the building is a large, open Yard (17), which connects to a stable area (18) and a series of hitching posts (19) jutting out to the far right.

**2. Upper Level:** 
This level has a very uniform, barracks-style layout. It consists of a long, straight horizontal hallway running through the middle of the building. Nineteen small, square guest rooms (labeled 1 through 19) are positioned on both the top and bottom sides of this hallway, like cells in a prison block.

**3. Cellar/Dungeon Level:** 
This map is distinctly different from the others. The lines are irregular, suggesting this level is carved into raw earth or uneven stone rather than built with standard walls. The rooms are scattered. Room 1 is a large, open entry space. Room 2 is off to the side, and Room 3 is a larger, prominent chamber. Room 4 is a square, central room containing a cistern. Rooms 5, 6, 7, and 8 are clustered together toward the bottom and right, representing the cold storage areas where the "supplies" are kept.
