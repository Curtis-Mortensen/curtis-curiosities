### Map Description for Play
The dungeon is represented by four distinct 3x3 square grid "tiles." To play this without seeing the map, understand that the dungeon is not a static building but a shifting set of rooms:

*   **Tile A:** A basic room with no exits. In the center sits the pedestal with the portrait.
*   **Tile B:** A room with two exits: one leading North and one leading South.
*   **Tile C:** A room with three exits: North, South, and West.
*   **Tile D:** A four-way room with exits in all directions (North, South, East, and West).

**How to run it:**
Instead of drawing a traditional map, provide the players with a list of room numbers. When they enter a room, tell them which tile layout it uses (e.g., "You enter a four-way room (Tile D)"). If they attempt to map the dungeon on paper, the GM should inform them that their marks mysteriously change whenever they return to a previous room, reinforcing that the dungeon is "non-Euclidean." The rooms do not have fixed geography; they loop and rearrange themselves.
