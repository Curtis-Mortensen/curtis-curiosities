### Transcription of Written Text

**A Tempest in a Teaspoon**
By Todd Mitchell

**Mission Briefing:**
You have been approached by an agent representing a former employee of the defunct OmniTech corporation to retrieve the sensor data from a monitor device deep in the gravity well of a pint-sized, but massive Neutron Star. Will you hire a Tug or pilot yourself? Hopefully your pilot is up to the task because if you don’t follow the proper route to the monitor, gravity shear will tear your ship apart. And did we mention it’s in an asteroid field?

**Mining Tugs:** 
These small ships have powerful engines and thick radiation shielding. They are slow, with powerful engines and have decent sensor capability. Miners are a cagy bunch and their tugs are often jury rigged with weapon mounts or electronic countermeasures.

**Asteroids and X-Rays:**
Shifts in the surface gravity of the neutron star cause the orbits of smaller asteroids to be unstable. The closer to the star, the more this occurs. It also emits bursts of X-Rays which disrupt systems. Roll 1d6 more frequently as the players progress down the gravity well:
*   **1:** Impact! Ship damage or Hull Breach.
*   **2:** A Near miss! Proximity alarms will go off.
*   **3-5:** All Clear.
*   **6:** X-Ray burst – sensors out for the next few turns.

**Gravitational Shear:**
The blue spiral represents the optimal path through the warped space of the star’s gravitational field past the orbital debris and larger asteroids. Ships travelling off the optimal path will be subject to turbulence, increased danger form asteroids and finally hull breaching shear forces. 

**Locations:**
1.  **OT station:** This station was originally an OmniTech science outpost. It is now owned by a struggling mining consortium. The station is mostly mothballed, but includes the "War Daddies" night club and "Sam Ford’s Salvage and Loan." Sam Ford runs both and is involved in illegal activities; he doesn't want the monitor data retrieved. There are over 600 people living and working here.
2.  **Mining Asteroid:** Home to about 1500 miners. This is the primary refining facility for the area. Most miners will not go deeper into the gravity well.
3.  **Listening post:** A small, hard-to-detect post manned by two of Sam Ford’s crew. They notify the Drug Lab (#8) of any inbound ship traffic. They are armed and can be bribed or fought.
4.  **Wreck of the Pequod:** An old, derelict ship missing its bridge and life support. It has been picked over, but a search reveals it was hunting a "space creature" highly resistant to energy attacks.
5.  **Asteroids:** Unremarkable rocks; they can reflect false signals or contain useful elements.
6.  **Interesting Rock:** A body made of materials originating from outside the system.
7.  **The Kraken:** A ship-sized tentacled creature that feeds on metal. It uses ion propulsion to match the speed of a fast cruiser. It is highly resistant to energy attacks, camouflaged, and ill-tempered.
8.  **The Lab:** A secret drug and bio-weapons lab. It contains 8-10 scientists and 25-30 guards. They have a missile battery and can command the Kraken if provoked.
9.  **Blanket of doom:** A 2km sq flat object (an old light sail). There is a 75% chance its position will focus stellar radiation onto passing ships, causing severe damage.
10. **The Monitor:** Located deep in the high-shear gravity well. Retrieving the data requires piloting skill and EVA, with constant risk of asteroids and X-ray bursts. Players must then navigate back out past the station and Sam Ford.
