**[Header]**
Dimensional Den: Small Adventure for levels 4 to 6. By Tony Garcia (mgleitef@gmail.com).

**[Story]**
A wizard is known as Ulter, the “Southpaw,” who discovered the terrible secret of traveling with portals. However, because it was mighty magic, he was unable to control it and ended up creating an area of instability in his old magic laboratory. Ulter is gone, but rumors that in his laboratory there is a legendary treasure, as well as the secret of this powerful magic, have attracted adventurers. However, a curse remains on the spot: each time an adventurer explores it, the place is never the same, and if this treasure exists, it seems that Ulter has disappeared with it. Is the challenge launched, ready to take a chance?

**[Room I]**
You enter a hexagon-shaped room in the old Ulter laboratory. There are strange symbols drawn on the walls and remnants of furniture shattered on the floor covered with dust and dirt. The air has a strong smell of ozone, and at the other end, there is a magical dazzling portal in purple, waiting for you to cross.

**[General Rules]**
Starting, put the “adventurer counter” in room I. This small “dungeon” can be played solo or with a group of 3 players with eight game rounds. These eight rounds are divided into Phase I (from round 1 to 4) and Phase II (from round 5 to 8). At the start of each round, roll 1d6. 
*   **Room:** Consult the "Room Type" table and the "Rooms" table where the portal took him. In each room, there is a description and the possibility of an encounter.
*   **Missing:** The player is lost between dimensions and is considered dead. 
*   **Phase II:** The "Missing" result is transformed into "Exit." It means the portal leads to an exit.
*   If the player reaches the last room (marked with the letter E) in the eighth round, roll 1d6 on the Exit table. In the final of each round (after an encounter roll), move your adventurer counter to the next room.

**[Rules for 2 or 3 players]**
In the case of encounters, the number of monsters must be multiplied by the number of players (e.g., if two zombies are found, these will be 4). In the case of treasure found, each player rolls 1d6 to determine their treasure.

**[Room/Phase Tables]**
*   **Phase I:** 1,5 - Room | 6 - Missing
*   **Phase II:** 1,4 - Room | 5,6 - Exit
*   **Room Type (Roll 1d6):** 1-Type I, 2-Type II, 3-Type III, 4-Type IV, 5-Type V, 6-Type VI (Desert).

**[Room Descriptions]**
*   **Type I (Tropical Forest):** 1-3 No Encounter, 4-5 1 Tiger, 6 Giant Constrictor Snake.
*   **Type II (Swamp):** 1,3 No Encounter, 4-2 Crocodiles, 5-2 Zombies, 6-1 Wight.
*   **Type III (Old Temple):** 1,4 No Encounter, 5-4 Skeletons, 6-1 Mummy.
*   **Type IV (Cavern):** 1-2 No Encounter, 3-4 Orcs, 5-4 Lizardfolks, 6-1 Ogre.
*   **Type V (Dungeon):** 1-2 No Encounter, 3-2 Ghouls, 5-1 Doppelganger, 6-1 Minotaur.
*   **Type VI (Desert):** 1-2 No Encounter, 3-2 Harpies, 5-1 Giant Scorpion, 6-1 Manticore.

**[Treasure Table (Roll 1d6)]**
1: 1 D10x100 Gold Pieces | 2: 1 Magic Weapon | 3: 1 Cursed Weapon | 4: 1 Magic Armour (Class Type) | 5: 2 d10 x 100 gold pieces diamond | 6: 1 Cursed Armour (Class Type)

**[Exit Table (Roll 1d6)]**
1,3: Exit without a treasure | 4,5: Treasure (roll on Treasure Table) | 6: You are disintegrated by the magic of the portals and die.

### 3. Map Description for Playability
The "map" is not a spatial floor plan, but an **abstract linear track** designed to track the progress of the game rounds.

*   **The Path:** Imagine a horizontal line consisting of 8 connected hexagons arranged in a single row from left to right.
*   **Starting Point:** The far-left hexagon is labeled **"Room I."** This is where your counter begins.
*   **Progression:**
    *   There are 4 hexagons labeled with a **"?"** symbol underneath the label **"Phase I."**
    *   There are 3 hexagons labeled with a **"?"** symbol underneath the label **"Phase II."**
    *   The final, 8th hexagon on the far right is labeled **"E"** (for Exit).
*   **How to use it:** You are essentially moving your "Adventurer" marker from one hexagon to the next for each of the 8 game rounds.
    *   **Round 1:** You are in Room I.
    *   **Rounds 2, 3, 4:** You move through the Phase I "unknown" rooms.
    *   **Rounds 5, 6, 7:** You move through the Phase II "unknown" rooms.
    *   **Round 8:** You arrive at the "E" (Exit/Final) space.
*   **Visual Aid:** Because the room type is determined by a die roll, you do not need a visual map to navigate. You only need a marker (a coin, token, or die) to track which of the 8 rounds you are currently in. Whenever you move to a new hexagon, you roll your 1d6 to determine which of the six room types (Forest, Swamp, Temple, Cavern, Dungeon, or Desert) you have teleported into.
