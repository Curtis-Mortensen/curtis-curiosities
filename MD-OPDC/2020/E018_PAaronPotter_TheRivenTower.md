The Warlock of Netherspire was renowned for his ability to summon—and banish—almost any entity. **Mechanics**
Every room has a “Risk” number in {brackets}. Whenever vigorous activity (combat, running, jumping) is undertaken in that room without precautions (roped, held by others), characters roll 1d10 + dexterity mod. 
*   If the Result >= Risk: there is no effect.
*   Miss by 1: cracks! Risk increases by 1.
*   Miss by 2: shift! All in room fall prone.
*   Miss by 3: Fall! to ledge or lower room.
*   Miss by 4+: Collapse! Entire room falls to a ledge lower down the cliff, everyone is injured.

### Map Description for Play (Accessibility Guide)

The map is designed as a vertical slice of a tower that has been split down the middle by a magical explosion. The "cliff" side of the drawing is the bottom, showing the dark, jagged rocks of the canyon.

1.  **The Verticality:** The tower is built in levels. The **Observatory** is at the very top. Below it is the **Mid-tower Bedroom**. The middle section contains the **Grand Hall** (the largest, most central room). The lower section (the "basement" and surrounding areas) features the **Basement Lab**, **Conservatory**, and **Terrace**, all of which appear to be hanging precariously over the canyon void.
2.  **Access Points:** Most of the structure is ruined, so there are no standard staircases. You must traverse gaps, climb over piles of rubble, or balance on teetering floorboards. 
    *   The **Grand Hall** serves as the central hub; from there, you can see the **Ballroom** to the side and the **Courtyard** near the base.
    *   The **Basement Lab** is located in the lowest physical point of the structure, directly accessible from the central ruins.
    *   The **Dining Room** and **Library** are located on a higher tier on the left side of the tower.
3.  **The "Ledges":** Throughout the map, there are several small, disconnected ledges depicted outside the tower walls. These are important for the mechanics—if a player fails a "Risk" check, they are likely to fall onto these specific ledges or plunge further down the cliff face.
4.  **Points of Interest:**
    *   **The Tower Top:** A distinct, circular room at the peak (Observatory).
    *   **The Center:** A large, open-air feeling space (Grand Hall) filled with debris and hazards.
    *   **The Hanging Sections:** On the bottom right and left, you have the Conservatory and Terrace, which are illustrated as if they are falling away from the main building, supported only by vines or stone fragments.
