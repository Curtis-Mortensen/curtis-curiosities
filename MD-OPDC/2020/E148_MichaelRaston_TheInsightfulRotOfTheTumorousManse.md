#### Introduction & Hooks
**The Insightful Rot of The Tumorous Manse**
A manse in the dusty outskirts of the city has been lost to corruption.

**Why go there, d6?**
1. Local authorities would like the rot to halt its spreading.
2. A rival assassin group requires intel on Starkan.
3. A pink robed wizard would like a Fleshweed Seed.
4. An eccentric collector would like the Esoteric Book: “Manifesting Dream Treasure”.
5. A dark robed figure wants their brother Balsan recovered (in fact they would like to assassinate them).
6. Rumours of treasure left unguarded (Mirror Dagger and Holy Vials).

#### Glossary & Entities
*   **Fleshweed:** Coral-like growths of flesh. Tumours scintillating with pink tendrils. Happily grasps any flesh and chews it with tiny mouths.
*   **Ambulatory Fleshweed:** Dog-sized clumps that wriggle like worms or stumble on malformed legs. Aggressive.
*   **Fleshweed Seed:** White-pink, fist-sized pearl. Ever drips blood. Implanting into a brain will begin a new Fleshweed growth.
*   **Balsan:** Haggard, emaciated, delirious priest assassin. Mouth oozing Fleshweed. Wants food and to escape. Knows Starkan found a Seed in a dream.
*   **Starkan:** The consciousness of the head assassin priest in the form of a wall-covering amalgamation of flesh and organs. Speaks from six ambulatory mouths. Wants another Seed.
*   **Acolyte:** Pink, hairless apes with slug-like heads terminating in a single human eye. Brute strength. Touch sends "loving" messages to victims.
*   **Deacon:** Pale, 8-foot malformed man. Face featureless save for two spiral horns. Wears twisting Fleshweed like a snake/scarf. Regenerates.
*   **Mirror Dagger:** Infinitely sharp. Slices flesh when blade is an inch away. Difficult to heal.
*   **Holy Vial:** Injecting makes flesh rubbery, twistable, and impervious to all but sharpest blades. Climbing becomes easy. Lasts d4 hours.
*   **Shrine:** Tongue surrounded by eyeball arch. Implants Fleshweed node and bestows a blessing (d6 table results: 1. Command Fleshweed, 2. Vivify dead flesh, 3. Mind reading, 4. Immunity to consumption).
*   **Esoteric Books:** (d10 list: 1. Weaponizing Vermin, 2. Ensuring of Conflict, 3. Manifesting Dream Treasure, 4. Illuminating the Otherworld, 5. Everyday Poisons, 6. Labyrinth of The Mind, 7. Meat Bacterium, 8. My Time with The Brain Dwellers, 9. Holy Rot and The Glory of the Slug, 10. Politics of Death).

### Map Navigation Guide
This map is designed as a series of connected chambers. To navigate it, keep this layout in mind:

*   **The Entrance (Southwest):** The path starts at **Room 1**, a large storage room. You move north through **Room 2** (the cloister) to reach the **Courtyard (Room 4)** where the survivor, Balsan, is hiding.
*   **The Western Stairwell:** From the courtyard area, you find **Room 5**, which leads to the upper levels of the manse.
*   **The Central Courtyard:** Moving east from the entrance area, you enter **Room 6**, a dusty, open-air area containing a Shrine. This connects north to **Room 7** (a library/debris field) and leads into the ground-floor interior rooms (**8, 9, 10**), which are pitch black and laboratory-like.
*   **The Upper Levels:** The eastern section of the map is a cluster of connected rooms (**12 through 21**). You can access these via **Room 11** (the second staircase).
*   **The Interior Cluster (The Heart):** Once upstairs, the rooms are highly interconnected. **Room 12** acts as a choke point full of Fleshweed. From there, you can access the balcony areas (**15, 16**). Moving further into the core of the manse leads you through the flowery, overgrown corridors (**18, 19**) directly to the final chambers.
*   **The Final Sanctum:** The furthest points (North/Northeast) are **Room 20** (where the boss, Starkan, resides) and **Room 21** (the final lab/loot room).

**Key Tactile/Navigation Features:**
*   **Blocked Doors:** Look for the "Fleshweed choked door" icons between corridors; these represent physical barriers that may require force or specific interaction to pass.
*   **Verticality:** There are two distinct staircases (**5 and 11**). If you go up the wrong one, you may be cut off from the main boss or the loot, as the upper rooms are tightly packed.
*   **The Shrines:** Scattered throughout (**6, 14, 15, 16**), these act as dangerous "interactive points" that can provide powerful boons or corrupt the player.
