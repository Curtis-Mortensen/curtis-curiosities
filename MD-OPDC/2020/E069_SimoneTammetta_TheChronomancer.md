*The spirit of the Chronomancer lives: trapped in time, waits the end of all things. In the Palace of the Autarch, past, present and future converge to create a dissonant harmony that has already taken many lives. Will you be strong enough to survive this madness? Will you find his Chronoglobes?*

**Purpose**
The Chronomancer gained his power from 1d4 Chronoglobes he created (roll as many time as obtained result on Rooms/Places to see where can be found). But, lost the control on objects of his power, was imprisoned in time. Destroy them to free him.

**NOTE:** after PCs destroy the last Chronoglobe, will appear the Chronomancer. If PCs have met him: 1 time -> he will thank them and ask to be killed, tired of this world; 2 times -> he will travel into time again, leaving this world; 3 times -> he will fight PCs to imprison them in the Palace, nullifying their efforts. ### Map Description for Gameplay
The map is not a traditional floor plan with rooms connected by lines or grids. Instead, it is an **abstract, surreal art piece** that symbolizes the dungeon's theme: time collapsing in on itself.

*   **Visual Representation:** Imagine a large, dark circular nebula or a portal occupying the left side of the page. It is rendered in high-contrast ink-blot style, appearing as if space and time are physically swirling into a drain.
*   **Key Details within the Illustration:**
    *   **Faces and Figures:** Within the swirling "clouds" of the dark mass, there are faint, distorted outlines of human faces and reaching hands, representing the trapped souls or past inhabitants.
    *   **Mechanical Fragments:** Scattered within the darkness are hints of clockwork: pieces of gears, pendulum-like shapes, and straight, rigid lines that contrast with the organic, swirling ink.
    *   **No Grid:** Because the rooms shift randomly (based on the 1d12 table), this art serves as a "flavor map." It represents the *instability* of the palace rather than a physical space you can navigate on paper. 

**Advice for playing:** Since this is a non-linear dungeon, describe it to players as a "non-Euclidean" space where doors lead to different areas based on the *current flow of time* rather than geography. The map is a representation of the Chronomancer's prison—a whirlpool of disparate moments trapped in a single void.
