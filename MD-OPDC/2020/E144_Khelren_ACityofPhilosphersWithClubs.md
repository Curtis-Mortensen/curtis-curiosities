**Header**
**A CITY OF PHILOSOPHERS WITH CLUBS**
by Khelren (https://khelren.itch.io/)

**Intro**
Unbeknown to anyone, the city has been the field of a metaphysical battle that could lead to nothing less than its entire displacement to another plane of existence. Each alignment will try to influence people to become the prevailing philosophy. To further their goal, each has an avatar in a position of power. Will the PCs join one of these factions or will they free the city from this fearful fate?

**Avatars and Factions**
*The Avatars pose as important people, acting as de facto leaders of each district. They never act by themselves; instead they always direct one of their affiliated factions to make a move. Make no mistake: they are not concerned by the fate of mere mortals. The city must fall in their realm of alignment!*

*   **The Blind Maiden (Loyal Good - War and Industry):** Azure Paladins, Smith Guilds, Iron Maidens, The Forgiveness, The Steel Tower, The Masks.
*   **The Father of Traditions (True Loyal - Fate and Laws):** The Seers, The Weavers, The Maestro, Scribes’ Guild, Vows of Prani, The Creed.
*   **The Beggar King (Loyal Evil - Shadow and Secrets):** The Spymasters, The Cutthroats, The Little Hand, Funerary, The Cant, Ring o’ Shadow.
*   **The One-eyed Slayer (Chaotic Evil - Strength and Storm):** The Warband, The Bashers, The Red Barons, The Pigs, Blood Brothers, The Tempest.
*   **The Trickster Prince (True Chaotic - Time and Eloquence):** Docks’ Lords, The Gentlemen, Brethren Court, The Delirium, The Poets’ Circle, The Adversary.
*   **The Horned Beast (Chaotic Good - Fertility and Life):** High Venator, The Sharp Fang, The Snake, Tree of Worlds, The Inner Circle, Kennelmasters.

**Actions (d6)**
1. Punish / Subjugate / Assault / Spread rumours / Quarrel with / Punish
2. Protect / Protect / Spy on / Steal / Disruption / Protect
3. Set free / Threaten / Threaten / Destroy / Befoul / Overthrow
4. Ally with / Enforce / Kill / Kill / Lie about / Disobey
5. Help / Enforce / Assault / Conquer / Overthrow / Steal
6. Enforce / Rule on / Threaten / Threaten / --- / Set free

**How to use this?**
Roll 3d6. The first two dice indicate which faction or avatar is making a move. The third says which action they are undertaking. 
Roll another 2d6. The first die indicates on which district they are planning to make their move (if you roll the district number, they move against The Hill). The second die indicates the specific target in that district. With that, make the city a constantly evolving battleground.

**The Dark of the City**
The truth is that The Hill district, at the centre of the city, is decisive to the cosmic balance. If the Lady in the Mirror (True Neutral, Magic and Undead), a true neutral avatar, were to be defeated, her district would merge with the district of the avatar that defeated her. She is defended by The Undead and the Wizards’ Guild.

**A Quick Tour of the City**
1. **The University District:** Middle-class. Smog, noxious fumes, cosmopolitan, inebriated students, The white robes of the Brothers of Compassion.
   *   *Locations:* The Royal University, All Saints Orphanage, Mor Sisters’ Dispensary, The Leper House, The Gnomish Factory, The Dwarven Furnace.
2. **The Tribunal District:** Rich. Seat of power, noble houses, hackney cabs, black attire of clerks, sweet music.
   *   *Locations:* The Tribunal, The Queen’s Ice Palace, Ivory Tower, The Scripturium, The Jade House, The Memory Lapse.
3. **The Rail District:** Poor. Beggars, puffing trains, slang, dilapidated houses, shadowy streets, ale that tastes like piss.
   *   *Locations:* Zeleznic Rail Station, The Court of Miracles, The Threshold, Old Slavers’ Market, The Underworld Inn, The End of the Line.
4. **The Arena District:** Middle-class. Bragging military, rioting sports fans, dead bodies, blood, a summoned demon.
   *   *Locations:* The Krev Arena, Mercenary Guild, The Demonborn Inn, Hell’s Bazaar, Duelists’ Plaza, The Crematorium.

   *   *Locations:* The Docks, Alzbeth’s Clock Tower, The Flesh Palace, Black Kings Bridge, Vylet’s Lodging House, The Elvish Ghetto.

   *   *Locations:* The Silver Gardens, Leto Hunting Pavilion, The Wild Stones, Exotic Botanical Garden, The Sun Canals, The Ruins.

*(Center District)* **The Hill:** Rich. Colourful houses, eerie, muffled sounds, arcane amenities (magic lights, void sewers), neutral ground.

### 3. Map Description for Play
The map is constructed in a "honeycomb" or "hive" pattern. Imagine a large central hexagon representing the heart of the city, surrounded by six larger hexagons that interlock around it like petals on a flower.

*   **The Hub:** The center hexagon is "The Hill." It is the neutral ground and the central power point of the city.
*   **The Satellites:** Surrounding the center are six numbered hexagons, each representing a distinct district. They are arranged clockwise starting from the top:
    *   **Hex 1 (Top Left):** The University District.
    *   **Hex 2 (Top Right):** The Tribunal District.
    *   **Hex 3 (Middle Right):** The Rail District.
    *   **Hex 4 (Bottom Right):** The Arena District.
    *   **Hex 5 (Bottom Left):** The Docks.
    *   **Hex 6 (Middle Left):** The Park.

When playing, you use the grid to visualize the physical location of a conflict. If a faction in District 1 makes a move, they are acting within that "petal." If they make a move against the center, they are threatening "The Hill." Because they all touch the central hexagon, the movement between the districts and the seat of power is direct and spatially intuitive.
