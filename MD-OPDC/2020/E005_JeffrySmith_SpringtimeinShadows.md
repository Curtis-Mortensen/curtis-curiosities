### 2. Transcription of Text

**Jail-** 2-story building. Barred windows on the 1st floor. Locked windows on the 2nd floor. Main entrance on the N side. 2 guards patrol the perimeter, making the circle every 5 minutes. The interior is an open courtyard with cells facing inward on the E, S, and W. 4 cells per side, 12 cells total. Contains d12 other prisoners (criminals). Roll d12 for the location of the target (clockwise, starting top-right). Guards at the jail check-in every 15 minutes. When they miss a check-in, the alarm is raised*, and all guards are alerted to the escape and become auto-aggressive. The party has 15 minutes to get in and out before reinforcements arrive (6 guards every 10 minutes).

**Transport Boat-** If the party fails to jailbreak the target or the target is recaptured and taken on the boat**, the mission fails, and the party must make it to the safehouse to survive.

**Start time: Sunset**

**Safehouse-** Entering the safehouse undetected means success!

**Posted Guards-** 2 guards posted. If no alarm is raised, they are merely suspicious. If the alarm is raised, they are aggressive, attack, and try to recapture the target and kill anyone in the way.

**Mayor's Quarters-** The Mayor has the hard evidence against the Mad King; the target insists upon its retrieval.

**Catacombs-** 4 access points form the perimeter of the catacombs, which follow the street layout. At any turn, roll 1d4 for an encounter: 1-Giant Rat, 2-Angry Spirit, 3-locked gate, 4-nothing. The SW access point exits on the outside of the city wall, 5 feet above the waterway.

**Treasury-** 2-story building. Guard patrol follows the same rules as the Jail. Locked windows on the 2nd floor. Inside: 2d100 gold.

**Armory-** No patrol. Inside: 1d100 arrows, 1d4 Alchemist Fire, 1d4 Healing Potions.

***Raised alarm-** Active guard patrols in the city. 1d4 at every turn or every 2 blocks: 1-2 no guards, 3-4 encounter patrol: 2 guards.

***If jailbreak fails or target is recaptured**, guards will take the target to the transport boat by the most direct route.
