**ETINA’S LABYRINTH**
*An adventure by Dylan Barker*

The followers of Etina, the God of insight, would take a once-in-a-lifetime pilgrimage to walk Etina’s Labyrinth. It was said that walking the path of the labyrinth was both a privilege and a demanding endeavor. Worshipers of Etina were often wandering soothsayers, able to foresee future events and have insight into secrets. Their religious order was purged after a warring royal deemed them a dangerous cult. Etina's ways have all but been forgotten and her labyrinth has fallen into neglect. Bandits have moved in and made it their home.

**PLOT HOOKS**
*   **Greta** has sworn to honour the memory of her grandfather, a follower of Etina, by clearing out the bandits. She doesn’t have much to offer, but she does have a map of the labyrinth.
*   **Etina** speaks directly to one of the adventurers in a vision asking them to clear her sanctuary and reconsecrate it.
*   **Locals** talk of a scourge of bandits who are robbing the travellers on the road. They seem to be using unusually shrewd tactics.

**THE LABYRINTH**
The large stones used to build the solid walls of the labyrinth glow gently with a deep red hue. The minerals that have leached out the rocks seem to grant the denizens of the labyrinth enhanced intelligence and insight.

**THE BANDIT HIDEOUT**

**ETINA’S PATH**
The pilgrimage taken by Etina’s acolytes was one of deep personal reflection. It was said that Etina would grant great insight and revelation to those who stayed on the labyrinth's path. The labyrinth was a test of faith. 

Along the winding path, stone dais’ have been built at each corner to encourage the acolytes and warn them of the sacrifice faith requires. 
*   **(2a)** The first dais has an intricately carved pedestal with a large open eye on top. Faded inscriptions warn pilgrims that only those who stay on the path will receive the blessing of Etina. 
*   **(2b)** The second dais features a low-walled well (1ft diameter). Bandits use it for trash, but an inscription states pilgrims must give a personally valuable gift to receive insight. 
*   **(2c)** This dais is in ruins.

**THE LONG WALK**
*   **(2d)** Rubbish piles indicate the bandits have not ventured further than this point. The inscription warns pilgrims to prepare for the ‘long walk.’ 
*   **(2e–2h)** As the adventurers walk the outer ring, time warps; mental focus becomes difficult, conversations stretch like whale song, and visions of their own past occur. The perceptive may notice the webs of a giant spider (2f) with a pulsating, pustule-filled abdomen. 
*   **(2g)** At the end of the outer ring, a dais holds a shallow basin of healing liquid; an inscription reads: ‘Wash away your past and walk on into the future.’ 
*   **(2h)** As the air grows moist and stalagmites appear, a malnourished roper hides among the stone growths. 
*   **(2i)** Dripping water has eroded this dais; the remaining carvings task pilgrims with bringing Etina’s insight to the world. 
*   **(2j)** The penultimate corner reveals an altar where the party’s previously sacrificed items lie glowing. 
*   **(2m)** A perfectly transparent gelatinous cube hangs in the corridor. Etina will blast away the rocks sealing the exit once the sanctuary is cleared.
