**FEAST HALL OF THE IREIFYUG**
*OSR dungeon for characters levels 1-3*

**Entrances**
*   **Entrance 1:** At the bottom of a valley, a trail of blood leads to an entrance stone complex obscured by a landslide.
*   **Entrance 2:** Upon a hill, overlooking the stone complex, is a fissure at the base of which a tunnel can be seen.

**Room Keys**
*   **Room 1:** Along the southern wall is a pile of 12 corpses of men and a horse. Along the eastern wall is a pile of five corpses of men. Upon the wall, graffiti of blood reads "Here we will feast." Along the western wall is a wide puddle of blood containing two sacks of silver and a purse holding 7 small emeralds (50s each). Two Bagmen are in the process of butchering a horse in the center of the room.
*   **Room 2:** Bloodied footprints lead to the northern door as well as the descending staircase. Upon the western wall, above a small altar, stands a mosaic depicting a four-armed demon holding knives standing over a dying figure. The stairs lead down to Room 16.
*   **Room 3:** Smoke and heat rise from floor gaps. Hanging from the ceiling are chains ending in hooks; one is jammed into a skeleton's rib cage. A hungry ghost of the man is hidden amid the smoke.
*   **Room 4:** A trio of stone spearmen statues stand near the eastern wall, eyes empty and faces marred. A Bagman feebly attempts to open the southern door.
*   **Room 5:** Eastern wall has an opened sarcophagus. A corpse of a Bagman is slumped against the northern wall, throat slit, holding a cursed, ash-covered sword (-1 to hit; cannot be unhanded once wielded; forces save vs. slitting own throat).
*   **Room 6:** Center chamber has a massive burning fire illuminating a 20' radius. Dozen pairs of debris-covered tables (20'x5') and broken chairs fill the room. Hidden in the southeastern shadow, 9 chicken-men prostrate themselves before an animated Giant Cauldron that slams its limbs into its belly, beating a song. Southwestern passage leads to stairs down to Room 9.
*   **Room 7:** Northern wall has six small cages (two contain roosters). 11 chicken-men squat in a circle over fighting roosters. Southern wall features an altar with a mosaic of a four-armed demon throwing bolts of fire. Upon the altar is a small silver statue (50s) of a spider clutching a triangular diamond (1000s).
*   **Room 8:** Western wall is broken; a tunnel leads to the fissure at the base of the hill.
*   **Room 9:** Two stone tables covered by broken knives. Trio of skeletons bear hundreds of cut marks. Stairs up to the west of Room 6.
*   **Room 10:** Eastern wall contains a 10' bronze dragon statue. It breathes fire every minute onto a red-hot iron slab. Smoke exits via ceiling holes. The dragon's belly is a secret door to Room 11.
*   **Room 11:** Northern wall has a 5' circle of salt and wax with protective sigils. Trapped inside is a hunched flame demon pumping a mechanism. In the SE corner are three golden idols (300s each) shaped like the demon; they can be taken without breaking the seal.
*   **Room 12:** Empty 10'x70' basin in the center. Western wall depicts a four-armed demon butchering a giant bird. Northern wall has dozens of nests and 14 chicken-men. Eastern door covered in frost.
*   **Room 13:** Three halves of aurochs hang from hooks. Southern wall has an empty 5'x15' aquarium. A trail of ice leads to the meat where a giant crab [3HD, armor as chain, 1d8 x 2 claws, morale 8] snips at it, freezing the meat.
*   **Room 14:** Corpses of 8 men, 2 horses, and a bear hang on rusted iron hooks.
*   **Room 15:** Smells of burning flesh. Large stone sarcophagus in center contains a burning inanimate skeleton holding a vorpal sword [forces save vs death on hit]. Buried alongside are 500 silver coins.
*   **Room 16:** Eastern wall has a mosaic of a butchered bull. NE and SE corners have statues of bull-headed men pulling out their own intestines. 6 Bagmen search for the northern door. Stairs up to Room 2.
*   **Room 17:** Stinks of spoiled wine. Walls covered in alcoves with broken bottles. 7 intact bottles (70s each).
*   **Room 18:** Northern wall has a tapestry of fishermen under a "Meat Without Feet" banner. Viewers are drawn into a busy port in the tapestry. Fishermen offer rare fish for 1d4*50s per HD of fish. They purchase "meat without feet" for 100 silver/HD. Eating the fish turns the eater into that fish for 2d6 turns. Western wall has barrel of spices worth 1200s.
*   **Room 19:** Giant squid chained to the northern wall. 16 barrels of cheese.

### Map Description for Non-Visual Players

The dungeon is presented as a subterranean structure on two levels. 

**Level 1 (Rooms 1-8):** 
The floor plan is a rough "L" shape oriented toward the top-left of the page. You enter from the left side. 
*   **Entrance 1** leads into the long, vertical **Room 1**. 
*   From **Room 1**, you can move north into **Room 2** (which features a staircase down to Level 2, Room 16). 
*   **Room 2** connects to a hallway that leads eastward into **Room 3**, then further east into **Room 4**. 
*   A corridor from **Room 4** leads north to **Room 5**. 
*   To the right, a large central area contains **Room 6**, a massive chamber that acts as the hub. 
*   To the north of the hub is **Room 7** and **Room 8**. 
*   The exit for **Entrance 2** is at the far western wall of **Room 8**.

**Level 2 (Rooms 9-19):**
This floor is a rectangular complex located beneath the first level.
*   It is accessed via stairs in **Room 16** (from Room 2 above) and stairs in **Room 9** (from Room 6 above).
*   **Room 9** acts as the western entry point. 
*   To the east of **Room 9** are **Room 10** and **Room 11** (connected by a secret door). 
*   Further east is the long corridor of **Room 12**, connecting to **Room 13** and **Room 14**. 
*   To the south of these are **Room 15** and **Room 16** (the central hub for the lower level). 
*   To the east of the hub are **Room 17**, **Room 18**, and **Room 19**. 

In essence, you have two interconnected loops: one starting in Room 1 (Level 1) moving clockwise toward Room 6, and a second on Level 2 starting from Room 9 or 16, branching out into a series of interconnected chambers forming the remainder of the complex.
