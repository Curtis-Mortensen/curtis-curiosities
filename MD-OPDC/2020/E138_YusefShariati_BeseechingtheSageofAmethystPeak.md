#### Introduction & Hook
**Beseeching the Sage of Amethyst Peak**
Prince Haldir, the heir-apparent, is dying from the Poisoned Arrow which struck him during battle with the barbarian tribes. Even the holiest of the King’s men could not heal him. The only hope lies in beseeching Wise Sage Thesuvotai, the exiled brother of the King. You were not the Queen’s first choice for emissary. However, Sir Orion Jing—bearer of the Queen’s letter to the Sage—has gone missing. The Prince has but three days left to live. The Queen begs you now: Find Jing, retrieve the Letter, and deliver it to the Sage of the Mountain. Any reward you desire shall then be yours.

**The Queen’s Letter:** Begs Thesuvotai to divine the name of the archer and so save her son the Prince. She invokes their secret love & infidelity from long ago. Reveals the Prince is actually the Sage’s son!

**The Poisoned Arrow:** Sticks fast into the flesh and burrows towards the heart, killing the victim in 7 days. Irremovable without saying the name of the archer who loosed it. In this case: Princess Safiya, the Prince’s sister!

#### Encounter Tables
**d8 Penitents, Pilgrims, and Other Folk of the Road**
1. Ignatius the blind mendicant sells tawdry little tin-amalgam mirrors “for luck.” (7-hour bane when broken) Won’t donate? He throws one at you.
2. Eiko the seamstress is cursed: her hair has turned into white silk. Salmonius the venal merchant wrings his hands, plotting to enslave her.
3. Nandini, spy for the Cult of Banyan. Poses as pious pilgrim. Chatty, tries to determine richest victims/travelers. Hidden bandits await her signal.
4. Family of four carry grandmother on litter to abandon in the mountains.
5. Laughing dancing monk preaches nonattachment, throws pearls like confetti. Winded fat jewel merchant arrives 5 min later yelling “Thief!”
6. Hu Pan, the Worm-Priest, inches along the ground in ragged puce robes. Grabs at legs of any he encounters, humbly requests a beating. The first to comply is blessed with a worm’s regeneration for one day.
7. Procession of eight flagellants make for the Shrine. One, Tuc Dindev, is a fugitive (killed the man who cuckolded him) with a price on his head.
8. A mile-long funeral procession fills the road: 1,000 hired mourners wail, beat their breasts, soil their faces, and rend their black & yellow garments. A solid gold beehive houses the sacred queen bee’s body.

**So you went off the path...roll 1d4+Altitude Index**

*   **The Sword “Mille-feuille”:** Cuts anything, ignores armor.
*   **The Fairy Crocus “Kúm-kúm”:** Purple petals. Has three saffron stigmas (regrows 1/day); eating one effects Protection from Evil until dawn.

### Part 3: Map Description for Gameplay

The map is a vertical track starting at the base (5,000 ft) and ending at the summit (20,000 ft). Navigation is dictated by the **Altitude Index** on the right and **Time** markers on the lines connecting locations.

*   **The Navigation Mechanic:** The "Road" (red dashed line) is the safe route. Leaving the road for the "Wilds" (dotted red line) triggers the "Off the Path" encounter table.
*   **Key Locations on the Path:**
    *   **Start/Bottom (5,000 ft):** The journey begins here.
    *   **Grotto of Alms:** Located near the bottom. An enchanted pool that transmutes metal into organic matter (gold to goldfish). Guarded by a giant anglerfish.
    *   **Bandit Cult Hideout:** Located near the bottom in a fig grove. This is where Sir Orion Jing is found, depressed and selling his armor. King Nanoc (the bandit leader) has the Queen's Letter on his desk.
    *   **Herbal Vista:** Found mid-way. A patch of Yarrow used for healing.
    *   **Bridge of Humility:** Requires passing Yseba, the arrogant swordsman. You must duel him or kiss his feet to pass. He wields the *Mille-feuille* sword.
    *   **Heavenly Ascent:** An armored elevator operated by Frank the Ogre Monk. He only permits the poor (the "unburdened") to rise.
    *   **Cliffs of Ecstasy:** Located at a higher altitude. Features alabaster idols and giant tarantula spiders.
    *   **Shrine of Atonement:** Home to barber-priests who purge disease and sins with nasty laxatives.
    *   **Poppy Valley:** A danger zone requiring a saving throw or the party falls asleep for 3d6 hours.
    *   **Sage’s Yurt:** The home of the Sage, currently guarded by the Fairy Crocus (the flower has its own personality/spirit).
    *   **Crystal Pass:** The final gate. An Amethyst Monk guards the way. To pass, you must answer his riddle: *"What runs up and down the mountain yet never moves?"* (Answer: The Road).
    *   **The Summit (20,000 ft):** The Sage resides here, slowly turning into amethyst, refusing to engage with the world until the Queen's Letter is presented.

**DM Tip:** Keep track of the "3 days" time limit mentioned in the intro. Use the hour markers (e.g., "1.0 hr," "2.5 hr") on the lines between locations to simulate travel time and pressure the players.
