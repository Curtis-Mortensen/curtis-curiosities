**Header:**
BY CHERYN RAPP AND BRANDON DINGESS
*A dusty tome is pulled from a neglected shop shelf. A blinding light radiates from its pages. The party vanishes as the Book clatters to the ground.*

**Title:** EX LIBRIS PENDYL

**The Maze:**
The party awakens in the center of a maze. As they advance they find numbered scraps of parchment with strange warnings or riddles. After they emerge, they are able to look back and see that the maze is actually a large, ornate letter P.

**You Are Here:**
As the party realizes this, the voice of the Book speaks. It was once the property of the scholar Pendyl, who imbued the Book with consciousness. After Pendyl’s death the Book languished with no purpose. Lonely, the Book wishes to help those worthy of the knowledge it contains. The Book explains that they must travel along the illuminated page through a series of encounters, then falls silent. If the party can survive the tale, they can help the Book escape by allowing its consciousness to imbue items of their own.

**Blackletter Forest:**
If the party strays from the path, they find themselves wandering through a thick forest of text. Within is Titivillus, a scribal demon who encourages procrastination and laziness. He will consistently encourage the party to stay and relax, even offering them (cursed) items in return. Attempting to leave causes Titivillus to attack. After escaping, they notice a disembodied Hand pointing back to the path.

**A Warning:**
Upon exiting the base of the illuminated P, the party comes upon a grid of strange floating letterforms. Beyond is a locked door with two keyholes, both of which must be unlocked to proceed.

**An Escape:**
Ink, quills and parchment rest on a table before a large door. Carved into the table’s surface is the following:
*Turn the page; open the door*
*Back to the life you knew before.*
*Armed anew; with knowledge as key*
*Use what’s at hand to set us all free.*
Ideally the players should use the writing implements to draw themselves escaping with the Book. The Book cheers after the final puzzle is solved. Now its consciousness can escape with the party if they’d be so kind as to allow its chapters to inhabit one or more of their items. The Book is wise and knowledgeable so any bonus from the now-magical item should reflect that. The party reappears in the shop as the proprietor admonishes them to quiet down and reminds them that if they break it, they’ve bought it.

**The Ascenders:**
Shortly after returning to the path, the group comes to a wall. A basket large enough to hold the party sits at its base. The basket is rigged to a rope-and-pulley system and it looks like it’s a long haul to the top of the wall. The rope and pulley must be operated manually from the tower at the top of the wall.

**Monstrous Marginalia:**
*   **Titivillus:** See Blackletter Forest.
*   **Harpy:** A terrible screech pierces the air as the basket rises up the wall. A winged Harpy dives down toward the party sporting twin daggers in each hand. The Harpy sets about trying to cut the rope and the group must fight it off while continuing to move upward.
*   **Knight and Snail:** The party comes across a noble chevalier battling a fierce, slathering Snail. The Knight’s armor and the Snail’s shell are damaged, indicating a long battle. If the party tries to intervene on behalf of either combatant, both will turn and attack the party.

**Sidebar/Puzzle Box:**
*   **How to Solve:** Three words are deconstructed within this chart according to a pattern. Find and identify the keyword to unlock both locks on the door.
*   **Grid:** 
    w t k l
    e n h a
    t i i a
    r h n c
    h k t n
*   **Hidden words:** watch, learn, think, think
*   **Keyword:** think
*   **To open door:** insert keyword into both keyholes. ‘think twice’

**Path Labels:**
I. Stay the path | II. Heed advice | III. Choose your battles wisely | IV. Beware distractions | V. Persist through adversity | VI. Begin anew

**GM’s Note:** You may treat the red trail that runs counterclockwise around the page as the ‘path’ the party must travel. Suggested order of encounters is also described counterclockwise along this page, beginning with The Maze and ending with An Escape.

### 3. Playable Map Description
To play this dungeon, imagine you are moving through a literal, gigantic manuscript. 

*   **The Structure:** The entire map is contained within the shape of a giant, decorative letter **"P."** 
*   **The Path:** Your party must follow a thin red line that winds counterclockwise. 
    *   **Start:** You begin in the center hole of the "P" (The Maze).
    *   **The Forest:** As you exit the maze, you travel along the left side of the vertical stem of the "P," which represents the "Blackletter Forest." If you veer off the red line here, you risk being waylaid by Titivillus the demon.
    *   **The Wall:** Continuing up the stem of the "P," you reach a wall. You must enter a basket to be hoisted up. This is where you encounter the **Harpy**—you are suspended in mid-air, defending the rope while climbing.
    *   **The Knight and Snail:** Along the path, you will see a side-encounter featuring a Knight fighting a giant Snail. It is best to avoid them; the encounter is a trap.
    *   **The Goal:** The red line leads to the bottom tip/base of the "P," where you find the door with two keyholes.
*   **The Final Lock:** You must input the word "THINK" into both keyholes (the clue "think twice" refers to the word "think" used for the two locks). 
*   **The Hand:** Be careful—there is a disembodied hand that will physically lift you and toss you back into the "Blackletter Forest" if you are not careful, forcing you to restart part of your journey.
