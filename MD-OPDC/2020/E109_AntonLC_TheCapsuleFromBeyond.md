### Map Description for Gameplay
To visualize the dungeon for gameplay, imagine you are standing in a central **Control Room** shaped like a triangle.

*   **The Interface:** The room is essentially a computer interface. It has three distinct "levers" or "panels." Each panel represents a binary toggle:
    *   **Toggle 1:** Width (Narrow or Wide)
    *   **Toggle 2:** Power/Altitude (Low or High)
    *   **Toggle 3:** Temperature (Cold or Warm)
*   **How it works:** Think of this like a combination lock. To change the state of the capsule, you must place fuel rods into the slots on these panels and press the start button. The number of rods you put in determines if the toggle flips to "0" (Narrow/Low/Cold) or "1" (Wide/High/Warm).
*   **The Result:** The room you are standing in "shifts" or "morphs" into the room described by the table based on the configuration of those three toggles. You are effectively shifting your reality to match one of the eight locations listed in the table.
*   **Resource Management:** You start with 57 fuel rods. You must manage your "Heat" and "Power" levels, which are displayed on the room's walls (the "Displays"). You are constantly trying to balance the inputs and outputs (e.g., repairing things to lower heat, sacrificing rods or victims to increase power) to achieve your goals, such as opening the outer doors. 

In short: You are in a "teleporting" square room. By changing the settings in your control room, you make the door lead to different realities.
