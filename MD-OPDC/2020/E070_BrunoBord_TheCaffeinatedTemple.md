### Transcription

**The Caffeinated Temple**
*a dungeon for coffee (or tea) drinkers*

**General Rules**
*   Links are corridors.
*   Double links are trapped corridors.
*   “O’s” are entrances.

**Rooms & Corridors**
“N” Rooms contain regular furnitures, like chairs and simple beds, usual items, altars and prayer books, nothing really valuable.
When the PCs enter or leave a room to walk a corridor, by default, they have 1 in 6 chance to make an encounter. Each time they’re entering a “N” Room for the first time, this chance is increased by 1. For each encounter, roll d6 on the table below.

**Treasures**
There’s a chest in each CH3 room. The ones close to the entrances contain minor coffee-related artifacts (silver pots & spoons, cups). 
The furthest CH3 room is the Grand Caffeine Priest apartment. There’s a 2 in 6 chance that the Grand Priest is studying here, along with other cultists. 
The room contains a large chest, trapped with a *Sleep* spell, which contains the golden coffee ritual items: pots and spoon, and a very large quantity of roasted coffee beans. Consider these as major treasures. 
The Grand Caffeine Priest owns an enchanted cup. If you drink coffee from this cup, you’ll be unable to sleep for 7 days in a row. 
You’ll also find his clothes and robes, and a collection of 1d6 parchments with a minor value.

**NPCs**
*   **Cultists (lvl 1 Priests):** Can cast the *Sleep* spell. When they’re suffering from withdrawal, they’re irritated, but will be treated as tired. When they’re overdosed, they’ll directly aggress any character in their line of sight. When the 7 days effect of the ritual cup has vanished, cultists simply pass out. They need at least a regular cup of coffee to be waken up properly. 
*   **Caffeine Priests (lvl 2):** Can cast *Sleep* & *Continual Flame* spells. 
*   **Grand Caffeine Priest (lvl 5):** Can cast *Sleep*, *Continual Flame* & *Revivify*. He carries a scroll with the *Raise Dead* spell on it (1 use), but will only use it to raise a Priest from the dead.

**Traps (d4)**
1. Coffee beans are ejected at high speed through small holes.
2. Boiling stream of a black substance springs from the roof and burns the victims.
3. Soporific gas are vaporized from several openings; having enough coffee in your body is enough to keep you awake.
4. Very large pit containing coffee beans is acting like quicksand.

**Encounters (d6)**
1. Coffee plant growing, their branches & roots will try to grapple the limbs of the PCs.
2. Caffeine Cultists (1-4) neutral.
3. Caffeine Cultists (1-3) withdrawal symptoms.
4. Caffeine Cultist (1) overdosed.
5. Caffeine Cultist (1) passed out.
6. Caffeine Priests (1-3).

### Map Description for Gameplay
The map is a non-traditional, node-based network laid out as a chemical diagram of a caffeine molecule. To play this, you must treat the atoms as "rooms" and the bonds as "hallways." 

**The Layout:**
*   **The Structure:** The dungeon consists of two fused rings (a hexagon and a pentagon) with several branches extending outward. 
*   **Nodes (Rooms):**
    *   **"N" Rooms:** There are four Nitrogen atoms in the structure labeled "N." These are your standard rooms containing furniture and minor items. 
    *   **"CH3" Rooms:** There are three specific branches labeled "CH3" that terminate at a terminal carbon group. These are the "treasure rooms" mentioned in the text. The one furthest from the entrance ("O" nodes) is the Grand Priest's apartment.
    *   **"O" (Entrances):** The Oxygen atoms located on the perimeter of the structure serve as the starting entry points for the players.
*   **Edges (Corridors):**
    *   **Paths:** The lines connecting the atoms are the corridors.
    *   **Traps:** Look for the double lines (double bonds) in the chemical structure. These specific corridors are trapped. You should assign one of the 4 traps from the "Traps" table to these double-link corridors.

**Navigating:**
When players move from an atom to an adjacent atom, they are traveling along a corridor. When they move across a double bond, they encounter a trap. Because it is a molecule, some paths will lead into loops (circular rooms) while the "CH3" branches act as cul-de-sacs (dead ends) where the loot is hidden. If you are drawing this out for players, simply sketch the chemical diagram and place a dot at every letter (N, O, CH3) to serve as your room keys.
