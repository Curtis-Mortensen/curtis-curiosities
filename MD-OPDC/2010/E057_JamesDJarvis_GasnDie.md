## Transcription

This one-page dungeon, titled **"Gas-n-Die,"** is a classic, stripped-back tabletop RPG module designed by James D. Jarvis. The layout is clean and minimalist, presented in high-contrast black-and-white. It features a technical line-drawing map on the left and organized text descriptions on the right. The aesthetic is inspired by post-apocalyptic road-trip tropes.

### Transcribed Text

**Title:** Gas -n- Die
**Author:** A One Page Dungeon by James D. Jarvis

**Random Encounter Table**
1. Mutant Bikers (2-5), armed with bowie-knives and chains; 20% chance one has a pistol
2. Cannibals (2-7), armed with clubs, knives and throwing nets
3. Rabid Coyote
4. Carnivorous Cactus
5. Radioactive Alley Cat
6. Unfortunate Road Runner, explodes on impact

**Location Descriptions**
*   **Gas Pumps:** The four pumps are without power. It’s likely these pumps have been sucked dry but that shouldn’t stop adventurers from trying all they can to milk out a few last drops.
*   **Convenience Store:** This tiny store front has been searched time and again but if someone is really stubborn they may just find a pack of chocolate bubble gum (infested with spider eggs).
*   **Front Office:** Smashed cash registers and dozens of empty cigarette cartons litter the floor along with hundreds of used Instant-Loto scratch tickets. There is a usable pair of cheap sun glasses still hanging on a wall display.
*   **Cooler:** Inside this otherwise empty cooler is a Combat Android scavenging the last of the refrigeration unit’s vital coolant.
*   **Back Office:** This office is locked shut from the inside. There is a dried up old corpse holding an ancient shotgun sitting in a chair next to a small coffee can fire place. Many empty cans of spam are piled in and about the wastebasket.
*   **Supply Room:** Everything of value has been stripped from this room long ago.
*   **Diner:** Someone barricaded the convenience store door and the outside door with the diner’s fixtures and furniture. The records in the ancient juke-box (propped up along with fixtures barricading the front door) are still mostly usable.
*   **Men’s Room:** Other then the dried chewing tobacco and chili on the walls there doesn’t seem to be much of interest here. Among the graffiti in the toilet stall one can find the message “ENTRY CODE 4312”.
*   **Women’s Room:** If one breaks down the locked door the Zombie within will most certainly attack.
*   **Kitchen:** This room is an awful mess but there might still be some usable knives or other such implements lost in the chaos. 100 Piranha-Roaches dwell under the sink.

### Map Description
For someone who cannot see the map, imagine a small, single-story roadside building complex oriented at an angle. The scale is 3 feet per unit, making the rooms relatively small and claustrophobic.

**Layout Overview:**
*   **Exterior:** At the bottom (south) of the map are four gas pumps arranged in two pairs.
*   **The Building:** The main structure is an irregular rectangle with an attached section at the top right for the restrooms.
*   **The Core:**
    *   Entering from the south, you step into the **Convenience Store**.
    *   To the left (west) of the store is the **Front Office**.
    *   Tucked behind the store and office is the **Cooler**, which acts as a central access point.
    *   North of the Cooler is the **Back Office** and the **Supply Room**.
    *   Along the top wall is the **Kitchen**.
*   **The Diner & Restrooms:**
    *   The eastern portion of the building is the **Diner**.
    *   Attached to the top-right exterior of the Diner are the **Men’s Room** and **Women’s Room**.

**Key Symbols/Navigation:**
*   **Doors:** Marked as small rectangular "gaps" in the walls with a line perpendicular to the wall. The **Back Office** and **Women’s Room** are specifically marked with distinct door symbols.
*   **Counters:** Indicated by thick, black lines within rooms (notably in the Convenience Store and the Diner).
*   **Windows:** Indicated by rectangles with a line running through the center, found primarily along the front of the Convenience Store and the Diner.
*   **Flow:** The map is designed with "choke points." For example, the Diner is barricaded, forcing players to navigate through the store or potentially break through windows. The internal layout is cramped, with most rooms being roughly 6–10 feet wide.
