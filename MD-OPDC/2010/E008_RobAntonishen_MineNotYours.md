### Transcription of Written Text

**Title:** Mine! Not Yours!
*Licensed under Creative Commons Attribution-Share Alike 3.0.*

**Map 'A' (The Surface):** A small mining settlement on a cliff overlooking the ocean. There are two larger houses and a mine works.
*   **A1:** A rough path runs around a mine shaft. The shaft goes deep; the bottom is not visible. The hoist is broken. (Shaft is 80 ft. deep).
*   **A2:** A large house, once housing several families, now completely trashed. No value inside.
*   **A3:** A house in better condition housing two hiding miners (manager and accountant). They tell of a collapse that revealed a new passage, leading to lizard-folk raids. They offer a reward to clear the mine.
*   **A4:** A metal ladder attached to the cliff leads to a mine entrance accessible only at low tide. (If waiting for low tide, 1d4+2 lizard-folk with spears will climb out of A1 to attack).

**Map 'B' (The Underground):** All mine tunnels are 6 ft. high. Extinguished oil lamps every 20 ft. contain 1d4-1 hours of oil.
*   **B1:** Bottom of the mine shaft with tracks for carts. (If not waiting for low tide, 1d4+2 lizard-folk with spears are here). Four tunnels exit: West, South, Southeast, and Northeast. The two southern tunnels slope downward.
*   **B2:** A mined-out room with a tipped mining cart. 6 lizard-folk are torturing a miner. If saved, he reveals a submerged tunnel to B8 where his friend hid gems.
*   **B3:** A natural cavern to the NW with a steep, rough tunnel leading north. Requires a Dexterity check to traverse.
*   **B4:** A tunnel to a cliff exit/ladder. Flooded at high tide; slippery with slime at low tide.
*   **B5:** A tunnel to a cliff exit with tracks. Flooded at high tide; slippery at low tide.
*   **B6:** Four-way intersection. North, West, and East tunnels. Southern tunnel is caved in. 2 lizard-folk guard the east path and will run for reinforcements.
*   **B7:** Low ceiling. Floor and ceiling slope into murky water. Mining gear sits at the edge. (Requires info from the miner in B2 to find the underwater passage, which is 6 ft. deep).
*   **B8:** Slopes out of the water to the east. A phantom fungus hides here. A corpse has a purse with 10 small diamonds and a large uncut ruby.
*   **B9:** A huge cavern with a 50 ft. ceiling. A large salt-water lake takes up most of the space. 20 lizard-folk are on the east shore with bows. A kraken lurks in the lake and will attack only after all lizard-folk are defeated or if the escape tunnel is blocked.
*   **B10:** Entrance blocked by boulders. Requires a Strength check. Contains the captured miners and families. One child will give a bottle of "soup" (a minor healing potion that also adds +1 to melee damage for 1d4+3 rounds).
*   **B11:** Staging area with poor-quality weapons. A tunnel leads up to a hidden cliff entrance.
*   **B12-B14:** Living quarters. B12 has 10 lizard-folk (rusty short swords); B13 has 5 lizard-folk (long swords) and a shaman; B14 contains the chieftain with a +5 masterwork great sword and an amulet of protection. The room contains various treasures.

### Map Description for Gameplay (Accessibility Guide)

For a player who cannot see the maps, here is the spatial layout:

**Map A (Surface):**
*   **Orientation:** The map is oriented with the ocean on the left (west side).
*   **Layout:** The cliff runs vertically. On the right, there is a grassy area with two houses. A1 is the mine shaft, situated in the center. A2 is the northernmost house, and A3 is the house closest to the shaft. A thin line represents a path connecting the buildings to the shaft. A small icon on the far left cliff edge denotes the metal ladder at A4.
*   **Scale:** Each square is 10 feet.

**Map B (Underground Mine):**
*   **Overview:** This is a branching network of interconnected tunnels and cavern chambers. The main entrance, B1, is a central hub.
*   **Branching Paths:**
    *   **The Southern Cluster (B4/B5):** These two tunnels act as side-exits leading to the cliff face. They are tight and prone to flooding.
    *   **The Western/Northern Cluster (B2/B3):** West of the hub, you find the combat area (B2) and the hazardous natural tunnel (B3) leading to the northern section.
    *   **The Eastern/Flooded Cluster (B6/B7/B8):** This is a critical tactical area. B6 acts as a choke point for reinforcements. B7 and B8 form a loop involving the underground lake's edge, requiring players to submerge to move between rooms.
    *   **The Main Cavern (B9):** A large, irregularly shaped chamber dominating the map, filled mostly by a lake. The shore is at the far east.
    *   **The Living Quarters (B10-B14):** Situated in the southeastern corner of the underground map. This area is more structured, representing rooms where the lizard-folk live. B14 is the final room at the end of the line.
*   **Navigation:** Remind players that the mine is dark, cramped (6 ft. ceilings), and partially flooded. Use the grid (1 square = 10 ft) to track movement, keeping in mind that some areas (like B3) require skill checks for movement, while others (B9) are primarily aquatic environments.
