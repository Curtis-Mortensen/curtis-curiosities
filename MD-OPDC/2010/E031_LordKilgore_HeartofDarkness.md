### Description of the Map
For someone unable to see the map, it is an **isometric-style hexagonal grid** representing a wilderness region.

*   **The River:** The defining feature is a large, winding river that flows diagonally across the map from the top-right toward the bottom-left. It acts as the primary thoroughfare for travel.

*   **Terrain Types:** The map uses standardized symbols within the hexes to denote terrain:

*   **Layout:**
    *   The "Start" point (0118) is located in the bottom-left corner, implying that the party begins at the base of the river and travels "upriver" (northeast) into the heart of the continent.
    *   As you move from the bottom-left to the top-right, the terrain transitions from generic forests into increasingly hostile zones like swamps, volcanic ash zones, and mountain ranges.
    *   **Scale:** Each individual hexagon represents 15 miles of travel. This is crucial for tracking the "days" of the journey, as dictated by the river travel rules (10 miles/day upstream).

**Gameplay Implementation:**
To play, you would present the player with the current hex coordinate they are in. When they move into a new hex, you consult the corresponding number in the text list to determine what they encounter (e.g., "You have entered hex 0312, where a man raised by apes confronts you"). The river provides a clear, linear progression path, but the surrounding hexes allow for "side-quests" or exploration off the main water path.
