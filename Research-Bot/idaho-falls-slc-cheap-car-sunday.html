1. What This Document Is

This report answers one traveler question: What are the cheapest realistic options to get from Idaho Falls to Salt Lake City on Sunday, especially if a car is preferred, and how do Allegiant flight partnerships, Turo, coupons, and bundles change the math?

It is written for a general audience. You do not need prior knowledge of this repository. Prices move constantly; treat every dollar figure as a snapshot or published range, then re-quote before you book.[1]

Bottom line up front: If you only need to arrive in SLC and do not need a car once there, Salt Lake Express from Idaho Falls → SLC Airport from about $42 is usually the cheapest clean option.[2] If you truly need a one-way rental car IDA→SLC, use a traditional agency (Enterprise / Alamo / National via Allegiant or Costco Travel when eligible) and expect the one-way drop fee to dominate the price — often tens to low hundreds of dollars on top of the daily rate.[3][4] Turo is a poor fit for true one-way IDA→SLC under current Turo rules.[5]

2. Limitations of This Research

2.1 Theoretical / market limitations

Car rental and peer-to-peer prices are dynamic inventory markets. A route that is cheap on Tuesday can be expensive on Sunday because fleets must be rebalanced after weekend demand.[6] Published “from $X/day” pages often mix round-trip and one-way samples, omit insurance, and age quickly.

2.2 Methodology limitations

  • Live aggregator search pages (Kayak/Expedia) for exact Sunday one-way IDA→SLC quotes did not return stable, scrapable result tables during this session (bot blocking / empty result shells).[7]
  • Therefore one-way rental dollar figures lean on aggregator secondary sources (Rental24h ranges), official policy pages, and membership-discount documentation rather than a single locked booking confirmation.
  • “Sunday” is interpreted as the next Sunday after research day Friday, July 10, 2026 → July 12, 2026. If the traveler meant a different Sunday, re-run quotes.
  • Allegiant does not fly Idaho Falls → Salt Lake City; Delta does.[8] Allegiant car deals still matter if the traveler is an Allegiant passenger using partner rates at IDA or elsewhere.

2.3 Strength of evidence in this field

Consumer travel pricing research is not an academic discipline with peer-reviewed “truth.” The useful evidence base is: (1) carrier and rental-company policy pages, (2) membership program terms, (3) aggregator snapshots, and (4) traveler forums for edge cases (Turo one-ways, ride boards). Policy pages are strong for what is allowed; aggregator pages are weak for exact Sunday totals unless you complete a checkout flow.

7. Option Deep Dive: Salt Lake Express (Usually Cheapest)

Salt Lake Express BEST IF NO CAR NEEDED

From ~$42 IDA → SLC Airport · homepage popular-route price

Salt Lake Express markets Idaho Falls → SLC Airport as a core route: about 3 hours, multiple daily departures, and a published “from $42” fare on the homepage.[2] The dedicated route page also lists Idaho Falls Airport (IDA) and Salt Lake City Airport stops, which is ideal if you are connecting to a flight or airport pickup.[11]

  • Book direct at saltlakeexpress.com / their ticket store — Greyhound/FlixBus often resell the same SLE-operated trip at higher displayed fares (example listing from ~$72).[10]
  • Some SLE marketing pages show higher “as low as” figures (e.g. ~$115 on a shuttle landing page); treat those as alternate fare classes or stale SEO copy and always search your exact Sunday date.[11]
  • Company claims free changes / refundable credit up to departure on many tickets — useful for Sunday schedule risk.[2]

8. Option Deep Dive: Traditional One-Way Car Rental

Major brands serving Idaho Falls Airport one-ways toward Salt Lake City include Alamo, Avis, Budget, Dollar, Enterprise, Hertz, National, and Thrifty (availability varies by date).[3][18]

8.1 How one-way pricing works

Enterprise defines a drop charge as the fee for returning a car to a different location; it varies by location and season and is disclosed at reservation time.[1] Budget likewise allows different-city returns when selected at booking; changing later by phone can add cost.[19]

For the IDA market, Rental24h states that published one-way prices already include one-way fees, commonly in a roughly $31–$143 band, and cites an Enterprise Volkswagen Jetta sample around $31/day as a cheap IDA one-way illustration (not a locked Sunday quote).[3] Reverse-direction SLC→IDA samples average about $68/day in the same aggregator’s writeup.[18]

Practical tip: Always search with different drop-off = Salt Lake City (airport or downtown) from the first query. A cheap round-trip IDA rate (Priceline/Expedia often show ~$60–$100/day local) is not your real cost if you will abandon the car in Utah.[20]

8.2 Where to pick up

Idaho Falls Regional Airport has on-airport rental counters near baggage claim for major brands; off-airport branches sometimes price differently but add shuttle friction.[20] For one-ways, airport-to-airport (IDA→SLC) is usually the inventory path companies prefer to sell.

9. Allegiant Bundles, Coupons & Partner Rates

Allegiant partners with Alamo, Enterprise, and National for negotiated car rates and encourages bundling a car with the flight booking.[4][21] There is also a car-only Allegiant passenger deal path via Alamo’s affinity partner page.[15]

9.1 What the discount actually covers

Alamo’s Allegiant terms are explicit: the discount applies to the base rate only. Taxes, airport fees, fuel, additional-driver fees, one-way rental charges, and optional coverages (e.g. CDW) are extra. Advance reservation required; may not combine with other discounts; blackout dates may apply.[15]

9.2 Does Allegiant help this specific trip?

  • If you are flying Allegiant into Idaho Falls (LAS, AZA, and seasonal LAX/OAK/SAN/PDX): yes — add Alamo/Enterprise/National through Allegiant manage-trip / cars pages and compare the total against Costco/open web.[8]
  • If your goal is IDA→SLC by air: Allegiant does not operate that route; Delta does year-round IDA–SLC.[8] A last-minute Delta hop can beat a one-way rental if you do not need a car in SLC — but Sunday seat sales vary; check same-day.
  • Allegiant “bundles” on the airline site are mostly flight+hotel/car vacation packages and ancillary bundles (bags/seats), not a special IDA→SLC ground product.[22]

11. Option Deep Dive: Turo

Turo WEAK FOR TRUE ONE-WAY

Turo is excellent for round-trip local rentals in Idaho Falls, including airport delivery (IDA is on Turo’s US airports-with-delivery-permits list).[26][27]

It is a poor default for IDA→SLC one-way because:

  1. At booking you cannot choose different pickup and return cities.[5]
  2. After booking you may request a location change, but the new return must already be a location the host offers or within their custom delivery radius.[5]
  3. Salt Lake City is ~190–217 miles away — far outside a typical Idaho Falls host delivery radius — so hosts must specially agree, often pricing in their own recovery (flight + drive back), which destroys the “cheap” thesis.[28]
  4. Improper return / abandoning a car can trigger fees (Turo cites a $50 improper-return fee class of penalties, plus host claims).[5]

When Turo still makes sense: (a) you find a host who explicitly messages “OK to leave in SLC” with a clear all-in price; (b) you rent round-trip in Idaho Falls for local use only; (c) you use Turo in SLC after taking the bus down.

14. Brief Field History (Why This Market Looks Like This)

Airport car rental in the U.S. consolidated around a few holding companies (Enterprise Holdings: Enterprise/Alamo/National; Hertz group; Avis Budget Group). One-way “drop charges” evolved as fleet-rebalancing tools: the renter pays to move metal the company would otherwise truck or leave idle.[6]

Airlines unbundled ancillaries in the 2000s–2010s; ultra-low-cost carriers like Allegiant leaned hard into vacation packages and partner car rates as margin centers rather than operating their own fleets.[4]

Peer-to-peer platforms (Turo, Getaround) grew post-2010 as round-trip local alternatives. Cross-metro one-ways remain an awkward edge case because private hosts cannot absorb repositioning the way national fleets can.[5]

Intercity shuttles on the I-15 corridor (Salt Lake Express and predecessors) filled the gap left by thin rail and expensive short-haul flying between secondary Idaho cities and SLC.

16. Conclusion

For a cheap Sunday Idaho Falls → Salt Lake City move, do not start with Turo. Start with Salt Lake Express (~$42+) if you do not need a car in Utah. If you do need a car, run a true one-way quote on Enterprise/Alamo (Allegiant or Costco when eligible) and Budget/Hertz (AAA when eligible), knowing the drop fee—not the pretty daily rate—is the real bill. Allegiant helps on partner base rates, not by erasing one-way charges. Gas for a borrowed car (~$35) remains the theoretical floor.
