1. Aims of This Follow-Up

The parent report mapped the full seller ladder. This add-on zooms into one budget that matters for many first-time buyers: around $10,000. It answers:

  1. Which nearby lots have the deepest range and best deal density in the ~$8k–$12k band?
  2. Among cars (sedans / small hatches—not trucks) that actually trade near $10k, which models combine the best EPA MPG with realistic local prices?
  3. If you finance a $10,000 car, what are realistic monthly payments and total interest at scores 600, 650, 700, and 750, comparing credit-union vs dealer rates and 0% vs 10% down, across typical auto-loan terms?
Quick verdict: For sheer $10k selection on Redwood Road, Vectra Auto Sales (avg listing ~$8.2k) beats franchise and mid-price indie lots. For MPG-per-dollar, hunt a Prius / Prius c first, then Civic / Corolla / Versa / Fiesta. For loan math, a Utah First–style CU rate at 750 with 10% down / 60 months can leave total interest under ~$825; the same car at a dealer-typical 15.9% with 0 down / 72 months at score 600 can burn over $5,500 in interest alone.[1][2][3]

2. Limitations

  • Inventory turns daily; listings sampled 2026-07-11 are examples, not a guaranteed shelf.
  • A $10k sticker is not out-the-door (Utah doc fees, tax, title still apply).[4]
  • Published APRs by score are population / rate-sheet figures, not a hard quote for your file. Down payment rarely changes the published APR sheet—it mainly changes principal (and sometimes approval odds / LTV overlays that are not public).[3]
  • High-mileage budget lots (Vectra’s typical odometer band is very high) trade price for remaining mechanical life—always budget a pre-purchase inspection.[1]
  • MPG figures are EPA combined estimates; real Utah canyon / winter / I-15 commuting will differ.[5]

7. How to Read the Hypotheticals

  • CU vs dealer gap widens as scores fall. At 750 / 60 mo / 0 down, CU interest ~$915 vs dealer ~$2,117. At 600, CU ~$2,238 vs dealer ~$4,569—dealer financing can cost more than two extra years of gas on a Prius.[2][3]
  • 10% down (~$1,000 cash) cuts monthly payment ~10% and interest ~10% in these fixed-APR scenarios (rows 1→2, 15→16). It also leaves a cash buffer for first repairs—critical on high-mile $10k cars.
  • Longer terms shrink the payment and grow the interest. Score-600 dealer 48 mo (~$283/mo, $3.6k interest) vs 72 mo (~$217/mo, $5.6k interest): you “save” ~$66/month and pay ~$2,000 more overall.
  • West Auto’s published down-payment floor ($0 good credit / ~$500 bad credit) aligns with getting into rows like #13–16 without a full 10%—but putting 10% still wins on total cost.[8]

9. Conclusion

In the ~$10k band near West Jordan / West Valley, Vectra Auto Sales currently offers the widest lot selection (average sticker ~$8.2k), with West Auto a strong alternative when older stock is on the lot and KSL the sticker-price floor. For cars that sip gas at that budget, prioritize a healthy Prius, then Civic / Corolla, then efficient small cars like Versa / Fiesta / Elantra—and treat high-mile SUVs as gas-budget traps.

Financing a $10,000 car at Utah First–like CU rates can keep total interest near $800–$1,400 for scores 650–750 on a 60-month loan (less with 10% down). The same principal through typical Utah dealer APRs at 600–650 can push interest toward $3,500–$5,600 depending on term. Get the CU quote first; use the lot’s lenders only if they beat it.
