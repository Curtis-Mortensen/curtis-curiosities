3. Is the Vectra Edge (~$7k / ~137k) a good deal?

For a mid-2010s (or earlier) Edge with roughly 130k–150k miles, national dealer “fair purchase” guides cluster near the high $6k to low $8k range. Kelley Blue Book’s national fair purchase for a 2013 Edge SE sits around $7,125–$7,430 (condition/mileage adjust from there).[1] CarGurus’ market value for 2013 Edges is in a similar ballpark (~$7k).[4]

Sticker vs guides: ~$7,000 at ~137k miles is in the right zip code for an older high-mile Edge. It is a reasonable ask if the car is clean-title, drives well, and passes inspection — not an obvious “must buy before someone else does” underpricing.

Against local clean-title peers (see table below), Vectra’s ask is often $1k–$5k cheaper than newer/lower-mile Edges at West Auto, Alpine, or Drive In Motors — which is exactly how budget lots price: you pay less cash and accept more age/miles/uncertainty.

Deal ≠ safe buy. A fair sticker on an AWD Edge with no PTU fluid history can still become a $2k–$4k repair. Treat the price as “OK if PPI is green,” not as proof the car is healthy.[5]

6. Mazda CX-50 reality check

The CX-50 launched for the 2023 model year. It has not depreciated into the Vectra / $7–10k indie-lot band.

  • CarGurus near Salt Lake City: ~118 used CX-50s, average about $30,618, range roughly $19,989–$41,894.[2]
  • Tim Dahle Mazda Murray stocks many used CX-50s; recent Select / Premium examples cluster mid-$20ks to low-$30ks (e.g. 2024 Select around mid-$20ks).[22]
  • None of Vectra, West Auto’s budget shelf, Immaculate’s under-$15k lane, or Image Auto’s rebuilt bargains are realistic places to expect a CX-50 near $7k.
If the goal is “CX-50 feel” on a first-time ~$7–10k budget, the practical substitutes are older compact crossovers (CR-V / RAV4 / Forester / Escape / Outback) from the companion $10k report — not a CX-50.

7. Edge buy risks — what to check before writing a check

Edges (especially AWD, ~2007–2016 and into mid-2010s) have a well-known weak point: the Power Transfer Unit (PTU) that feeds the rear axle. It sits near hot exhaust parts, holds little fluid, and was often treated as “lifetime fill.” Cooked fluid → whine / leak / failure; replacement commonly lands in the low-to-mid thousands.[5][23]

  1. Confirm drivetrain: FWD vs AWD. FWD skips the PTU risk entirely.
  2. Smell and listen: burnt / propane-like odor under the car after a highway run; whine or growl on low-speed turns.
  3. Look for leaks / staining around the PTU / right-side axle seal area.
  4. Ask for PTU & transmission fluid service records — assume neglected if none exist past ~100k.
  5. Pay for a PPI at an independent shop (not the selling lot). Budget that as part of the “deal.”
  6. Title / Carfax: clean vs rebuilt changes both value and insurance/finance options.

9. Conclusion

Ford Edge: The Vectra ~$7k / ~137k unit looks fairly priced against guides and against South Salt Lake peers at ~$8.6k–$9k. West Auto’s Edges ($12k / $19k) are nicer cars, not the same shopping trip. Treat it as a conditional yes: fair sticker → buy only if PPI (especially PTU on AWD) is clean.

Subaru Outback: Not really at West Auto / Prestman / Immaculate in the $7k band. Real alternatives are older high-mile Outbacks at smaller indie lots (~$5k–$8.5k / 145k–180k). Those can be great Utah cars — or expensive CVT/cooling projects.

Mazda CX-50: Skip for this budget. Valley used averages ~$30k; even “cheap” examples are usually still ~$20k+.
