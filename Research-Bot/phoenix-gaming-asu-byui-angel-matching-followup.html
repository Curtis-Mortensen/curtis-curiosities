1. Aims and Updated Profile

The parent report assumed a generic Phoenix tech startup. This follow-up updates the profile with three new facts and re-ranks opportunities:

  • Cofounder affiliation: Arizona State University (ASU)
  • Founder affiliation: BYU-Idaho (BYUI)
  • Sector: entertainment gaming (with an open question about SaaS framing)
  • New ask: angel investor matching, not only grants/competitions
Updated thesis: Entertainment gaming is usually not treated as classic B2B SaaS by Arizona’s SaaS-themed AIC cohorts — unless the product is a subscription platform (tools, live-service game with clear MRR, B2B game-tech). University doors split hard on enrollment: Venture Devils funding requires a current ASU/Maricopa student lead; alumni use Edson community programs + InvestU for investor matching. BYUI’s strongest cash comps are student-era; as alumni, the BYUI/BYU/Mountain West value is mostly network → Kickstart-class Mountain West capital and alumni angels. For angels in Phoenix, the practical matching stack is Canyon Angels + Desert Angels + Arizona Technology Investors / PHX Angels + Venture Madness + Venture Café, greased by Arizona’s Angel Investment Tax Credit if you certify as a Qualified Small Business.123

2. Precursor and Subset Questions

Is the ASU cofounder still enrolled?

Venture Devils (and its Demo Day grants) require at least one current ASU or Maricopa CC student as lead founder. Alumni are explicitly routed to other Edson programs as of August 2024.1

Game product vs. game platform?

A shippable entertainment title ≠ SaaS. A live-service game with subscriptions, a B2B game-ops tool, or a creator platform with recurring fees can be framed as software/SaaS for AIC and PHX FWD.4

Angel “matching” means what?

Three different things get called matching: (1) pitch-to-angel-group application funnels, (2) warm intros via university/alumni/LGBTQ networks, (3) state tax-credit programs that make angels more willing to write checks. This report covers all three.3

BYUI student vs. alumni?

Vision-to-Venture and Idaho Entrepreneur Challenge pathways are student-forward. Alumni leverage Entrepreneurship Society contacts, IBC culture, and Utah/Idaho/Mountain West investor gravity more than campus prize money.56

4. Does Entertainment Gaming Count as SaaS?

Short answer: Sometimes — but do not assume Fall-2025-style AIC SaaS rules cover a traditional entertainment game. Frame carefully.

Business shapeLikely AIC / PHX FWD framingNotes
One-time premium game (Steam/console) Weak as “SaaS” Still tech/software for broader AIC cycles historically; Fall 2025 SaaS+MRR cohort would be a stretch unless you invent recurring revenue.4
Live-service / battle pass / subscription game with measurable MRR Possible SaaS-like If ≥~$5K MRR and founder-led sales/GTM story, you can argue recurring software revenue. Confirm each cohort’s written rules.4
B2B game tooling, backend, analytics, UGC platform sold on subscriptions Strong SaaS Closest to what PHX FWD and SaaS-themed AIC want.10
Edu / serious games with institutional licenses Often SaaS/edtech InvestU portfolio already includes learning games (e.g., immersionEd).2
Content studio / work-for-hire Poor venture/SaaS fit Better for grants, publisher deals, or services revenue — not angel SaaS theses.

Practical advice: For IdeaFunding’s Tech track, Venture Madness, and non-SaaS AIC cycles, lead with “entertainment software / interactive entertainment tech.” For SaaS-themed AIC or PHX FWD, lead with recurring revenue mechanics (subscriptions, seats, live-service ARPU) — or wait until those exist.

5. ASU Cofounder Pathways

1. Venture Devils (Demo Day / Pitch Playoffs)

ASU · Fit: HIGH if currently enrolled / LOW if alumni-only

ASU’s flagship student venture program via Edson E+I. Mentorship, workshops, and competition for seed grants commonly cited in the $1K–$50K range; institute materials also reference $400K–$450K+ in annual startup funding across Demo Day tracks. Demo Days run roughly December and April (Fall 2026 Demo Day listed for Nov 14, 2026).11112

Hard gate: At least one lead founding team member must be a current ASU or Maricopa Community College student in a degree-seeking program taking ≥1 credit course (undergrad, grad, postdoc, or part-time/online OK). As of August 2024, alumni/community founders are directed away from Venture Devils to other Edson programs.1

Action: If the ASU cofounder can enroll in even one credit, this becomes one of the highest-ROI local grant competitions available. If not, skip to community programs below.

2. Edson Community Entrepreneurship / Pitch In / Prototyping Venture Boost

ASU · Fit: HIGH for alumni

Pitch In is an open community pitch/feedback event (historically 2nd & 4th Wednesdays). Prototyping Venture Boost and Chandler Endeavor / community incubator partnerships help alumni and non-student founders without Venture Devils eligibility. Email entrepreneurship@asu.edu for routing.1314

3. InvestU (ASU-affiliated investor matching)

ASU Angel/investor · Fit: HIGH for ASU-affiliated raise

InvestU connects accredited investors to ASU-affiliated startups. Public stats: 31 portfolio companies, >$6.5M direct investment, >$200M follow-on, 480+ jobs. Portfolio already includes a learning-games company (immersionEd) plus software/SaaS names — useful social proof that interactive media is not alien to this network.2

This is one of your best “angel matching” levers tied to the ASU cofounder. Treat it as a raise-path, not a grant.

4. New Venture Challenge / W. P. Carey accelerator paths

ASU · Fit: MEDIUM if grad-student path available

Course + competition for ASU graduate students and co-founders advancing a venture; W. P. Carey also describes accelerator support for ASU-affiliated founders preparing larger pre-seed/seed raises.15

5. Skysong Innovations Startup Challenge

6. ACA Venture Start @ ASU 850PBC (shared with parent report)

8.1 Structured angel groups (apply-to-pitch)

Canyon Angels (Phoenix / GCU)

Angel · Fit: HIGH first local application

Student-powered diligence + accredited member pitches. Public claims: 40+ angels per event, 70+ companies funded, $7.5M+ committed since founding. Apply via Dealum at canyonangels.org/pitch. They look for high-growth, product-market-fit companies aiming for >5x in 3–7 years.2122

Desert Angels

Angel · Fit: HIGH

Long-running AZ angel network (since ~2000). Founders apply separately from investor membership. Invests in preferred equity and convertible notes — not SAFEs. Review meetings monthly+. Strong process brand; gaming must still clear tech/scale diligence.23

Arizona Technology Investors (ATI)

Angel · Fit: MEDIUM–HIGH

Positions as Arizona’s large tech-focused angel org; sectors include software/SaaS, consumer tech with growth potential. Screening → pitch → diligence → decision process.24

PHX Angels (+ PHX FWD adjacency)

Angel · Fit: MEDIUM (stronger if software/MRR story)

Software-startup-forward angel community partnered with PHX FWD. More natural if you can tell a recurring-revenue software story; less natural for pure content entertainment without metrics.25

8.3 Arizona Angel Investment Tax Credit (makes angels say yes easier)

This is not a grant to you — it is a matching incentive for your investors. ACA can authorize up to $2.5M in tax credits per fiscal year (extended through the decade under current framing). Businesses certify as a Qualified Small Business; investors claim credits after investing.3

Basic QSB pattern (verify current ACA guidelines): Arizona entity (not sole prop), AZ ops, ≥2 principal non-administrative AZ-resident FTEs, assets under ~$10M (excl. IP/qualified investments), not in prohibited activities. Getting certified before or during a raise is a concrete “matching” advantage in angel conversations.3

10. Conclusion

With ASU + BYUI + entertainment gaming in the picture, the strategy shifts from “find LGBTQ grants” to a three-lane race:

  1. University lane: Venture Devils only if enrolled; otherwise Edson community + InvestU matching.
  2. Gaming lane: Epic (engine-gated), LGBTQ games mentorship (GLAAD QED), genre grants (Draknek when open), IdeaFunding for Arizona stage time.
  3. Angel lane: Canyon + Desert + ATI/PHX Angels applications, tax-credit certification, Venture Café / Venture Madness rooms, Gaingels + Mountain West corridor for identity and alumni gravity.

Entertainment gaming is venture-fundable, but angels will underwrite business model and traction more than “we’re making a cool game.” Your prior shipped product is the wedge — lead with that.

Further research worth doing next: (a) exact engine/genre and monetization; (b) ASU enrollment confirmation; (c) whether either founder wants a publisher path vs. venture path; (d) interviews with 2–3 Arizona gaming studios on which angel groups actually wrote entertainment checks; (e) City of Phoenix entertainment/future-tech office intro path.
